# Sample Problem 23.07: Electric field near two parallel nonconducting sheets with charge

## Problem
Figure 23-19a shows portions of two large, parallel, nonconducting sheets, each with a fixed uniform charge on one side. The magnitudes of the surface charge densities are \(\sigma^{(+)} = 6.8\ \text{mC/m}^2\) for the positively charged sheet and \(\sigma^{(-)} = 4.3\ \text{mC/m}^2\) for the negatively charged sheet.

Find the electric field:
(a) to the left of the sheets,
(b) between the sheets, and
(c) to the right of the sheets.

## Key ideas
With the charges fixed in place (they are on nonconductors), we can find the electric field of the sheets in Fig. 23-19a by:
1. finding the field of each sheet as if that sheet were isolated, and
2. algebraically adding the fields of the isolated sheets via the superposition principle.

We can add the fields algebraically because they are parallel to each other.

## Solution
At any point, the electric field due to the positive sheet is directed away from the sheet and, from Eq. 23-13, has the magnitude

\[
E^{(+)}=\frac{\sigma^{(+)}}{2\varepsilon_0}.
\]

Similarly, at any point, the electric field due to the negative sheet is directed toward that sheet and has the magnitude

\[
E^{(-)}=\frac{\sigma^{(-)}}{2\varepsilon_0}.
\]

Calculations:

\[
E^{(+)}=\frac{6.8\times 10^{-6}\ \text{C/m}^2}{2(8.85\times 10^{-12}\ \text{C}^2/\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)}
=3.84\times 10^5\ \text{N/C}.
\]

\[
E^{(-)}=\frac{4.3\times 10^{-6}\ \text{C/m}^2}{2(8.85\times 10^{-12}\ \text{C}^2/\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)}
=2.43\times 10^5\ \text{N/C}.
\]

Figure 23-19b shows the fields set up by the sheets to the left of the sheets (L), between them (B), and to their right (R).

The resultant fields in these three regions follow from the superposition principle.

To the left, the field magnitude is

\[
E_L = E^{(+)} - E^{(-)}.
\]

\[
E_L = 3.84\times 10^5\ \text{N/C} - 2.43\times 10^5\ \text{N/C}
=1.4\times 10^5\ \text{N/C}.
\]

Because \(E^{(+)}\) is larger than \(E^{(-)}\), the net electric field in this region is directed to the left, as Fig. 23-19c shows.

Between the sheets, the two fields add and we have

\[
E_B = E^{(+)} + E^{(-)}.
\]

\[
E_B = 3.84\times 10^5\ \text{N/C} + 2.43\times 10^5\ \text{N/C}
=6.3\times 10^5\ \text{N/C}.
\]

The electric field is directed to the right.

To the right of the sheets, the net field has the same magnitude as in the left region but is directed to the right, as Fig. 23-19c shows.

## Answer
(a) To the left of the sheets:
\[
E_L = 1.4\times 10^5\ \text{N/C},
\]
directed to the left.

(b) Between the sheets:
\[
E_B = 6.3\times 10^5\ \text{N/C},
\]
directed to the right.

(c) To the right of the sheets:
\[
E_R = 1.4\times 10^5\ \text{N/C},
\]
directed to the right.

## Key concepts used
- Electric field of an infinite nonconducting sheet:
  \[
  E=\frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_0}
  \]
- Direction of field from a positively charged sheet: away from the sheet
- Direction of field from a negatively charged sheet: toward the sheet
- Superposition principle for vector addition of electric fields
