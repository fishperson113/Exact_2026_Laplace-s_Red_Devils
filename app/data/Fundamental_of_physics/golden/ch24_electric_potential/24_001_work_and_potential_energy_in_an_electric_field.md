# Sample Problem 24.01: Work and potential energy in an electric field

## Problem
Electrons are continually being knocked out of air molecules in the atmosphere by cosmic-ray particles coming in from space. Once released, each electron experiences an electric force due to the electric field that is produced in the atmosphere by charged particles already on Earth. Near Earth’s surface the electric field has the magnitude \(E = 150\ \text{N/C}\) and is directed downward. What is the change \(\Delta U\) in the electric potential energy of a released electron when the electric force causes it to move vertically upward through a distance \(d = 520\ \text{m}\) (Fig. 24-3)? Through what potential change does the electron move?

## Key ideas
1. The change \(\Delta U\) in the electric potential energy of the electron is related to the work \(W\) done on the electron by the electric field. Equation 24-5 \((W = -\Delta U)\) gives the relation.
2. The work done by a constant force on a particle undergoing a displacement is
   \[
   W = \vec F \cdot \vec d.
   \]
3. The electric force and the electric field are related by
   \[
   \vec F = q \vec E,
   \]
   where here \(q\) is the charge of an electron \(( -1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C})\).

## Solution
Substituting the force equation into the work equation and taking the dot product yield
\[
W = q \vec E \cdot \vec d = qEd\cos\phi,
\]
where \(\phi\) is the angle between \(\vec E\) and \(\vec d\).

Here the electric field is downward and the electron moves upward, so \(\phi = 180^\circ\). Thus
\[
W = qEd\cos 180^\circ = -qEd.
\]

Because \(q = -e = -1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}\),
\[
W = -(-e)Ed = eEd.
\]

Now compute:
\[
W = (1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C})(150\ \text{N/C})(520\ \text{m})
\]
\[
W = 1.25 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{J}.
\]

Since \(W = -\Delta U\),
\[
\Delta U = -W = -1.25 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{J}.
\]

The potential change is related by
\[
\Delta U = q\Delta V,
\]
so
\[
\Delta V = \frac{\Delta U}{q}
= \frac{-1.25 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{J}}{-1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}}
= 7.8 \times 10^{4}\ \text{V}.
\]

## Answer
\[
\Delta U = -1.25 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{J}
\]
and the electron moves through a potential increase of
\[
\Delta V = +7.8 \times 10^{4}\ \text{V}.
\]

## Key concepts used
- Work done by a constant force: \(W = \vec F \cdot \vec d\)
- Electric force in a uniform field: \(\vec F = q\vec E\)
- Relationship between work and potential energy: \(W = -\Delta U\)
- Relationship between potential energy change and potential difference: \(\Delta U = q\Delta V\)
