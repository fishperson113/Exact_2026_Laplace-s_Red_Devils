# Sample Problem 25.01: Charging the plates in a parallel-plate capacitor

## Problem
In Fig. 25-7a, switch S is closed to connect the uncharged capacitor of capacitance $C = 0.25\ \text{mF} $ to the battery of potential difference $V = 12\ \text{V} $. The lower capacitor plate has thickness $L = 0.50\ \text{cm} $ and face area $A = 2.0 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2 $, and it consists of copper, in which the density of conduction electrons is $n = 8.49 \times 10^{28}\ \text{electrons/m}^3 $. From what depth $d $ within the plate (Fig. 25-7b) must electrons move to the plate face as the capacitor becomes charged?

## Key ideas
The charge collected on the plate is related to the capacitance and the potential difference across the capacitor by Eq. 25-1:
$$ 
q = CV.
 $$

## Solution
Because the lower plate is connected to the negative terminal of the battery, conduction electrons move up to the face of the plate.

From Eq. 25-1, the total charge that moves is
$$ 
q = CV = (0.25 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{F})(12\ \text{V}) = 3.0 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{C}.
 $$

That charge corresponds to a number of electrons
$$ 
N = \frac{q}{e}
= \frac{3.0 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{C}}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C/electron}}
\approx 1.9 \times 10^{13}\ \text{electrons}.
 $$

Those electrons come from a slab of the plate of volume
$$ 
Ad,
 $$
and the number of conduction electrons in that volume is
$$ 
N = nAd.
 $$

Thus,
$$ 
d = \frac{N}{nA}
= \frac{1.9 \times 10^{13}}{(8.49 \times 10^{28})(2.0 \times 10^{-4})}
\approx 1.1 \times 10^{-12}\ \text{m}.
 $$

## Answer
$$ 
d \approx 1.1 \times 10^{-12}\ \text{m}.
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Capacitance relation $q = CV $
- Charge is carried by conduction electrons
- Electron number from $N = q/e $
- Volume relation $N = nAd $
