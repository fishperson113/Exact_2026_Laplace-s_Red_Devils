# Sample Problem 25.05: Work and energy when a dielectric is inserted into a capacitor

## Problem
A parallel-plate capacitor whose capacitance \(C\) is \(13.5\ \text{pF}\) is charged by a battery to a potential difference \(V = 12.5\ \text{V}\) between its plates. The charging battery is now disconnected, and a porcelain slab \((k = 6.50)\) is slipped between the plates.

(a) What is the potential energy of the capacitor before the slab is inserted?

(b) What is the potential energy of the capacitor-slab device after the slab is inserted?

## Key ideas
We can relate the potential energy \(U_i\) of the capacitor to the capacitance \(C\) and either the potential \(V\) (with Eq. 25-22) or the charge \(q\) (with Eq. 25-21):

\[
U_i = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{q^2}{2C}.
\]

## Solution
### (a)
Because we are given the initial potential \(V = 12.5\ \text{V}\), we use Eq. 25-22 to find the initial stored energy:

\[
U_i = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
\]

\[
U_i = \frac{1}{2}(13.5 \times 10^{-12}\ \text{F})(12.5\ \text{V})^2
\]

\[
U_i = 1.055 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{J}
\]

### (b)
After the battery is disconnected, the charge \(q\) on the capacitor remains fixed. Inserting the dielectric increases the capacitance to

\[
C' = kC.
\]

Thus the new potential energy is

\[
U = \frac{q^2}{2C'} = \frac{q^2}{2kC}.
\]

Since initially

\[
U_i = \frac{q^2}{2C},
\]

the final energy is reduced by a factor of \(k\):

\[
U = \frac{U_i}{k}.
\]

So,

\[
U = \frac{1.055 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{J}}{6.50}
\approx 1.62 \times 10^{-10}\ \text{J}.
\]

## Answer
(a) \(\boxed{U_i = 1.055 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{J} \approx 1055\ \text{pJ}}\)

(b) \(\boxed{U = 1.62 \times 10^{-10}\ \text{J}}\)

## Key concepts used
- Capacitor energy: \(\displaystyle U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{q^2}{2C}\)
- With the battery disconnected, the charge \(q\) remains constant.
- Inserting a dielectric changes the capacitance to \(\displaystyle C' = kC\).
- Therefore the stored energy decreases by a factor of \(k\).
