# Sample Problem 28.01: Magnetic force on a moving charged particle

## Problem
A uniform magnetic field, with magnitude 1.2 mT, is directed vertically upward throughout the volume of a laboratory chamber. A proton with kinetic energy 5.3 MeV enters the chamber, moving horizontally from south to north. What magnetic deflecting force acts on the proton as it enters the chamber? The proton mass is \(1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg}\). Neglect Earth’s magnetic field.

## Key ideas
Because the proton is charged and moving through a magnetic field, a magnetic force can act on it. Because the initial direction of the proton’s velocity is not along a magnetic field line, the magnetic force is not simply zero.

Magnitude: To find the magnitude of the magnetic force, use
\[
F_B = |q|vB\sin\phi
\]
provided first the proton’s speed \(v\) is found.

The speed can be found from the kinetic energy:
\[
K=\frac12 mv^2
\]
Solving for \(v\),
\[
v=\sqrt{\frac{2K}{m}}
\]

## Solution
For the proton, the angle between the velocity and the magnetic field is \(90^\circ\), so \(\sin\phi=\sin 90^\circ=1\).

First find the speed from the kinetic energy:
\[
v=\sqrt{\frac{2K}{m}}
\]
with
\[
K=5.3\,\text{MeV}=(5.3)(1.60\times10^{-13})\,\text{J}
\]
Thus,
\[
v=\sqrt{\frac{(2)(5.3\,\text{MeV})(1.60\times10^{-13}\,\text{J/MeV})}{1.67\times10^{-27}\,\text{kg}}}
\]
\[
v\approx 3.2\times10^7\,\text{m/s}
\]

Now use Eq. 28-3:
\[
F_B=|q|vB\sin\phi
\]
\[
F_B=(1.60\times10^{-19}\,\text{C})(3.2\times10^7\,\text{m/s})(1.2\times10^{-3}\,\text{T})(\sin90^\circ)
\]
\[
F_B\approx 6.1\times10^{-15}\,\text{N}
\]

Direction: For a positively charged proton, with velocity from south to north and magnetic field vertically upward, the right-hand rule gives a force toward the east.

This may seem like a small force, but it acts on a particle of small mass, producing a large acceleration.

## Answer
\[
F_B = 6.1\times10^{-15}\,\text{N}
\]
directed toward the east.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a moving charge: \(F_B=|q|vB\sin\phi\)
- Kinetic energy relation: \(K=\frac12 mv^2\)
- Speed from kinetic energy: \(v=\sqrt{2K/m}\)
- Right-hand rule for the direction of magnetic force on a positive charge
