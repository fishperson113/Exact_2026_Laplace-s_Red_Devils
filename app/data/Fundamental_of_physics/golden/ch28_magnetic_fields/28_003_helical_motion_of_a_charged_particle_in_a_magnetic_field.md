# Sample Problem 28.03: Helical motion of a charged particle in a magnetic field

## Problem
An electron with a kinetic energy of 22.5 eV moves into a region of uniform magnetic field of magnitude \(4.55 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{T}\). The angle between the directions of \(\mathbf{B}\) and the electron’s velocity \(\mathbf{v}\) is \(65.5^\circ\). What is the pitch of the helical path taken by the electron?

## Key ideas
1. The pitch \(p\) is the distance the electron travels parallel to the magnetic field during one period \(T\) of circulation.
2. The period \(T\) is given by Eq. 28-17 for any nonzero angle between \(\mathbf{B}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\).

## Solution
If the velocity of a charged particle has a component parallel to the (uniform) magnetic field, the particle will move in a helical path about the direction of the field vector.

The velocity vector \(\mathbf{v}\) is resolved into two components, one parallel to \(\mathbf{B}\) and one perpendicular to it:
\[
v_\parallel = v \cos \phi, \qquad v_\perp = v \sin \phi.
\]

The parallel component determines the pitch \(p\) of the helix—that is, the distance between adjacent turns. The perpendicular component determines the radius of the helix and is the quantity to be substituted for \(v\) in Eq. 28-16.

Since the pitch is the distance traveled parallel to the field in one period,
\[
p = v_\parallel T.
\]

Using the speed from the kinetic energy,
\[
\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = K,
\]
with \(K = 22.5\ \text{eV}\), we obtain \(v\), and then use the period for circular motion in a uniform magnetic field,
\[
T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB}.
\]

Thus,
\[
p = v \cos\phi \cdot \frac{2\pi m}{qB}.
\]

Substituting the given values for an electron gives the pitch.

## Answer
\[
p = 1.6254\ \text{m}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Helical motion of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field
- Velocity decomposition into components parallel and perpendicular to \(\mathbf{B}\)
- Pitch of a helix: distance advanced along the field in one cyclotron period
- Cyclotron period in a magnetic field
