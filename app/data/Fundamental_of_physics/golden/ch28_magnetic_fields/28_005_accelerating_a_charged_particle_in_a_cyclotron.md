# Sample Problem 28.05: Accelerating a charged particle in a cyclotron

## Problem
Suppose a cyclotron is operated at an oscillator frequency of 12 MHz and has a dee radius \(R = 53\ \text{cm}\).

(a) What is the magnitude of the magnetic field needed for deuterons to be accelerated in the cyclotron? The deuteron mass is \(m = 3.34 \times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}\) (twice the proton mass).

(b) What is the resulting kinetic energy of the deuterons?

## Key ideas
KEY IDEA

For a given oscillator frequency \(f_{\text{osc}}\), the magnetic field magnitude \(B\) required to accelerate any particle in a cyclotron depends on the ratio \(m/q\) of mass to charge for the particle, according to Eq. 28-24.

## Solution
Calculation: For deuterons and the oscillator frequency \(f_{\text{osc}} = 12\ \text{MHz}\), we find

\[
B = \frac{2\pi m f_{\text{osc}}}{q}
\]

\[
B = \frac{(2\pi)(3.34 \times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg})(12 \times 10^{6}\ \text{s}^{-1})}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}}
\]

\[
B = 1.57\ \text{T} \approx 1.6\ \text{T}
\]

Note that, to accelerate protons, \(B\) would have to be reduced by a factor of 2, provided the oscillator frequency remained fixed at 12 MHz.

## Answer
(a) \(B = 1.57\ \text{T} \approx 1.6\ \text{T}\)

(b) The resulting kinetic energy of the deuterons is not given in the provided source excerpt.

## Key concepts used
- Cyclotron oscillator frequency
- Magnetic field requirement in a cyclotron
- Mass-to-charge ratio \(m/q\)
- Deuteron charge \(q = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}\)
