# Sample Problem 31.03: Purely resistive load: potential difference and current

## Problem
In Fig. 31-8, resistance \(R\) is \(200\ \Omega\) and the sinusoidal alternating emf device operates at amplitude \(\varepsilon_m = 36.0\ \text{V}\) and frequency \(f_d = 60.0\ \text{Hz}\).

(a) What is the potential difference \(v_R(t)\) across the resistance as a function of time \(t\), and what is the amplitude \(V_R\) of \(v_R(t)\)?

## Key ideas
In a circuit with a purely resistive load, the potential difference \(v_R(t)\) across the resistance is always equal to the potential difference \(\varepsilon(t)\) across the emf device.

## Solution
For our situation,
\[
v_R(t) = \varepsilon(t), \qquad V_R = \varepsilon_m.
\]

Since \(\varepsilon_m\) is given, we can write
\[
V_R = \varepsilon_m = 36.0\ \text{V}.
\]

To find \(v_R(t)\), we use Eq. 31-28 to write
\[
v_R(t) = \varepsilon(t) = \varepsilon_m \sin \omega_d t
\]
and then substitute \(\varepsilon_m = 36.0\ \text{V}\) and
\[
\omega_d = 2\pi f_d = 2\pi(60\ \text{Hz}) = 120\pi
\]
to obtain
\[
v_R(t) = (36.0\ \text{V})\sin(120\pi t).
\]

## Answer
\[
V_R = 36.0\ \text{V}
\]
\[
v_R(t) = (36.0\ \text{V})\sin(120\pi t)
\]

## Key concepts used
- Purely resistive load
- Alternating emf and sinusoidal time dependence
- Potential difference across a resistor equals the source potential difference
