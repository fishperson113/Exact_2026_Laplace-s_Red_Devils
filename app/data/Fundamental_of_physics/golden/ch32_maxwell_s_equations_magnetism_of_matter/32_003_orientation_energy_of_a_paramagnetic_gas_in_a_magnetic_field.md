# Sample Problem 32.03: Orientation energy of a paramagnetic gas in a magnetic field

## Problem
A paramagnetic gas at room temperature $T = 300\ \text{K} $ is placed in an external uniform magnetic field of magnitude $B = 1.5\ \text{T} $. The atoms of the gas have magnetic dipole moment $m = 1.0\,\mu_B $. Calculate the mean translational kinetic energy $K $ of an atom of the gas and the energy difference $\Delta U_B $ between parallel alignment and antiparallel alignment of the atom’s magnetic dipole moment with the external field.

## Key ideas
1. The mean translational kinetic energy $K $ of an atom in a gas depends on the temperature of the gas.
2. The energy $U_B $ of a magnetic dipole in an external magnetic field depends on the angle between the directions of $\vec m $ and $\vec B $.

## Solution
From Eq. 19-24, we have
$$ 
K = \frac{3}{2}kT
 $$
$$ 
K = \frac{3}{2}(1.38 \times 10^{-23}\ \text{J/K})(300\ \text{K})
 $$
$$ 
K \approx 6.2 \times 10^{-21}\ \text{J} \approx 0.039\ \text{eV}.
 $$

For the dipole energy in a magnetic field, use
$$ 
U_B = -\vec m \cdot \vec B = -mB\cos\theta.
 $$
For parallel alignment, $\theta = 0^\circ $, so
$$ 
U_{\parallel} = -mB.
 $$
For antiparallel alignment, $\theta = 180^\circ $, so
$$ 
U_{\text{anti}} = +mB.
 $$
Thus the energy difference between antiparallel and parallel alignment is
$$ 
\Delta U_B = U_{\text{anti}} - U_{\parallel} = 2mB.
 $$
With $m = 1.0\,\mu_B = 9.27\times 10^{-24}\ \text{J/T} $ and $B = 1.5\ \text{T} $,
$$ 
\Delta U_B = 2(9.27\times 10^{-24}\ \text{J/T})(1.5\ \text{T})
 $$
$$ 
\Delta U_B \approx 2.8 \times 10^{-23}\ \text{J} \approx 1.8\times 10^{-4}\ \text{eV}.
 $$

## Answer
$$ 
K \approx 6.2\times 10^{-21}\ \text{J} \approx 0.039\ \text{eV}
 $$
$$ 
\Delta U_B \approx 2.8\times 10^{-23}\ \text{J} \approx 1.8\times 10^{-4}\ \text{eV}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Mean translational kinetic energy of an ideal gas atom: $K = \frac{3}{2}kT $
- Magnetic dipole potential energy: $U_B = -\vec m \cdot \vec B = -mB\cos\theta $
- Energy difference between antiparallel and parallel orientations: $\Delta U_B = 2mB $
