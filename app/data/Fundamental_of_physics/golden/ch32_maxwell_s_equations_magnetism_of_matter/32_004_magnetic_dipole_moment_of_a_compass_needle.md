# Sample Problem 32.04: Magnetic dipole moment of a compass needle

## Problem
A compass needle made of pure iron (density 7900 kg/m³) has a length $L $ of 3.0 cm, a width of 1.0 mm, and a thickness of 0.50 mm. The magnitude of the magnetic dipole moment of an iron atom is $m_{\mathrm{Fe}} = 2.1 \times 10^{-23}\ \mathrm{J/T} $. If the magnetization of the needle is equivalent to the alignment of 10% of the atoms in the needle, what is the magnitude of the needle’s magnetic dipole moment?

## Key ideas
1. Alignment of all $N $ atoms in the needle would give a magnitude of $N m_{\mathrm{Fe}} $ for the needle’s magnetic dipole moment. However, the needle has only 10% alignment, so
   $$ 
   m = 0.10\,N m_{\mathrm{Fe}}.
    $$
2. We can find the number of atoms $N $ in the needle from the needle’s mass:
   $$ 
   N = \frac{\text{needle’s mass}}{\text{iron’s atomic mass}}.
    $$
   Iron’s atomic mass is not listed in Appendix F, but its molar mass $M $ is. Thus,
   $$ 
   \text{iron’s atomic mass} = \frac{\text{iron’s molar mass } M}{\text{Avogadro’s number } N_A}.
    $$

## Solution
Needle’s mass:
$$ 
m = (\text{needle’s density})(\text{needle’s volume})
 $$
The volume of the needle is
$$ 
V = (3.0\ \text{cm})(1.0\ \text{mm})(0.50\ \text{mm})
= (3.0\times10^{-2}\ \text{m})(1.0\times10^{-3}\ \text{m})(0.50\times10^{-3}\ \text{m})
= 1.5\times10^{-8}\ \text{m}^3.
 $$
Thus,
$$ 
m = (7900\ \text{kg/m}^3)(1.5\times10^{-8}\ \text{m}^3)
= 1.185\times10^{-4}\ \text{kg}.
 $$

Finding $N $: Using $M = 55.847\ \text{g/mol} = 0.055847\ \text{kg/mol} $ and $N_A = 6.02\times10^{23} $,
$$ 
N = (1.185\times10^{-4}\ \text{kg})\frac{6.02\times10^{23}}{0.055847\ \text{kg/mol}}
= 1.2774\times10^{21}.
 $$

Finding $m $: Substituting into Eq. 32-42,
$$ 
m = (0.10)(1.2774\times10^{21})(2.1\times10^{-23}\ \text{J/T})
= 2.682\times10^{-3}\ \text{J/T}.
 $$

## Answer
$$ 
\boxed{m \approx 2.7\times10^{-3}\ \mathrm{J/T}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic dipole moment of aligned atomic dipoles
- Mass from density and volume
- Number of atoms from mass, molar mass, and Avogadro’s number
