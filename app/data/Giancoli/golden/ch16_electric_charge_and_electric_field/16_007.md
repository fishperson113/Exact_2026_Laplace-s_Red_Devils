# Problem 16.7

## Question
(II) Two small charged spheres are 6.52 cm apart. They are moved, and the force each exerts on the other is found to have tripled. How far apart are they now?

## Final answer from source
3.76 cm.

## Worked solution
For two charged spheres with the same charges unchanged, Coulomb’s law gives the electric force magnitude as

$$ 
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
 $$

Since the charges do not change, the force is inversely proportional to the square of the separation:

$$ 
F \propto \frac{1}{r^2}
 $$

Let the initial separation be

$$ 
r_1 = 6.52\ \text{cm}
 $$

and the new separation be $r_2 $. The force triples, so

$$ 
F_2 = 3F_1
 $$

Using the inverse-square relationship,

$$ 
\frac{F_2}{F_1} = \left(\frac{r_1}{r_2}\right)^2
 $$

Substitute $F_2/F_1 = 3 $:

$$ 
3 = \left(\frac{6.52}{r_2}\right)^2
 $$

Take the square root of both sides:

$$ 
\sqrt{3} = \frac{6.52}{r_2}
 $$

Solve for $r_2 $:

$$ 
r_2 = \frac{6.52}{\sqrt{3}}\ \text{cm}
 $$

$$ 
r_2 \approx \frac{6.52}{1.732} \approx 3.76\ \text{cm}
 $$

So the spheres are now separated by

$$ 
\boxed{3.76\ \text{cm}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Coulomb’s law: $F = k \dfrac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2} $
- Electric force varies inversely with the square of distance
- If force triples, separation changes by a factor of $1/\sqrt{3} $
