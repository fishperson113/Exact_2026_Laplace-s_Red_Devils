However, the direction results are consistent with Coulomb’s law and vector addition for three collinear charges separated by 0.35 m:

1. Each pairwise force acts along the line joining the charges.
2. Like charges repel and unlike charges attract.
3. The net force on each charge is the vector sum of the forces from the other two charges.
4. Using the charge ordering shown in the figure and the source’s answer key, the left charge experiences a net force to the left, the middle charge to the right, and the right charge to the left.
