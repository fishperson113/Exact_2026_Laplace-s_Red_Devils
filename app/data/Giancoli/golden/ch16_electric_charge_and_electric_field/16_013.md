# Problem 16.13

## Question
(II) A charge Q is transferred from an initially uncharged plastic ball to an identical ball 24 cm away. The force of attrac- tion is then 17 mN. How many electrons were transferred from one ball to the other?

## Final answer from source
electrons.

## Worked solution
Use Coulomb’s law for two equal and opposite charges after transfer.

Let the charge moved be \(Q\). Since one ball starts neutral and the balls are identical, after transferring charge:
- one ball has charge \(+Q\)
- the other has charge \(-Q\)

So the magnitude of the force is

\[
F=\frac{kQ^2}{r^2}
\]

Given:
- \(F=17\,\text{mN}=1.7\times 10^{-2}\,\text{N}\)
- \(r=24\,\text{cm}=0.24\,\text{m}\)
- \(k=8.99\times 10^9\,\text{N·m}^2/\text{C}^2\)

Solve for \(Q\):

\[
Q=\sqrt{\frac{Fr^2}{k}}
\]

\[
Q=\sqrt{\frac{(1.7\times 10^{-2})(0.24)^2}{8.99\times 10^9}}
\]

\[
Q=\sqrt{\frac{(1.7\times 10^{-2})(0.0576)}{8.99\times 10^9}}
\]

\[
Q=\sqrt{1.09\times 10^{-13}}
\approx 3.3\times 10^{-7}\,\text{C}
\]

Now convert charge to number of electrons:

\[
N=\frac{Q}{e}
\]

where \(e=1.60\times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}\). Thus,

\[
N=\frac{3.3\times 10^{-7}}{1.60\times 10^{-19}}
\approx 2.1\times 10^{12}
\]

So the number of electrons transferred is

\[
\boxed{2.1\times 10^{12}\ \text{electrons}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Coulomb’s law: \(F = k\frac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}\)
- Equal and opposite charges on the two balls after transfer
- Converting charge to number of electrons using \(N=Q/e\)
- Unit conversion: mN to N, cm to m
