# Problem 16.19

## Question
(I) Determine the magnitude and direction of the electric force on an electron in a uniform electric field of strength 2460 N/C that points due east.

## Final answer from source
west.

## Worked solution
The electric force on a charge in a uniform electric field is

$$ 
\vec F = q\vec E
 $$

For an electron,

$$ 
q = -e = -1.60\times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}
 $$

and the field magnitude is

$$ 
E = 2460\ \text{N/C}
 $$

### Magnitude
$$ 
F = |q|E = (1.60\times 10^{-19})(2460)
 $$

$$ 
F = 3.94\times 10^{-16}\ \text{N}
 $$

### Direction
Because the electron has negative charge, its force is opposite the direction of the electric field.

- Electric field: due east
- Force on electron: due west

So the force has magnitude $3.94\times 10^{-16}\ \text{N} $ and points west.

## Key concepts used
- Electric force in a uniform field: $ \vec F = q\vec E  $
- Magnitude of force: $ F = |q|E  $
- Negative charges experience force opposite the electric field direction
