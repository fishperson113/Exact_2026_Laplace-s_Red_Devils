# Problem 16.31

## Question
(II) Determine the direction and magnitude of the electric field at the point P in Fig. 16-56. The charges are separated by a distance 2a, and point P is a distance x from the midpoint between the two charges. Express your answer in terms of Q, x, a, and k. (cid:6)Q a (cid:5)Q a P x FIGURE 16-56 Problem 31.

## Final answer from source
to the left.

## Worked solution
Because the figure is not fully reproduced in the prompt, the exact placement/signs of the charges must be inferred from the text and the answer key only. The source answer gives only the direction, so the magnitude cannot be checked against the source with certainty.

Assuming the standard arrangement shown in such problems: two equal and opposite charges, \(+Q\) and \(-Q\), separated by \(2a\), with point \(P\) located on the perpendicular bisector a distance \(x\) from the midpoint.

### 1) Geometry
The distance from each charge to point \(P\) is

\[
r=\sqrt{x^2+a^2}.
\]

### 2) Field from each charge
The magnitude of the electric field from either charge at \(P\) is

\[
E_1=E_2=\frac{kQ}{r^2}=\frac{kQ}{x^2+a^2}.
\]

### 3) Resolve components
By symmetry, the vertical components cancel, and the horizontal components add.

If \(\theta\) is the angle each field makes with the horizontal, then

\[
\cos\theta=\frac{a}{\sqrt{x^2+a^2}}.
\]

So the horizontal component from one charge is

\[
E_{1x}=E_1\cos\theta
=\frac{kQ}{x^2+a^2}\cdot \frac{a}{\sqrt{x^2+a^2}}
=\frac{kQa}{(x^2+a^2)^{3/2}}.
\]

Since the two horizontal components point in the same direction, the total field magnitude is

\[
E = 2\frac{kQa}{(x^2+a^2)^{3/2}}.
\]

### 4) Direction
From the source answer, the net electric field at \(P\) is **to the left**.

So the vector form is

\[
\vec E = -\,2\frac{kQa}{(x^2+a^2)^{3/2}}\,\hat{\mathbf{x}}
\]

if rightward is taken as the positive \(x\)-direction.

## Key concepts used
- Electric field of a point charge: \(E = kQ/r^2\)
- Superposition of electric fields
- Symmetry of vector components
- Resolving vectors into horizontal and vertical components
- Net field direction from component cancellation
