For a uniformly charged sphere, the electric field just outside the surface is

\[
E=\frac{kQ}{r^2}
\]

so the total charge on the sphere is

\[
Q=\frac{Er^2}{k}
\]

Given:
- diameter \(=1.0\ \text{m}\), so \(r=0.50\ \text{m}\)
- \(E=5000\ \text{N/C}\)
- \(k=8.99\times10^9\ \text{N·m}^2/\text{C}^2\)

Thus,

\[
Q=\frac{(5000)(0.50)^2}{8.99\times10^9}
=\frac{1250}{8.99\times10^9}
\approx 1.39\times10^{-7}\ \text{C}
\]

Now use the charge per toner particle from Example 16-6. Let that charge be \(q_p\). Then the number of particles is

\[
N=\frac{Q}{q_p}
\]

and the total mass is

\[
m_{\text{tot}} = N\,m_p
\]

where \(m_p\) is the mass of one toner particle from Example 16-6.
