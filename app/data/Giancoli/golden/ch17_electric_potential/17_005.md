# Problem 17.5

## Question
(I) An electron acquires of kinetic energy when it is accelerated by an electric field from plate A to plate B. What is the potential difference between the plates, and which plate is at the higher potential? 6.45 * 10-16 J

## Final answer from source
4030 V, plate B.

## Worked solution
For a charge moving through a potential difference, the change in electric potential energy is

\[
\Delta U = q\,\Delta V
\]

For an electron, \(q = -e = -1.60\times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}\).

The electron gains kinetic energy, so the electric field does positive work on it. The increase in kinetic energy equals the decrease in electric potential energy:

\[
\Delta K = -\Delta U
\]

Given

\[
\Delta K = 6.45\times 10^{-16}\,\text{J}
\]

so

\[
\Delta U = -6.45\times 10^{-16}\,\text{J}
\]

Now solve for the potential difference:

\[
\Delta V = \frac{\Delta U}{q}
= \frac{-6.45\times 10^{-16}}{-1.60\times 10^{-19}}
\approx 4.03\times 10^3\,\text{V}
\]

Thus the magnitude of the potential difference is

\[
\boxed{4.03\times 10^3\ \text{V}}
\]

To determine which plate is at higher potential: for a negative charge like an electron to accelerate from A to B and gain kinetic energy, it must move toward higher electric potential. Therefore, plate B is at the higher potential.

So the answer is:

\[
\boxed{4030\ \text{V},\ \text{plate B}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Electric potential energy change: \(\Delta U = q\Delta V\)
- Relation between kinetic energy gain and potential energy loss
- Electron charge is negative
- Negative charges accelerate toward higher electric potential
