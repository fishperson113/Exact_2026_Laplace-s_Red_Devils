# Problem 17.55

## Question
(II) A homemade capacitor is assembled by placing two 9-in. pie pans 4 cm apart and connecting them to the opposite terminals of a 9-V battery. Estimate (a) the capacitance, (b) the charge on each plate, (c) the electric field halfway between the plates, and (d) the work done by the battery to charge them. (e) Which of the above values change if a dielectric is inserted? The separation of the plates is then halved. (a) By what factor does the energy stored in the electric field change? (b) How much work must be done to reduce the plate separation from d to …

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) (c) (d) (e) capacitance, charge, work done.

We model the two pie pans as parallel plates:
- diameter of each pan: 9 in = 22.9 cm
- radius: \(r = 11.45\text{ cm} = 0.1145\text{ m}\)
- separation: \(d = 4.0\text{ cm} = 0.040\text{ m}\)

Plate area:
\[
A = \pi r^2 = \pi (0.1145)^2 \approx 4.12\times 10^{-2}\ \text{m}^2
\]

### (a) Capacitance
For a parallel-plate capacitor in air:
\[
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d}
\]
\[
C = (8.85\times 10^{-12})\frac{4.12\times 10^{-2}}{0.040}
\approx 9.1\times 10^{-12}\ \text{F}
\]
So,
\[
\boxed{C \approx 9\ \text{pF}}
\]

### (b) Charge on each plate
With a 9-V battery:
\[
Q = CV = (9.1\times 10^{-12})(9)
\approx 8.2\times 10^{-11}\ \text{C}
\]
So each plate has equal and opposite charge:
\[
\boxed{Q \approx 8\times 10^{-11}\ \text{C}}
\]

### (c) Electric field halfway between the plates
For parallel plates,
\[
E = \frac{V}{d} = \frac{9}{0.040} = 225\ \text{V/m}
\]
Thus,
\[
\boxed{E \approx 2.3\times 10^2\ \text{V/m}}
\]

### (d) Work done by the battery
The battery’s work in charging the capacitor is
\[
W = QV = CV^2
\]
Using \(C \approx 9.1\times 10^{-12}\ \text{F}\):
\[
W = (9.1\times 10^{-12})(9^2)
= (9.1\times 10^{-12})(81)
\approx 7.4\times 10^{-10}\ \text{J}
\]
So,
\[
\boxed{W \approx 7\times 10^{-10}\ \text{J}}
\]

### (e) Which values change if a dielectric is inserted?
If a dielectric fully fills the space while the capacitor remains connected to the battery:
- capacitance increases by factor \(\kappa\)
- charge increases by factor \(\kappa\)
- electric field remains \(V/d\), so it does not change
- work done by the battery changes because \(Q\) changes

So the values that change are:
\[
\boxed{\text{capacitance, charge, work done}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Parallel-plate capacitor approximation
- Capacitance: \(C=\varepsilon_0 A/d\)
- Charge on a capacitor: \(Q=CV\)
- Uniform field between plates: \(E=V/d\)
- Battery work in charging: \(W=QV=CV^2\)
- Effect of a dielectric at constant battery voltage: \(C\) and \(Q\) increase, \(E\) stays fixed
