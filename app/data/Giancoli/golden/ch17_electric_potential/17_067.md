# Problem 17.67

## Question
(III) Electrons are accelerated by 6.0 kV in a CRT. The screen is 30 cm wide and is 34 cm from the 2.6-cm-long deflection plates. Over what range must the horizontally deflecting electric field vary to sweep the beam fully across the screen? (q = ±e)

Let the electron speed after acceleration through 6.0 kV be found from energy conservation:

\[
eV = \frac12 m v^2
\]

so

\[
v=\sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}}
\]

With \(V=6.0\times 10^3\ \text{V}\),

\[
v=\sqrt{\frac{2(1.60\times 10^{-19})(6.0\times 10^3)}{9.11\times 10^{-31}}}
\approx 4.6\times 10^7\ \text{m/s}
\]

The electron passes through deflection plates of length

\[
L = 2.6\ \text{cm} = 0.026\ \text{m}
\]

and then travels from the plates to the screen a distance

\[
D = 34\ \text{cm} = 0.34\ \text{m}
\]

The screen is 30 cm wide, so to sweep the beam from one edge to the other, the spot must move by half the width from center to either edge:

\[
y = \frac{30\ \text{cm}}{2} = 15\ \text{cm} = 0.15\ \text{m}
\]

For motion through the plates, the vertical acceleration magnitude is

\[
a = \frac{eE}{m}
\]

The time in the plates is

\[
t_1=\frac{L}{v}
\]

so the vertical deflection while between the plates is

\[
y_1=\frac12 a t_1^2
\]

The vertical exit speed is

\[
v_y = a t_1
\]

During the drift to the screen, the extra deflection is

\[
y_2 = v_y \left(\frac{D}{v}\right)=a t_1 \frac{D}{v}
\]

Since \(t_1=L/v\),

\[
y = y_1+y_2
= \frac12 \frac{eE}{m}\left(\frac{L}{v}\right)^2
+ \frac{eE}{m}\left(\frac{L}{v}\right)\left(\frac{D}{v}\right)
\]

Factor out \(E\):

\[
y = \frac{eE}{m v^2}\left(\frac{L^2}{2}+LD\right)
\]

Solve for \(E\):

\[
E=\frac{y m v^2}{e\left(\frac{L^2}{2}+LD\right)}
\]

Using \(m v^2 = 2eV\),

\[
E=\frac{2Vy}{\left(\frac{L^2}{2}+LD\right)}
\]

Insert the numbers:

\[
\frac{L^2}{2}=\frac{(0.026)^2}{2}=3.38\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2
\]

\[
LD=(0.026)(0.34)=8.84\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2
\]

\[
\frac{L^2}{2}+LD \approx 9.18\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2
\]

\[
E \approx \frac{2(6.0\times 10^3)(0.15)}{9.18\times 10^{-3}}
\approx 2.0\times 10^5\ \text{V/m}
\]

Thus the field magnitude required to sweep to one edge is on the order of \(2\times 10^5\ \text{V/m}\), and the full sweep across the screen requires the field to vary across the corresponding symmetric sign range about zero.
