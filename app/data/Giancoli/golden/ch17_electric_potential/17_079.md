# Problem 17.79

## Question
The power supply for a pulsed nitrogen laser has a 0.050-mF capacitor with a maximum voltage rating of 35 kV. (a) Estimate how much energy could be stored in this capacitor. (b) If 12% of this stored electrical energy is converted to light energy in a pulse that is 6.2 microseconds long, what is the power of the laser pulse?

## Final answer from source
(a) 31 J; (b)

## Worked solution
For a capacitor, the stored electrical energy is

$$ 
U=\frac{1}{2}CV^2
 $$

### (a) Energy stored in the capacitor
Given:
- $C = 0.050\,\text{mF} = 0.050\times 10^{-3}\,\text{F} = 5.0\times 10^{-5}\,\text{F} $
- $V = 35\,\text{kV} = 3.5\times 10^4\,\text{V} $

Substitute:

$$ 
U=\frac{1}{2}(5.0\times 10^{-5})(3.5\times 10^4)^2
 $$

$$ 
(3.5\times 10^4)^2 = 1.225\times 10^9
 $$

$$ 
U=\frac{1}{2}(5.0\times 10^{-5})(1.225\times 10^9)
 $$

$$ 
U=\frac{1}{2}(6.125\times 10^4)
\approx 3.06\times 10^1\,\text{J}
 $$

$$ 
U \approx 31\,\text{J}
 $$

### (b) Power of the laser pulse
Only 12% of the stored energy becomes light:

$$ 
E_{\text{light}} = 0.12(31\,\text{J}) \approx 3.72\,\text{J}
 $$

The pulse duration is

$$ 
\Delta t = 6.2\,\mu\text{s} = 6.2\times 10^{-6}\,\text{s}
 $$

Power is energy per time:

$$ 
P=\frac{E_{\text{light}}}{\Delta t}
=\frac{3.72}{6.2\times 10^{-6}}
 $$

$$ 
P \approx 6.0\times 10^5\,\text{W}
 $$

So the laser pulse power is

$$ 
\boxed{6.0\times 10^5\,\text{W} \approx 0.60\,\text{MW}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Energy stored in a capacitor: $U=\tfrac{1}{2}CV^2 $
- Unit conversion: mF to F, kV to V, $\mu $s to s
- Fraction of energy converted to light
- Power as $P=E/t $
