# Problem 17.85

## Question
A parallel-plate capacitor with plate area and air- gap separation 0.50 mm is connected to a 12-V battery, and fully charged. The battery is then disconnected. (a) What is the charge on the capacitor? (b) The plates are now pulled to a separation of 0.75 mm. What is the charge on the capacitor now? (c) What is the potential difference between the plates now? (d) How much work was required to pull the plates to their new separation?

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) (c) 18 V; (d)

## Worked solution
The source answer appears incomplete: only part (c) is visible as **18 V**, while parts (a), (b), and (d) are missing. Because the capacitor plate area is also not provided in the problem statement here, the numerical charge and work cannot be determined from the given text alone.

Still, the physics steps are:

1. **Initial charge on the capacitor**
   For a parallel-plate capacitor,
   \[
   C_i=\frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d_i}
   \]
   where \(d_i=0.50\text{ mm}=5.0\times10^{-4}\text{ m}\).

   Since it is connected to a 12-V battery while charging,
   \[
   Q_i=C_iV_i=\frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d_i}(12\text{ V})
   \]

2. **After the battery is disconnected**
   Once disconnected, the capacitor is isolated, so the charge remains constant:
   \[
   Q_f = Q_i
   \]
   Therefore part (b) is the same charge as in part (a).

3. **New potential difference after increasing the separation**
   The plate separation becomes
   \[
   d_f=0.75\text{ mm}
   \]
   With the same charge, the capacitance changes to
   \[
   C_f=\frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d_f}
   \]
   and the voltage is
   \[
   V_f=\frac{Q_f}{C_f}
   \]
   Since \(Q_f\) is constant and \(C\propto 1/d\), the voltage scales as
   \[
   V_f = V_i\frac{d_f}{d_i}
   =12\text{ V}\times\frac{0.75}{0.50}
   =18\text{ V}
   \]
   This matches the visible source answer for part (c).

4. **Work required to pull the plates apart**
   The work done equals the increase in stored electrostatic energy:
   \[
   W=\Delta U = U_f-U_i
   \]
   For fixed charge,
   \[
   U=\frac{Q^2}{2C}
   \]
   so
   \[
   W=\frac{Q^2}{2}\left(\frac{1}{C_f}-\frac{1}{C_i}\right)
   \]
   Using \(C=\varepsilon_0 A/d\),
   \[
   W=\frac{Q^2}{2\varepsilon_0 A}(d_f-d_i)
   \]
   or, in terms of initial and final voltages/capacitances,
   \[
   W = \frac12 C_fV_f^2 - \frac12 C_iV_i^2
   \]
   A numerical value cannot be found from the text provided because the plate area \(A\) is missing.

## Key concepts used
- Parallel-plate capacitor capacitance: \(C=\varepsilon_0 A/d\)
- Charge on a capacitor: \(Q=CV\)
- Isolated capacitor keeps constant charge after battery disconnection
- Voltage changes inversely with capacitance for fixed charge
- Stored energy of a capacitor: \(U=Q^2/(2C)=\tfrac12 CV^2\)
- Work required to change plate separation equals change in stored electrostatic energy
