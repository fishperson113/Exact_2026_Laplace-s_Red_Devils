- The charges are initially separated by \(r_0 = 4.0\ \text{cm} = 0.040\ \text{m}\).
- As the charges repel, the initial electrostatic potential energy is converted into kinetic energy.
- For two point charges,
  \[
  U_i = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r_0}, \qquad U_f \to 0 \text{ as } r \to \infty.
  \]

### (a) One charge released, the other fixed
If only \(Q_1\) is released and \(Q_2\) remains fixed, then energy conservation gives
\[
\frac{k q_1 q_2}{r_0} = \frac12 m_1 v_1^2,
\]
so
\[
v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{2k q_1 q_2}{m_1 r_0}}.
\]

### (b) Both charges released simultaneously
If both charges are released, then momentum is conserved and the center of mass remains at rest. Let the final speeds be \(v_1\) and \(v_2\), with opposite directions and
\[
m_1 v_1 = m_2 v_2.
\]
Energy conservation gives
\[
\frac{k q_1 q_2}{r_0} = \frac12 m_1 v_1^2 + \frac12 m_2 v_2^2.
\]
Using \(v_2 = \frac{m_1}{m_2} v_1\), one finds
\[
\frac{k q_1 q_2}{r_0}
= \frac12 m_1 v_1^2\left(1+\frac{m_1}{m_2}\right),
\]
so
\[
v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{2k q_1 q_2}{r_0\,m_1\left(1+\frac{m_1}{m_2}\right)}}.
