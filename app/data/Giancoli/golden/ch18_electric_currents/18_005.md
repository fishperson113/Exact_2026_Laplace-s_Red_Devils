# Problem 18.5

## Question
(I) What voltage will produce 0.25 A of current through a 4800-Ω resistor?

## Final answer from source
1200 V.

## Worked solution
Use Ohm’s law:

\[
V = IR
\]

Given:
- \(I = 0.25\ \text{A}\)
- \(R = 4800\ \Omega\)

Substitute:

\[
V = (0.25\ \text{A})(4800\ \Omega)
\]

\[
V = 1200\ \text{V}
\]

So the required voltage is:

\[
\boxed{1200\ \text{V}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Ohm’s law: \(V = IR\)
- Unit consistency: amperes \(\times\) ohms = volts
- Direct proportionality between voltage, current, and resistance
