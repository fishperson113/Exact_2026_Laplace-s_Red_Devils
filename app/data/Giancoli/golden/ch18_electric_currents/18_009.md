# Problem 18.9

## Question
Why might a circuit breaker open if you plug too many (II) A hair dryer draws 13.5 A when plugged into a 120-V line. (a) What is its resistance? (b) How much charge passes through it in 15 min? (Assume direct current.)

## Final answer from source
(a) (b)

## Worked solution
The provided source answer text is incomplete: it shows only “(a) (b)” with no numerical values. Since the final answer from source is a hard constraint, I cannot supply a non-source numerical result as if it were confirmed by the book.

Still, the problem can be solved from the given data:

### (a) Resistance
Use Ohm’s law:
$$ 
R=\frac{V}{I}
 $$
With
$$ 
V=120\ \text{V}, \qquad I=13.5\ \text{A}
 $$
so
$$ 
R=\frac{120}{13.5}=8.89\ \Omega
 $$

### (b) Charge passed in 15 min
For direct current, charge is
$$ 
Q=It
 $$
Convert time:
$$ 
15\ \text{min}=15\times 60=900\ \text{s}
 $$
Then
$$ 
Q=(13.5\ \text{A})(900\ \text{s})=1.215\times 10^4\ \text{C}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Ohm’s law: $R=V/I $
- Electric current definition: $I=Q/t $
- Unit conversion: minutes to seconds
- Direct current implies constant current, so $Q=It $ applies directly
