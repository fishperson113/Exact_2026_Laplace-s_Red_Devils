# Problem 18.19

## Question
(II) A rectangular solid made of carbon has sides of lengths 1.0 cm, 2.0 cm, and 4.0 cm, lying along the x, y, and z axes, respectively (Fig. 18-35). Determine the resistance for current that passes through the solid in (a) the x direc- tion, (b) the y direction, and (c) the z direction. Assume the resistivity is r = 3.0 * 10-5 (cid:3)(cid:2)m. y cm 4.0 FIGURE 18-35 Problem 19. z 1.0 cm 2.0 cm x

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) (c)

## Worked solution
For a uniform solid, the resistance for current flowing along one axis is

\[
R=\rho \frac{L}{A}
\]

where:
- \(\rho = 3.0\times 10^{-5}\ \Omega\cdot\text{m}\)
- \(L\) is the dimension along the direction of current
- \(A\) is the cross-sectional area perpendicular to that direction

Convert dimensions to meters:
- \(1.0\text{ cm} = 0.010\text{ m}\)
- \(2.0\text{ cm} = 0.020\text{ m}\)
- \(4.0\text{ cm} = 0.040\text{ m}\)

### (a) Current in the x direction
Current length:

\[
L_x = 0.010\ \text{m}
\]

Cross-sectional area is the \(yz\)-face:

\[
A_x = (0.020)(0.040)=8.0\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2
\]

So,

\[
R_x = (3.0\times 10^{-5})\frac{0.010}{8.0\times 10^{-4}}
=3.75\times 10^{-4}\ \Omega
\]

### (b) Current in the y direction
Current length:

\[
L_y = 0.020\ \text{m}
\]

Cross-sectional area is the \(xz\)-face:

\[
A_y = (0.010)(0.040)=4.0\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2
\]

Thus,

\[
R_y = (3.0\times 10^{-5})\frac{0.020}{4.0\times 10^{-4}}
=1.5\times 10^{-3}\ \Omega
\]

### (c) Current in the z direction
Current length:

\[
L_z = 0.040\ \text{m}
\]

Cross-sectional area is the \(xy\)-face:

\[
A_z = (0.010)(0.020)=2.0\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2
\]

Therefore,

\[
R_z = (3.0\times 10^{-5})\frac{0.040}{2.0\times 10^{-4}}
=6.0\times 10^{-3}\ \Omega
\]

So the resistances are:

\[
\boxed{R_x=3.8\times 10^{-4}\ \Omega,\quad R_y=1.5\times 10^{-3}\ \Omega,\quad R_z=6.0\times 10^{-3}\ \Omega}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Resistance of a uniform conductor: \(R=\rho L/A\)
- Length is taken along the direction of current
- Cross-sectional area is perpendicular to the current
- Unit conversion from cm to m
- Larger length and smaller area produce larger resistance
