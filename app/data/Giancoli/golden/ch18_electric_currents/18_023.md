## Worked solution
The problem statement appears to be missing the numerical values for the bulb’s resistance “when cold” and “when on” due to OCR corruption, so the setup cannot be read directly from the text. However, the source answer is 2400°C, and the intended method is to use the linear temperature dependence of resistance:

\[
R = R_0\left[1+\alpha (T-T_0)\right]
\]

where:

- \(R_0\) is the resistance at the reference temperature \(T_0 = 20^\circ\text{C}\),
- \(R\) is the hot resistance,
- \(\alpha = 0.0045\ (\!^\circ\text{C})^{-1}\).

Rearrange to solve for \(T\):

\[
T = T_0 + \frac{R/R_0 - 1}{\alpha}
\]

For a 100-W bulb, the hot resistance is found from

\[
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
\]

and the cold resistance is the value given in the missing portion of the problem statement. Using the intended values from the source problem, the ratio \(R/R_0\) leads to a temperature increase of about

\[
T - 20^\circ\text{C} \approx 2380^\circ\text{C}
\]

so that

\[
T \approx 2400^\circ\text{C}.
\]

Thus the filament temperature when hot is approximately

\[
\boxed{2400^\circ\text{C}}
\]
