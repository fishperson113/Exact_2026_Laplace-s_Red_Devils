# Problem 18.35

## Question
(II) A 120-V fish-tank heater is rated at 130 W. Calculate (a) the current through the heater when it is operating, and (b) its resistance.

## Final answer from source
(a) 1.1 A; (b)

## Worked solution
For an electrical device, power is related to voltage and current by

$$ 
P = IV
 $$

### (a) Current
Solve for the current:

$$ 
I = \frac{P}{V}
 $$

Substitute the given values:

$$ 
I = \frac{130\ \text{W}}{120\ \text{V}} = 1.083\ \text{A}
 $$

Rounded to two significant figures:

$$ 
I \approx 1.1\ \text{A}
 $$

This matches the source answer for part (a).

### (b) Resistance
For a resistive device,

$$ 
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
 $$

so

$$ 
R = \frac{V^2}{P}
 $$

Substitute the values:

$$ 
R = \frac{(120\ \text{V})^2}{130\ \text{W}}
= \frac{14400}{130}\ \Omega
\approx 1.11\times 10^2\ \Omega
 $$

$$ 
R \approx 1.1\times 10^2\ \Omega \approx 110\ \Omega
 $$
