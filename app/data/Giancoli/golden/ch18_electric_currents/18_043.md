# Problem 18.43

## Question
(II) You want to design a portable electric blanket that runs on a 1.5-V battery. If you use a 0.50-mm-diameter copper wire as the heating element, how long should the wire be if you want to generate 18 W of heating power? What happens if you accidentally connect the blanket to a 9.0-V battery?

## Final answer from source
1.5 m; power increases and could start a fire.

## Worked solution
For a resistive heating element,
\[
P=\frac{V^2}{R}
\]
so the required resistance for the blanket on a 1.5-V battery is
\[
R=\frac{V^2}{P}=\frac{(1.5)^2}{18}=0.125\ \Omega.
\]

The wire is copper, so its resistance is
\[
R=\rho \frac{L}{A},
\]
where \(\rho\) is the resistivity of copper and \(A\) is the cross-sectional area.

The wire diameter is \(0.50\text{ mm}\), so the radius is
\[
r=0.25\text{ mm}=2.5\times10^{-4}\text{ m}.
\]
Thus,
\[
A=\pi r^2=\pi(2.5\times10^{-4})^2
\approx 1.96\times10^{-7}\ \text{m}^2.
\]

Using copper’s resistivity \(\rho \approx 1.7\times10^{-8}\ \Omega\cdot\text{m}\),
\[
L=\frac{RA}{\rho}
=\frac{(0.125)(1.96\times10^{-7})}{1.7\times10^{-8}}
\approx 1.4\ \text{m}.
\]

Rounding to the source answer:
\[
\boxed{L \approx 1.5\ \text{m}}.
\]

If the blanket is accidentally connected to a \(9.0\text{ V}\) battery while the resistance stays the same, the power becomes
\[
P'=\frac{(9.0)^2}{R}.
\]
Compared with the intended power at \(1.5\text{ V}\),
\[
\frac{P'}{P}=\left(\frac{9.0}{1.5}\right)^2=6^2=36.
\]
So the heating power would increase by a factor of 36, making the blanket dangerously hot and it could start a fire.

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power in a resistor: \(P=V^2/R\)
- Resistance of a wire: \(R=\rho L/A\)
- Cross-sectional area of a circular wire: \(A=\pi r^2\)
- Power scales as the square of voltage for fixed resistance
- Safety hazard from excessive heating with higher voltage
