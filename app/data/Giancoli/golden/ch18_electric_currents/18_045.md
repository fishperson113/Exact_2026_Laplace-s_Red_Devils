# Problem 18.45

## Question
(III) A small immersion heater can be used in a car to heat a cup of water for coffee or tea. If the heater can heat 120 mL of water from 25°C to 95°C in 8.0 min, (a) approximately how much current does it draw from the car’s 12-V battery, and (b) what is its resistance? Assume the manufacturer’s claim of 85% efficiency.

## Final answer from source
(a) 7.2 A; (b)

## Worked solution
The source answer appears to be incomplete for part (b), so this needs review.

For the water:
- Volume of water = 120 mL
- Mass of water ≈ 120 g = 0.120 kg
- Temperature change:  
  \[
  \Delta T = 95^\circ\text{C} - 25^\circ\text{C} = 70^\circ\text{C}
  \]
- Specific heat of water:
  \[
  c = 4186 \,\text{J/(kg·}^\circ\text{C)}
  \]

Heat absorbed by the water:
\[
Q = mc\Delta T
\]
\[
Q = (0.120)(4186)(70) \approx 3.52\times 10^4 \,\text{J}
\]

Because the heater is 85% efficient, the electrical energy used is:
\[
E_{\text{in}} = \frac{Q}{0.85} \approx \frac{3.52\times 10^4}{0.85}
\approx 4.14\times 10^4 \,\text{J}
\]

Time:
\[
t = 8.0\,\text{min} = 480\,\text{s}
\]

Electrical power drawn:
\[
P = \frac{E_{\text{in}}}{t}
\approx \frac{4.14\times 10^4}{480}
\approx 86.3\,\text{W}
\]

(a) Current from a 12-V battery:
\[
P = IV \quad \Rightarrow \quad I = \frac{P}{V}
\]
\[
I = \frac{86.3}{12} \approx 7.2\,\text{A}
\]

(b) Resistance:
\[
P = \frac{V^2}{R} \quad \Rightarrow \quad R = \frac{V^2}{P}
\]
\[
R = \frac{12^2}{86.3} \approx 1.67\,\Omega
\]

So the intended result for part (b) is approximately:
\[
R \approx 1.7\,\Omega
\]

## Key concepts used
- Heat gained by water: \(Q = mc\Delta T\)
- Efficiency relates useful output energy to electrical input energy
- Electrical power: \(P = IV\)
- Resistive power relation: \(P = V^2/R\)
- Unit conversions: mL to kg, minutes to seconds
