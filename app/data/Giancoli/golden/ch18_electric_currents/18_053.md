# Problem 18.53

## Question
(II) Determine (a) the maximum instantaneous power dissi- pated by a 2.2-hp pump connected to a ac power source, and (b) the maximum current passing through the pump. 240 -Vrms 8-(cid:3)

## Final answer from source
(a) 3300 W; (b) 9.7 A.

## Worked solution
The pump’s rated power is given in horsepower, so first convert to watts:

\[
P_{\text{avg}} = 2.2\,\text{hp}\left(746\,\frac{\text{W}}{\text{hp}}\right)=1641.2\,\text{W}
\]

For a device on an ac source with sinusoidal current and voltage in phase, the instantaneous power varies as

\[
p(t)=v(t)i(t)=V_{\max}I_{\max}\sin^2(\omega t)
\]

so the maximum instantaneous power is

\[
p_{\max}=V_{\max}I_{\max}
\]

and the average power is

\[
P_{\text{avg}}=\frac{1}{2}V_{\max}I_{\max}=\frac{1}{2}p_{\max}
\]

Hence,

\[
p_{\max}=2P_{\text{avg}}=2(1641.2\,\text{W})=3282.4\,\text{W}\approx 3.3\times 10^3\,\text{W}
\]

This gives part (a):

\[
\boxed{p_{\max}\approx 3300\ \text{W}}
\]

Now use the rms voltage:

\[
V_{\text{rms}}=240\,\text{V}
\]

For sinusoidal ac,

\[
V_{\max}=\sqrt{2}\,V_{\text{rms}}=\sqrt{2}(240\,\text{V})\approx 339\,\text{V}
\]

Then from

\[
p_{\max}=V_{\max}I_{\max}
\]

we get

\[
I_{\max}=\frac{p_{\max}}{V_{\max}}=\frac{3282.4}{339}\approx 9.7\,\text{A}
\]

So part (b) is

\[
\boxed{I_{\max}\approx 9.7\ \text{A}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Power conversion: \(1\,\text{hp}=746\,\text{W}\)
- Relationship between average and maximum power for sinusoidal ac: \(P_{\text{avg}}=\tfrac12 V_{\max}I_{\max}\)
- RMS and peak voltage relation: \(V_{\max}=\sqrt{2}\,V_{\text{rms}}\)
- Maximum current from \(p_{\max}=V_{\max}I_{\max}\)
