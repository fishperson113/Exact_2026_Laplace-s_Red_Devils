# Problem 18.59

## Question
(II) A neuron is stimulated with an electric pulse. The action potential is detected at a point 3.70 cm down the axon 0.0052 s later. When the action potential is detected 7.20 cm from the point of stimulation, the time required is 0.0063 s. What is the speed of the electric pulse along the axon? (Why are two measurements needed instead of only one?)

## Final answer from source
(possible delay between nerve stimulation and generation of action potential).

## Worked solution
Let the pulse travel along the axon with constant speed \(v\), but let there also be an unknown delay \(t_0\) between the instant of stimulation and the actual start of the action potential.

Then the measured time satisfies

\[
t = t_0 + \frac{x}{v}
\]

for a distance \(x\) along the axon.

We are given two measurements:

1. \(x_1 = 3.70\text{ cm} = 0.0370\text{ m}\), \(t_1 = 0.0052\text{ s}\)
2. \(x_2 = 7.20\text{ cm} = 0.0720\text{ m}\), \(t_2 = 0.0063\text{ s}\)

Subtract the two equations to eliminate the unknown delay \(t_0\):

\[
t_2 - t_1 = \frac{x_2 - x_1}{v}
\]

So,

\[
v = \frac{x_2 - x_1}{t_2 - t_1}
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
v = \frac{0.0720 - 0.0370}{0.0063 - 0.0052}
\]

\[
v = \frac{0.0350}{0.0011}
\approx 31.8\text{ m/s}
\]

So the speed of the electric pulse is

\[
\boxed{3.2\times 10^{1}\ \text{m/s}}
\]

or about \(32\text{ m/s}\).

Why are two measurements needed? Because the measured times may include a possible delay between the nerve stimulation and the generation of the action potential, so one measurement alone would not separate that delay from the true travel time.

## Key concepts used
- Constant-speed motion relation: \(x = vt\)
- Need for an intercept term when there is an unknown initial delay
- Using two data points to eliminate an unknown offset
- Unit conversion from cm to m
- Subtracting equations to isolate the speed
