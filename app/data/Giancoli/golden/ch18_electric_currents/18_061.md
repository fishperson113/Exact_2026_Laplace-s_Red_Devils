# Problem 18.61

## Question
A person accidentally leaves a car with the lights on. If each of the two headlights uses 40 W and each of the two taillights 6 W, for a total of 92 W, how long will a fresh 12-V battery last if it is rated at Assume the full 12 V appears across each bulb. 75 A(cid:2)h?

## Final answer from source
9.8 h.

## Worked solution
The battery rating is in ampere-hours, so first interpret the capacity:

- Battery capacity = $75\ \text{A·h} $
- Battery voltage = $12\ \text{V} $

This means the total energy available is

$$ 
E = (75\ \text{A·h})(12\ \text{V}) = 900\ \text{W·h}
 $$

since $1\ \text{A·V} = 1\ \text{W} $.

The total power used by the lights is given as

$$ 
P = 92\ \text{W}
 $$

Using

$$ 
P = \frac{E}{t}
\quad \Rightarrow \quad
t = \frac{E}{P}
 $$

we get

$$ 
t = \frac{900\ \text{W·h}}{92\ \text{W}} \approx 9.78\ \text{h}
 $$

Rounding appropriately:

$$ 
t \approx 9.8\ \text{h}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Electric power $P = IV $
- Battery capacity in ampere-hours
- Energy conversion: $(\text{A·h})(\text{V}) = \text{W·h} $
- Time from energy and power: $t = E/P $
