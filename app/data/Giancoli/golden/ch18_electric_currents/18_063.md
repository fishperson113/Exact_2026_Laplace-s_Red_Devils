# Problem 18.63

## Question
What is the average current drawn by a 1.0-hp 120-V motor? (1 hp = 746 W.)

## Final answer from source
6.22 A.

## Worked solution
The motor’s power output is given as

\[
P = 1.0\,\text{hp}
\]

Using the conversion factor

\[
1\,\text{hp} = 746\,\text{W},
\]

the power is

\[
P = 746\,\text{W}.
\]

For an electrical device, power is related to voltage and current by

\[
P = IV.
\]

Solve for the current:

\[
I = \frac{P}{V}.
\]

Substitute the given values:

\[
I = \frac{746\,\text{W}}{120\,\text{V}} = 6.216\ldots\,\text{A}.
\]

Rounding to three significant figures gives

\[
I \approx 6.22\,\text{A}.
\]

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power relation: \(P = IV\)
- Unit conversion: horsepower to watts
- Solving for current: \(I = P/V\)
- Significant figures and rounding
