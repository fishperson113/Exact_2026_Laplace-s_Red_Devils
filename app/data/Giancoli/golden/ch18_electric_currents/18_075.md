# Problem 18.75

## Question
An electric power plant can produce electricity at a fixed power \(P\), but the plant operator is free to choose the voltage \(V\) at which it is produced. This electricity is carried as an electric current \(I\) through a transmission line (resistance \(R\)) from the plant to the user, where it provides the user \(P'\) with electric power. (a) Show that the reduction in \(\Delta P = P - P'\) due to transmission losses is given by
\[
\Delta P = \frac{P^{2}R}{V^{2}}.
\]
(b) In order to reduce power losses during transmission, should the operator choose \(V\) to be as large or as small as possible?

## Final answer from source
(b) As large as possible.

## Worked solution
Let the power produced at the plant be \(P\). For a transmission line carrying current \(I\) at voltage \(V\),

\[
P = IV.
\]

So the current is

\[
I = \frac{P}{V}.
\]

The power lost as heat in the transmission line resistance \(R\) is

\[
P_{\text{loss}} = I^2 R.
\]

Substitute \(I = P/V\):

\[
P_{\text{loss}} = \left(\frac{P}{V}\right)^2 R
= \frac{P^2 R}{V^2}.
\]

Since the reduction in power delivered to the user is exactly the transmission loss,

\[
\Delta P = P - P' = P_{\text{loss}} = \frac{P^2 R}{V^2}.
\]

For part (b), note that

\[
\Delta P \propto \frac{1}{V^2}.
\]

Thus, increasing \(V\) decreases the power loss. Therefore, to reduce transmission losses, the operator should choose \(V\) to be as large as possible.

## Key concepts used
- Electric power relation: \(P = IV\)
- Transmission loss in a resistor: \(P_{\text{loss}} = I^2R\)
- Substitution of \(I = P/V\)
- Inverse-square dependence of loss on voltage
- Higher transmission voltage reduces resistive losses
