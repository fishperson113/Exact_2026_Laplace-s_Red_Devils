# Problem 18.83

## Question
Lightbulb A is rated at 120 V and 40 W for household appli- cations. Lightbulb B is rated at 12 V and 40 W for automotive applications. (a) What is the current through each bulb? (b) What is the resistance of each bulb? (c) In one hour, how much charge passes through each bulb? (d) In one hour, how much energy does each bulb use? (e) Which bulb requires larger diameter wires to connect its power source and the bulb?

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) (c) (d) (e) Bulb B.

## Worked solution
Use the power relation
\[
P=IV
\]
and Ohm’s law
\[
V=IR
\]
for each bulb.

### (a) Current through each bulb
For bulb A:
\[
I_A=\frac{P}{V}=\frac{40\ \text{W}}{120\ \text{V}}=0.333\ \text{A}
\]

For bulb B:
\[
I_B=\frac{P}{V}=\frac{40\ \text{W}}{12\ \text{V}}=3.33\ \text{A}
\]

### (b) Resistance of each bulb
From
\[
P=\frac{V^2}{R}
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
R=\frac{V^2}{P}
\]

For bulb A:
\[
R_A=\frac{(120\ \text{V})^2}{40\ \text{W}}=\frac{14400}{40}=360\ \Omega
\]

For bulb B:
\[
R_B=\frac{(12\ \text{V})^2}{40\ \text{W}}=\frac{144}{40}=3.6\ \Omega
\]

### (c) Charge passing through each bulb in one hour
Charge is
\[
Q=It
\]
with
\[
t=1.0\ \text{h}=3600\ \text{s}
\]

For bulb A:
\[
Q_A=(0.333\ \text{A})(3600\ \text{s})=1.20\times 10^3\ \text{C}
\]

For bulb B:
\[
Q_B=(3.33\ \text{A})(3600\ \text{s})=1.20\times 10^4\ \text{C}
\]

### (d) Energy used in one hour
Energy is
\[
E=Pt
\]

For either bulb, since each is rated at 40 W:
\[
E=(40\ \text{W})(3600\ \text{s})=1.44\times 10^5\ \text{J}
\]

So both bulbs use the same energy in one hour:
\[
E_A=E_B=1.44\times 10^5\ \text{J}
\]

### (e) Which bulb requires larger diameter wires?
The bulb with the larger current requires thicker wires to reduce heating losses. Since
\[
I_B=3.33\ \text{A} > I_A=0.333\ \text{A},
\]
bulb B requires larger diameter wires.

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power: \(P=IV\)
- Ohm’s law: \(V=IR\)
- Resistance from power: \(R=V^2/P\)
- Charge transported: \(Q=It\)
- Energy used: \(E=Pt\)
- Larger current requires thicker connecting wires to reduce resistive heating
