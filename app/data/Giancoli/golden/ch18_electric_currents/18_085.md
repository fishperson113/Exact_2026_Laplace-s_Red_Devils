# Problem 18.85

## Question
An electric wheelchair is designed to run on a single 12-V battery rated to provide 100 ampere-hours (100 A·h). (a) How much energy is stored in this battery? (b) If the wheelchair experiences an average total retarding force (mainly friction) of 210 N, how far can the wheelchair travel on one charge?

## Final answer from source
(a) (b)

## Worked solution
The battery rating in ampere-hours can be converted to charge:

\[
Q = (100\ \text{A·h})(3600\ \text{s/h}) = 3.60\times 10^5\ \text{C}
\]

### (a) Energy stored in the battery
Electrical energy delivered by a battery is

\[
E = VQ
\]

with \(V = 12\ \text{V}\) and \(Q = 3.60\times 10^5\ \text{C}\):

\[
E = (12)(3.60\times 10^5) = 4.32\times 10^6\ \text{J}
\]

So the energy stored is

\[
\boxed{4.3\times 10^6\ \text{J}}
\]

### (b) Distance the wheelchair can travel
If the retarding force is constant on average, the work done against friction is

\[
W = Fd
\]

Assuming all the battery energy goes into overcoming the retarding force,

\[
Fd = E
\]

so

\[
d = \frac{E}{F} = \frac{4.32\times 10^6\ \text{J}}{210\ \text{N}}
\]

\[
d = 2.06\times 10^4\ \text{m}
\]

Thus the wheelchair can travel

\[
\boxed{2.1\times 10^4\ \text{m} \approx 21\ \text{km}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Convert ampere-hours to coulombs: \(1\ \text{A·h} = 3600\ \text{C}\)
- Electrical energy from a battery: \(E = VQ\)
- Work done against a constant force: \(W = Fd\)
- Equate available energy to work against retarding force
