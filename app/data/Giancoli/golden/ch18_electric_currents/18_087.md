# Problem 18.87

## Question
Copper wire of diameter 0.259 cm is used to connect a set of appliances at 120 V, which draw 1450 W of power total. (a) What power is wasted in 25.0 m of this wire? (b) What is your answer if wire of diameter 0.412 cm is used?

## Final answer from source
(a) 12 W; (b) 4.6 W.

## Worked solution
We use the resistive power loss in the wire:

$$ 
P_{\text{waste}} = I^2 R
 $$

First find the current drawn by the appliances from the supplied power and voltage:

$$ 
I = \frac{P}{V} = \frac{1450\ \text{W}}{120\ \text{V}} \approx 12.1\ \text{A}
 $$

The resistance of a wire is

$$ 
R = \rho \frac{L}{A}
 $$

For copper, take

$$ 
\rho = 1.72\times 10^{-8}\ \Omega\cdot\text{m}
 $$

and the wire length is $L = 25.0\ \text{m} $.

### (a) Diameter $d = 0.259\ \text{cm} $

Convert diameter to meters:

$$ 
d = 0.259\ \text{cm} = 2.59\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}
 $$

So the radius is

$$ 
r = \frac{d}{2} = 1.295\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}
 $$

Area:

$$ 
A = \pi r^2 = \pi(1.295\times 10^{-3})^2 \approx 5.27\times 10^{-6}\ \text{m}^2
 $$

Resistance of the 25.0 m wire:

$$ 
R = (1.72\times 10^{-8})\frac{25.0}{5.27\times 10^{-6}} \approx 8.16\times 10^{-2}\ \Omega
 $$

Power wasted:

$$ 
P_{\text{waste}} = I^2R = (12.1)^2(8.16\times 10^{-2}) \approx 12\ \text{W}
 $$

### (b) Diameter $d = 0.412\ \text{cm} $

Convert diameter to meters:

$$ 
d = 0.412\ \text{cm} = 4.12\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}
 $$

Radius:

$$ 
r = 2.06\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}
 $$

Area:

$$ 
A = \pi r^2 = \pi(2.06\times 10^{-3})^2 \approx 1.33\times 10^{-5}\ \text{m}^2
 $$

Resistance:

$$ 
R = (1.72\times 10^{-8})\frac{25.0}{1.33\times 10^{-5}} \approx 3.23\times 10^{-2}\ \Omega
 $$

Power wasted:

$$ 
P_{\text{waste}} = I^2R = (12.1)^2(3.23\times 10^{-2}) \approx 4.6\ \text{W}
 $$

Thus the wasted power is:

$$ 
\boxed{(a)\ 12\ \text{W}\quad (b)\ 4.6\ \text{W}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power relation $P = VI $
- Resistance of a uniform wire: $R = \rho L/A $
- Cross-sectional area of a circular wire: $A = \pi (d/2)^2 $
- Resistive power loss: $P_{\text{waste}} = I^2R $
- Smaller diameter means larger resistance and greater power loss
