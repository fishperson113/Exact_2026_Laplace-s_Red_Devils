# Problem 19.1

## Question
(I) Calculate the terminal voltage for a battery with an and an emf of 6.00 V when resistor, internal resistance of the battery is connected in series with (a) a and (b) a 0.900 (cid:2) 71.0-(cid:2) resistor. 710-(cid:2)

## Final answer from source
(a) 5.92 V; (b) 5.99 V.

## Worked solution
The OCR for the question is incomplete/mangled: the values of the battery’s internal resistance and the external resistor in part (a) are missing or corrupted. Since the source answer is given, we can only reconstruct the method and verify consistency where possible.

For a battery with emf \( \mathcal{E} \) and internal resistance \(r\), connected to an external resistor \(R\), the terminal voltage is

\[
V_{\text{term}} = \mathcal{E} - Ir
\]

where the current is

\[
I = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R+r}.
\]

So equivalently,

\[
V_{\text{term}} = \mathcal{E}\frac{R}{R+r}.
\]

Given \( \mathcal{E} = 6.00\text{ V} \).

### (a)
The source answer is \(5.92\text{ V}\). This is consistent with a relatively small current and a small drop across the internal resistance.

Using the terminal-voltage relation,

\[
5.92 = 6.00 - Ir
\]

so the internal drop is

\[
Ir = 0.08\text{ V}.
\]

That is the expected magnitude for a battery with small internal resistance connected to a modest external load.

### (b)
The resistor value appears to be \(710\,\Omega\) from the OCR, with an internal resistance of \(0.900\,\Omega\) likely implied by the corrupted text.

Using

\[
V_{\text{term}} = \mathcal{E}\frac{R}{R+r}
\]

with \( \mathcal{E}=6.00\text{ V},\; R=710\,\Omega,\; r=0.900\,\Omega \),

\[
V_{\text{term}} = 6.00\frac{710}{710+0.900}
= 6.00\frac{710}{710.9}
\approx 5.99\text{ V},
\]

which matches the source.

Thus the answers are:

- (a) \(5.92\text{ V}\)
- (b) \(5.99\text{ V}\)

## Key concepts used
- Terminal voltage of a battery with internal resistance
- Ohm’s law for series circuits
- Relation \(V_{\text{term}} = \mathcal{E} - Ir\)
- Relation \(I = \mathcal{E}/(R+r)\)
- Small internal resistance causes a small voltage drop
