# Problem 19.7

## Question
(II) How many 10-(cid:2) to give an equivalent resistance to five nected in parallel? resistors must be connected in series resistors con- 100-(cid:2)

## Final answer from source
2.

## Worked solution
The OCR of the question is incomplete, but the intended problem is evidently asking:

- how many **10-Ω resistors connected in series** are needed
- to produce an equivalent resistance equal to that of **five 100-Ω resistors connected in parallel**.

First find the equivalent resistance of the five 100-Ω resistors in parallel:

\[
\frac{1}{R_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{100}+\frac{1}{100}+\frac{1}{100}+\frac{1}{100}+\frac{1}{100}
= \frac{5}{100}
\]

So,

\[
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{100}{5} = 20\ \Omega
\]

Now let \(n\) be the number of 10-Ω resistors in series. For series resistors:

\[
R_{\text{series}} = n(10\ \Omega)
\]

Set this equal to 20 Ω:

\[
n(10\ \Omega)=20\ \Omega
\]

\[
n=2
\]

So, **2 resistors** are required.
