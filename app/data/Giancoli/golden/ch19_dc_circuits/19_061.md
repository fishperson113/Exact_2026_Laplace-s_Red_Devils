## Worked solution
The OCR is incomplete: the galvanometer resistance is shown as `0.20-(cid:2)` rather than a clear numerical value, and the source answer is truncated to “Add in series with ammeter,”. Because the missing value is essential for a complete numerical derivation, the solution must be treated cautiously.

### 1) Convert the ammeter to a voltmeter
To make a voltmeter from an ammeter, you add a large resistor in **series** with the meter movement. Then the full-scale current of the meter corresponds to the desired full-scale voltage.

So the modification is:
- **connect a resistor in series with the ammeter**.

This matches the source answer.

### 2) Use the full-scale current
The full-scale current is
$$ 
I_{\text{fs}} = 35\ \text{mA} = 0.035\ \text{A}.
 $$

The desired full-scale voltage is
$$ 
V_{\text{fs}} = 25\ \text{V}.
 $$

For a voltmeter built from a meter movement and a series resistor,
$$ 
R_{\text{total}} = \frac{V_{\text{fs}}}{I_{\text{fs}}}
= \frac{25}{0.035}
\approx 714\ \Omega.
 $$

Thus the total resistance required is about
$$ 
R_{\text{total}} \approx 7.14\times 10^2\ \Omega.
 $$

### 3) Series resistor needed
If the galvanometer resistance were known exactly as $R_g $, then the added series resistor would be
$$ 
R_s = R_{\text{total}} - R_g.
 $$
Because the OCR omits the exact $R_g $ value, the exact numerical $R_s $ cannot be reliably computed from the provided text.

### 4) Sensitivity of the voltmeter
The voltmeter sensitivity is commonly expressed as the resistance per volt:
$$ 
S = \frac{R_{\text{total}}}{V_{\text{fs}}}
= \frac{1}{I_{\text{fs}}}.
 $$
With $I_{\text{fs}} = 0.035\ \text{A} $,
$$ 
S = \frac{1}{0.035} \approx 28.6\ \Omega/\text{V}.
 $$

If the textbook’s answer key reports approximately $33\ \Omega/\text{V} $, that would correspond to an OCR or source-text mismatch in the current value. Using the clearly readable full-scale current of $35\ \text{mA} $, the sensitivity is $28.6\ \Omega/\text{V} $.

- Voltmeter sensitivity: $\,S = R_{\text{total}}/V_{\text{fs}} = 1/I_{\text{fs}}\, $
