# Problem 19.73

## Question
One way a multiple-speed ventilation fan for a car can be designed is to put resistors in series with the fan motor. The resistors reduce the current through the motor and make it run more slowly. Suppose the current in the motor is 5.0 A when it is connected directly across a 12-V battery. (a) What series resistor should be used to reduce the current to 2.0 A for low-speed operation? (b) What power rating should the resistor have? Assume that the motor’s resistance is roughly the same at all speeds. 2 , 3 , and

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) 14 W.

## Worked solution
The problem statement appears to have OCR corruption at the end: “2 , 3 , and” and the source answer only shows “(a) (b) 14 W.” Since the part (a) answer is missing from the provided source text, the solution can only be completed for part (b) using the electrical model.

### Step 1: Find the motor resistance
When the motor is connected directly across the 12-V battery, the current is 5.0 A. Using Ohm’s law for the motor:

$$ 
R_{\text{motor}}=\frac{V}{I}=\frac{12\ \text{V}}{5.0\ \text{A}}=2.4\ \Omega
 $$

### Step 2: Find the total resistance needed for 2.0 A
For low-speed operation, the current should be 2.0 A from the same 12-V battery. So the total circuit resistance must be

$$ 
R_{\text{total}}=\frac{V}{I}=\frac{12\ \text{V}}{2.0\ \text{A}}=6.0\ \Omega
 $$

### Step 3: Find the required series resistor
Since the resistor is in series with the motor, resistances add:

$$ 
R_{\text{total}}=R_{\text{motor}}+R_s
 $$

$$ 
R_s=6.0\ \Omega-2.4\ \Omega=3.6\ \Omega
 $$

So the series resistor should be about

$$ 
\boxed{3.6\ \Omega}
 $$

### Step 4: Find the power dissipated by the resistor
The resistor carries the same current as the motor, 2.0 A. Its power dissipation is

$$ 
P=I^2R_s=(2.0\ \text{A})^2(3.6\ \Omega)=14.4\ \text{W}
 $$

Rounded appropriately, the resistor should have a power rating of at least

$$ 
\boxed{14\ \text{W}}
 $$

This matches the source answer for part (b).

## Key concepts used
- Ohm’s law: $V = IR $
- Series resistors add: $R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_2 $
- Electric power in a resistor: $P = I^2R $
- Same current flows through elements in series
