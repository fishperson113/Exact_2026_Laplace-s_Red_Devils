# Problem 20.7

## Question
Which of the following statements is false? The magnetic (II) The force on a wire is a maximum of 8.50 * 10-2 N when placed between the pole faces of a magnet. The current flows horizontally to the right and the magnetic field is vertical. The wire is observed to “jump” toward the observer when the current is turned on. (a) What type of magnetic pole is the top pole face? (b) If the pole faces have a diameter of 10.0 cm, estimate the current in the wire if the field is 0.220 T. (c) If the wire is tipped so that it makes an angle of 10.0° with the horizontal, what force will it now feel? [Hint: What length of wire will now be in the field?]

## Final answer from source
(a) South pole; (b) 3.86 A; (c)

## Worked solution
We use the magnetic force on a current-carrying wire:

\[
F = I L B \sin\theta
\]

where:
- \(F\) is the force,
- \(I\) is the current,
- \(L\) is the length of wire in the magnetic field,
- \(B\) is the magnetic field,
- \(\theta\) is the angle between the current direction and the magnetic field.

### (a) Identify the pole face
The wire is observed to move toward the observer when the current is turned on.

Using the right-hand rule for \(\vec F = I \vec L \times \vec B\), with current to the right and force toward the observer, the magnetic field must be vertical downward. Outside a magnet, field lines go from north to south, so the top pole face must be the **south pole**.

### (b) Find the current
The force is maximum, so the wire is perpendicular to the field and

\[
\sin\theta = 1
\]

Thus,

\[
F = I L B
\]

The length of wire in the field is approximately the pole-face diameter:

\[
L = 10.0\text{ cm} = 0.100\text{ m}
\]

Given:
\[
F = 8.50\times 10^{-2}\text{ N}, \quad B = 0.220\text{ T}
\]

Solve for \(I\):

\[
I = \frac{F}{LB}
= \frac{8.50\times 10^{-2}}{(0.100)(0.220)}
\]

\[
I = \frac{0.0850}{0.0220} \approx 3.86\text{ A}
\]

### (c) Wire tipped by \(10.0^\circ\)
If the wire is tipped, only the portion still inside the field contributes. The hint indicates to adjust the length of wire in the field accordingly.

For a wire of the same total current, the force depends on the length of wire within the field and on the angle between current and field.
