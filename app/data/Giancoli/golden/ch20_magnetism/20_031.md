# Problem 20.31

## Question
(II) A rectangular loop of wire is placed next to a straight wire, as shown in Fig. 20-55. There is a current of 3.5 A in both wires. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net force on the loop. 3.0 cm 3.5 A 3.5 A 5.0 cm FIGURE 20-55 Problem 31. 10.0 cm

## Final answer from source
toward wire.

## Worked solution
Because the problem refers to Fig. 20-55, the exact geometry must be inferred from the OCR text alone. The figure labels indicate:

- current in the straight wire: \(I_1 = 3.5\ \text{A}\)
- current in the loop: \(I_2 = 3.5\ \text{A}\)
- the loop’s side lengths are \(10.0\ \text{cm}\) and \(5.0\ \text{cm}\)
- the near side of the loop is \(3.0\ \text{cm}\) from the straight wire

The net force on a current loop near a long straight wire comes from the magnetic forces on the two vertical sides of the loop. The forces on the top and bottom horizontal segments are equal and opposite, so they cancel.

### 1) Magnetic field from the straight wire
At distance \(r\) from a long straight wire,
\[
B(r)=\frac{\mu_0 I_1}{2\pi r}.
\]

### 2) Force on a straight current segment in a magnetic field
For a segment of length \(L\) carrying current \(I_2\) perpendicular to \(B\),
\[
F = I_2 L B.
\]

The two vertical sides of the loop experience forces in opposite directions:

- the nearer side is at \(r_1 = 3.0\ \text{cm} = 0.030\ \text{m}\)
- the farther side is at \(r_2 = 3.0\ \text{cm} + 5.0\ \text{cm} = 8.0\ \text{cm} = 0.080\ \text{m}\)

The length of each vertical side is
\[
L = 10.0\ \text{cm} = 0.10\ \text{m}.
\]

### 3) Compute the force on each vertical side
Using
\[
F(r)=I_2 L \frac{\mu_0 I_1}{2\pi r},
\]
with \(\mu_0 = 4\pi\times 10^{-7}\ \text{N/A}^2\),

\[
F(r)=\frac{(3.5)(0.10)(4\pi\times10^{-7})(3.5)}{2\pi r}.
\]

Simplify:
\[
F(r)=\frac{(3.5)(3.5)(0.10)(2\times10^{-7})}{r}
=\frac{2.45\times10^{-7}}{r}.
\]

For the near side:
\[
F_1=\frac{2.45\times10^{-7}}{0.030}
=8.17\times10^{-6}\ \text{N}.
\]

For the far side:
\[
F_2=\frac{2.45\times10^{-7}}{0.080}
=3.06\times10^{-6}\ \text{N}.
\]

### 4) Net force
The forces are in opposite directions, so
\[
F_{\text{net}} = F_1 - F_2
= (8.17-3.06)\times10^{-6}
=5.11\times10^{-6}\ \text{N}.
\]

The near side experiences the stronger attraction/repulsion, so the net force is **toward the straight wire**.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic field of a long straight current-carrying wire
- Force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field
- Net force on a rectangular current loop
- Cancellation of forces on opposite horizontal segments
- Inverse-distance dependence of magnetic field, making the nearer side dominate
