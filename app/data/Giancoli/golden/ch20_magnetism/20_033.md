# Problem 20.33

## Question
(II) A long horizontal wire carries 24.0 A of current due north. What is the net magnetic field 20.0 cm due west of the wire if the Earth’s field there points downward, 44° below the horizontal, and has magnitude 5.0 * 10-5 T?

## Final answer from source
below horizontal.

## Worked solution
For a long straight wire, the magnetic field at distance \(r\) is

\[
B_{\text{wire}}=\frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
\]

with
- \(I=24.0\ \text{A}\)
- \(r=20.0\ \text{cm}=0.200\ \text{m}\)
- \(\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\ \text{T·m/A}\)

So

\[
B_{\text{wire}}=\frac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(24.0)}{2\pi(0.200)}
=2.4\times10^{-5}\ \text{T}
\]

### Direction of the wire’s field
Take:
- north = \(+\hat y\)
- east = \(+\hat x\)
- up = \(+\hat z\)

The current is due north. At a point due west of the wire, the right-hand rule gives the wire’s magnetic field pointing vertically downward.

So the wire contributes a field of magnitude

\[
2.4\times10^{-5}\ \text{T downward}
\]

### Earth’s field
The Earth’s field has magnitude

\[
B_E=5.0\times10^{-5}\ \text{T}
\]

and points downward, \(44^\circ\) below the horizontal. Therefore its vertical component is

\[
B_{E,v}=B_E\sin 44^\circ
\]

and its horizontal component is

\[
B_{E,h}=B_E\cos 44^\circ
\]

Numerically,

\[
B_{E,v}=(5.0\times10^{-5})(\sin 44^\circ)\approx3.5\times10^{-5}\ \text{T downward}
\]

\[
B_{E,h}=(5.0\times10^{-5})(\cos 44^\circ)\approx3.6\times10^{-5}\ \text{T horizontal}
\]

### Net field
The wire’s field is vertical downward, so it adds to the downward vertical component of Earth’s field:

\[
B_{\text{net},v}=2.4\times10^{-5}+3.5\times10^{-5}
=5.9\times10^{-5}\ \text{T downward}
\]

The horizontal component remains

\[
B_{\text{net},h}=3.6\times10^{-5}\ \text{T}
\]

Thus the net field points below the horizontal. Its angle below the horizontal is

\[
\theta=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{B_{\text{net},v}}{B_{\text{net},h}}\right)
=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{5.9}{3.6}\right)\approx58^\circ
\]

So the net magnetic field is directed **below horizontal**.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic field of a long straight wire: \(B=\mu_0 I/(2\pi r)\)
- Right-hand rule for the direction of the wire’s magnetic field
- Resolving a vector into horizontal and vertical components
- Adding magnetic fields as vectors component-by-component
