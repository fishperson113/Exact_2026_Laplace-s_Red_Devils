# Problem 20.35

## Question
(II) Determine the magnetic field midway between two long straight wires 2.0 cm apart in terms of the current I in one when the other carries 25 A. Assume these currents are (a) in the same direction, and (b) in opposite directions.

## Final answer from source
(a) (b)

## Worked solution
For a long straight wire, the magnetic field a distance \(r\) from the wire is

\[
B=\frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}.
\]

The wires are \(2.0\ \text{cm}\) apart, so the midpoint is

\[
r = 1.0\ \text{cm} = 0.010\ \text{m}
\]

from each wire.

Let the current in one wire be \(I\), and the current in the other be \(25\ \text{A}\). Then the magnitudes of the fields at the midpoint are

\[
B_I=\frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}, \qquad B_{25}=\frac{\mu_0 (25)}{2\pi r}.
\]

### (a) Currents in the same direction
At the midpoint, the magnetic fields from the two wires point in opposite directions by the right-hand rule, so the net field is the difference:

\[
B_{\text{net}}=\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi r}\,|I-25|.
\]

Substitute \(r=0.010\ \text{m}\):

\[
B_{\text{net}}=\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi(0.010)}\,|I-25|.
\]

Since

\[
\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi}=2.0\times 10^{-7}\ \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A},
\]

we get

\[
B_{\text{net}}=(2.0\times 10^{-5})\,|I-25|\ \text{T}.
\]

### (b) Currents in opposite directions
At the midpoint, the magnetic fields from the two wires point in the same direction, so the net field is the sum:

\[
B_{\text{net}}=\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi r}(I+25).
\]

Substitute \(r=0.010\ \text{m}\):

\[
B_{\text{net}}=\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi(0.010)}(I+25)
=(2.0\times 10^{-5})(I+25)\ \text{T}.
\]

So the answers are:

\[
\boxed{(a)\ B=(2.0\times 10^{-5})\,|I-25|\ \text{T}}
\]

\[
\boxed{(b)\ B=(2.0\times 10^{-5})\,(I+25)\ \text{T}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic field of a long straight current-carrying wire: \(B=\mu_0 I/(2\pi r)\)
- Superposition of magnetic fields
- Right-hand rule for field directions
- Midpoint distance between two wires is half the separation
