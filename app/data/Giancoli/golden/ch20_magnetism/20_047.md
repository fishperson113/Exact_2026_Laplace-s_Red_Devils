# Problem 20.47

## Question
(II) A 550-turn horizontal solenoid is 15 cm long. The current in its coils is 38 A. A straight wire cuts through the center of the solenoid, along a 3.0-cm diameter. This wire carries a 22-A current downward (and is connected by other wires that don’t concern us). What is the force on this wire assuming the solenoid’s magnetic field points due east?

## Final answer from source
0.12 N, south.

## Worked solution
The magnetic force on a straight current-carrying wire in a uniform magnetic field is

\[
F = I L B \sin\theta
\]

where:
- \(I\) is the current in the wire,
- \(L\) is the length of wire in the field,
- \(B\) is the magnetic field strength,
- \(\theta\) is the angle between the wire current direction and the magnetic field.

### 1) Find the magnetic field inside the solenoid
For an ideal solenoid,

\[
B = \mu_0 n I_s
\]

with
\[
n = \frac{N}{L_{\text{sol}}}
\]

Given:
- \(N = 550\)
- \(L_{\text{sol}} = 15\text{ cm} = 0.15\text{ m}\)
- \(I_s = 38\text{ A}\)

So,

\[
n = \frac{550}{0.15} = 3667\ \text{turns/m}
\]

Then

\[
B = (4\pi\times10^{-7})(3667)(38)
\]

\[
B \approx 0.175\ \text{T}
\]

### 2) Find the length of wire inside the field
The wire passes through the center along a 3.0-cm diameter, so the length inside the solenoid is

\[
L = 3.0\text{ cm} = 0.030\text{ m}
\]

### 3) Compute the force magnitude
The wire current is downward, while the solenoid’s magnetic field points due east, so the angle between them is \(90^\circ\). Thus \(\sin\theta = 1\).

\[
F = (22)(0.030)(0.175)
\]

\[
F \approx 0.116\ \text{N} \approx 0.12\ \text{N}
\]

### 4) Determine the direction
Use the right-hand rule for \(\vec{F} = I\vec{L}\times \vec{B}\):

- Current is downward.
- Magnetic field is due east.

The cross product of downward with east gives a force toward the **south**.

Therefore, the force is:

\[
\boxed{0.12\ \text{N, south}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic field inside an ideal solenoid: \(B=\mu_0 n I\)
- Number of turns per unit length: \(n=N/L\)
- Magnetic force on a current-carrying wire: \(F=ILB\sin\theta\)
- Right-hand rule for \(\vec{F}=I\vec{L}\times\vec{B}\)
