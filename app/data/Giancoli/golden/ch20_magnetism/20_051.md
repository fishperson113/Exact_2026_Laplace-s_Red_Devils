# Problem 20.51

## Question
(I) A single square loop of wire 22.0 cm on a side is placed with its face parallel to the magnetic field as in Fig. 20-34b. 0.325 m(cid:2)N. When 5.70 A flows in the coil, the torque on it is What is the magnetic field strength?

## Final answer from source
1.18 T.

## Worked solution
The problem text appears to be missing or garbled in one place:  
“0.325 m(cid:2)N. When 5.70 A flows in the coil, the torque on it is What is the magnetic field strength?”  
This strongly suggests the intended statement is that the torque is **0.325 m·N·m** (or equivalently 0.325 N·m if the OCR omitted a symbol), and we are to find the magnetic field strength.

For a current loop in a magnetic field, the torque is

\[
\tau = N I A B \sin\theta
\]

where:
- \(N = 1\) for a single loop,
- \(I = 5.70\ \text{A}\),
- \(A\) is the area of the square loop,
- \(B\) is the magnetic field,
- \(\theta\) is the angle between the loop’s normal and the magnetic field.

The loop’s **face is parallel to the magnetic field**, so the loop’s normal is perpendicular to \(\vec B\). Thus,

\[
\theta = 90^\circ,\qquad \sin\theta = 1
\]

So the torque magnitude becomes

\[
\tau = IAB
\]

### 1) Compute the area of the square loop
Side length:

\[
s = 22.0\ \text{cm} = 0.220\ \text{m}
\]

Area:

\[
A = s^2 = (0.220)^2 = 0.0484\ \text{m}^2
\]

### 2) Solve for \(B\)

Assuming the torque is \(0.325\ \text{N·m}\),

\[
B = \frac{\tau}{IA}
\]

\[
B = \frac{0.325}{(5.70)(0.0484)}
\]

\[
B = \frac{0.325}{0.27588} \approx 1.18\ \text{T}
\]

This matches the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic torque on a current loop: \(\tau = N I A B \sin\theta\)
- For a single loop, \(N=1\)
- If the loop’s face is parallel to \(\vec B\), then \(\theta = 90^\circ\) and \(\sin\theta = 1\)
- Area of a square: \(A = s^2\)
- Solve for magnetic field: \(B = \tau/(IA)\)
