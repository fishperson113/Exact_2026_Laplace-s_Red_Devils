# Problem 20.55

## Question
(I) Protons move in a circle of radius 6.10 cm in a 0.566-T magnetic field. What value of electric field could make their paths straight? In what direction must the electric field point?

## Final answer from source
perpendicular to velocity and magnetic field, and in opposite direction to magnetic force on protons.

## Worked solution
A proton moving in a magnetic field experiences the magnetic force

$$ 
F_B = qvB
 $$

when the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field, as is implied by circular motion.

For the path to be straight, the electric force must cancel the magnetic force:

$$ 
F_E = F_B
 $$

Since

$$ 
F_E = qE
 $$

we set

$$ 
qE = qvB
 $$

so

$$ 
E = vB
 $$

To determine $v $, use the fact that the proton moves in a circle of radius $r $ under magnetic force alone:

$$ 
qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}
 $$

Solve for $v $:

$$ 
v = \frac{qBr}{m}
 $$

Then

$$ 
E = vB = \frac{qB^2r}{m}
 $$

Now substitute:
- $q = 1.60\times 10^{-19}\,\text{C} $
- $m_p = 1.67\times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg} $
- $B = 0.566\,\text{T} $
- $r = 6.10\,\text{cm} = 0.0610\,\text{m} $

$$ 
E = \frac{(1.60\times 10^{-19})(0.566)^2(0.0610)}{1.67\times 10^{-27}}
 $$

$$ 
E \approx 2.0\times 10^6\ \text{V/m}
 $$

So the electric field must have magnitude

$$ 
\boxed{2.0\times 10^6\ \text{V/m}}
 $$

and it must point opposite the magnetic force on the protons, so that the electric force cancels it and the path becomes straight. Equivalently, the electric field is perpendicular to both the velocity and the magnetic field.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a moving charge: $F_B = qvB $
- Electric force on a charge: $F_E = qE $
- Circular motion under magnetic force: $qvB = mv^2/r $
- Straight-line motion requires net force $=0 $
- Electric field direction must oppose the magnetic force on a positive charge
