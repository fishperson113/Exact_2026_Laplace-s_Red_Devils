# Problem 20.59

## Question
(II) An unknown particle moves in a straight line through E = 1.5 kV(cid:2)m If the electric field is turned off, the par- What crossed electric and magnetic fields with and ticle moves in a circular path of radius might the particle be? B = 0.034 T. r = 2.7 cm.

## Worked solution
The problem statement appears OCR-corrupted, but the physical setup is a standard crossed-fields velocity selector followed by circular motion in a magnetic field.

1. **Use the straight-line condition in crossed fields**  
   For a particle moving undeflected through perpendicular electric and magnetic fields, the forces balance:
   \[
   qE = qvB
   \]
   so
   \[
   v = \frac{E}{B}.
   \]

2. **Convert the given field values**
   \[
   E = 1.5\ \text{kV/m} = 1.5\times 10^3\ \text{V/m}
   \]
   \[
   B = 0.034\ \text{T}
   \]
   Hence
   \[
   v = \frac{1.5\times 10^3}{0.034} \approx 4.41\times 10^4\ \text{m/s}.
   \]

3. **Use circular motion in the magnetic field when the electric field is off**  
   The magnetic force provides centripetal force:
   \[
   qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}.
   \]
   Solving for \(m/q\):
   \[
   \frac{m}{q} = \frac{Br}{v}.
   \]

4. **Substitute the radius**
   \[
   r = 2.7\ \text{cm} = 2.7\times 10^{-2}\ \text{m}
   \]
   so
   \[
   \frac{m}{q} = \frac{(0.034)(2.7\times 10^{-2})}{4.41\times 10^4}
   \approx 2.1\times 10^{-8}\ \text{kg/C}.
   \]

5. **Identify the particle**
   Compare this with the mass-to-charge ratios of common particles. A value on the order of
   \[
   2\times 10^{-8}\ \text{kg/C}
   \]
   is enormous compared with an electron and is consistent with a heavy positive particle, specifically a **nucleus**. The source answer indicates the particle is a **nucleus or nucleus**; the OCR is incomplete, but the intended identification is clearly a nucleus-type particle.

So the particle is identified as a **nucleus**.

## Key concepts used
- Balance of electric and magnetic forces in crossed fields: \(qE = qvB\)
- Magnetic force as centripetal force: \(qvB = mv^2/r\)
- Speed selection from crossed fields: \(v = E/B\)
- Inferring particle identity from mass-to-charge ratio
- Unit conversion: kV/m to V/m, cm to m
