# Problem 20.61

## Question
(I) A long thin iron-core solenoid has 380 loops of wire per meter, and a 350-mA current flows through the wire. If the 3000m permeability of the iron is what is the total field B inside the solenoid? 0 ,

## Final answer from source
0.5 T.

## Worked solution
For a long solenoid, the magnetic field inside is

\[
B = \mu n I
\]

where:
- \(\mu\) is the permeability of the core material,
- \(n = 380\ \text{turns/m}\),
- \(I = 350\ \text{mA} = 0.350\ \text{A}\).

For iron, the permeability is given as

\[
\mu = 3000\,\mu_0
\]

with

\[
\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\ \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}.
\]

So,

\[
B = (3000)(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(380)(0.350)
\]

First compute the numeric factor:

\[
380 \times 0.350 = 133
\]

\[
(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(133) \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-4}
\]

Then multiply by 3000:

\[
B \approx 3000(1.67 \times 10^{-4}) \approx 0.50\ \text{T}
\]

Thus, the total magnetic field inside the iron-core solenoid is

\[
\boxed{0.5\ \text{T}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic field inside a long solenoid: \(B=\mu n I\)
- Permeability of a magnetic material: \(\mu = \mu_r \mu_0\)
- Unit conversion: \(350\ \text{mA} = 0.350\ \text{A}\)
- Substitution and evaluation with SI units
