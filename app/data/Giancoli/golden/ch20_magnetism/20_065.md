# Problem 20.65

## Question
Protons with momentum are magnet- ically steered clockwise in a circular path 2.2 m in diameter. Determine the magnitude and direction of the field in the magnets surrounding the beam pipe. 4.8 * 10-21 kg(cid:2)m(cid:2)s

## Final answer from source
upward.

## Worked solution
A proton moving in a circular path in a magnetic field satisfies

\[
r=\frac{p}{qB}
\]

so

\[
B=\frac{p}{qr}.
\]

Given:
- momentum \(p = 4.8\times 10^{-21}\ \text{kg·m/s}\)
- diameter \(= 2.2\ \text{m}\), so radius \(r = 1.1\ \text{m}\)
- proton charge \(q = +1.60\times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}\)

Compute the field magnitude:

\[
B=\frac{4.8\times 10^{-21}}{(1.60\times 10^{-19})(1.1)}
\approx 2.7\times 10^{-2}\ \text{T}.
\]

Direction: the protons are positively charged and move clockwise in the circular path. Using the right-hand rule for \(\vec F = q\,\vec v \times \vec B\), the magnetic field must point upward to produce the required centripetal force.

Therefore,

\[
\boxed{B \approx 2.7\times 10^{-2}\ \text{T},\ \text{upward}}
\]

which is consistent with the source answer direction.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force provides centripetal force for circular motion
- Momentum relation for charged particles in a magnetic field: \(r = p/(qB)\)
- Right-hand rule for the direction of \(\vec v \times \vec B\)
- Positive charge motion and force direction in circular paths
