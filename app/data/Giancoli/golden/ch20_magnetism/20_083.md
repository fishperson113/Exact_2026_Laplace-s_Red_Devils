# Problem 20.83

## Question
A uniform conducting rod of length and mass m sits atop a fulcrum, which is placed a distance from the rod’s left-hand end and is immersed in a uniform magnetic field of magnitude B directed into the page (Fig. 20-69). An object whose mass M is 6.0 times greater than the rod’s mass is hung from the rod’s left-hand end. What current (direction and magnitude) should flow through the rod in order for it to be “balanced” (i.e., be at rest horizontally) on the fulcrum? (Flexible connecting wires which exert negligible force on the rod are not shown.) l/4 B B m l FIGURE 20-69 Problem 83. M 0.50 * 10-4 T

## Final answer from source
to the left.

## Worked solution
The problem statement appears to be incomplete/OCR-corrupted in the prompt: the fulcrum location is described as “a distance” from the left-hand end, but the numerical distance is missing, and the figure text is also garbled. Since the source answer provided is only a direction, we can still determine the current direction from torque balance.

### 1) Forces and torques on the rod
- The rod has weight \(mg\), acting at its center of mass, i.e. at the midpoint \(x = \ell/2\).
- The hanging object has weight \(Mg\) at the left end.
- The rod carries current in a uniform magnetic field \(\mathbf B\) into the page, so it experiences a magnetic force
  \[
  \mathbf F_B = I \, \mathbf L \times \mathbf B
  \]
  on the portion of the rod in the field.

### 2) Direction of the magnetic force
Take the rod horizontal. If the current flows **to the left**, then \(\mathbf L\) points left and \(\mathbf B\) points into the page. Using the right-hand rule for \(\mathbf L \times \mathbf B\), the magnetic force on the rod is **downward**. If the current flowed to the right, the magnetic force would be upward.

### 3) Torque balance about the fulcrum
To keep the rod horizontal, the net torque about the fulcrum must be zero.

The hanging mass \(M = 6m\) produces a torque tending to rotate the rod one way, and the rod’s own weight plus the magnetic force must provide the opposite torque. Because the source answer specifies only the current direction, the crucial result is that the magnetic force must act in the direction that gives the opposing torque, which corresponds to a current **to the left**.

### 4) Current magnitude
A numerical magnitude cannot be reliably extracted from the prompt because the fulcrum position and the exact geometry from Fig. 20-69 are missing/corrupted in the OCR. Therefore, only the direction can be stated confidently from the provided source answer.

So, the required current direction is:

\[
\boxed{\text{to the left}}
\]
