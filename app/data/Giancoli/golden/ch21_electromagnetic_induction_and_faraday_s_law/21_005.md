# Problem 21.5

## Question
(II) An 18.5-cm-diameter loop of wire is initially oriented perpendicular to a 1.5-T magnetic field. The loop is rotated so that its plane is parallel to the field direction in 0.20 s. What is the average induced emf in the loop?

## Final answer from source
0.20 V.

## Worked solution
Use Faraday’s law for the average induced emf:

$$ 
|\mathcal{E}_{\text{avg}}|=\frac{|\Delta \Phi_B|}{\Delta t}
 $$

where magnetic flux is

$$ 
\Phi_B = BA\cos\theta
 $$

Here:
- Diameter of loop = $18.5\text{ cm} $, so radius
  $$ 
  r=\frac{18.5}{2}\text{ cm}=9.25\text{ cm}=0.0925\text{ m}
   $$
- Area of the loop:
  $$ 
  A=\pi r^2=\pi(0.0925)^2 \approx 0.0269\text{ m}^2
   $$
- Magnetic field:
  $$ 
  B=1.5\text{ T}
   $$

### Initial flux
The loop is initially oriented perpendicular to the magnetic field, so the loop’s area vector is parallel to $B $. Thus $\theta=0^\circ $, and

$$ 
\Phi_i = BA
 $$

### Final flux
The loop is rotated so its plane is parallel to the field. Then the area vector is perpendicular to $B $, so $\theta=90^\circ $, and

$$ 
\Phi_f = BA\cos 90^\circ = 0
 $$

### Change in flux
$$ 
|\Delta \Phi_B| = |\Phi_f-\Phi_i| = BA
 $$

So

$$ 
|\mathcal{E}_{\text{avg}}|=\frac{BA}{\Delta t}
 $$

Substitute values:

$$ 
|\mathcal{E}_{\text{avg}}|=\frac{(1.5)(0.0269)}{0.20}
\approx \frac{0.0404}{0.20}
\approx 0.202\text{ V}
 $$

Rounding appropriately:

$$ 
\boxed{0.20\text{ V}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Faraday’s law of induction: $\,|\mathcal{E}_{\text{avg}}| = |\Delta \Phi_B|/\Delta t $
- Magnetic flux: $\,\Phi_B = BA\cos\theta $
- Relationship between loop orientation and flux
- Area of a circular loop: $\,A=\pi r^2 $
