# Problem 21.21

## Question
(II) A 550-loop circular armature coil with a diameter of 8.0 cm rotates at in a uniform magnetic field of strength 0.55 T. (a) What is the rms voltage output of the generator? (b) What would you do to the rotation fre- quency in order to double the rms voltage output? 120 rev(cid:2)s

## Final answer from source
(a) 810 V; (b) double the rotation frequency.

## Worked solution
For a rotating coil generator, the induced emf is

$$ 
\varepsilon(t)=N B A \omega \sin(\omega t)
 $$

so the peak emf is

$$ 
\varepsilon_0 = N B A \omega
 $$

and the rms value is

$$ 
\varepsilon_{\text{rms}}=\frac{\varepsilon_0}{\sqrt{2}}
=\frac{N B A \omega}{\sqrt{2}}.
 $$

### Given
- Number of loops: $N=550 $
- Diameter: $d=8.0\text{ cm}\Rightarrow r=4.0\text{ cm}=0.040\text{ m} $
- Magnetic field: $B=0.55\text{ T} $
- Rotation frequency: $120\ \text{rev/s} $

Convert the rotation frequency to angular speed:

$$ 
\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi(120)=240\pi \approx 754\ \text{rad/s}.
 $$

Compute the coil area:

$$ 
A=\pi r^2=\pi(0.040)^2=\pi(0.0016)\approx 5.03\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2.
 $$

Now calculate the peak emf:

$$ 
\varepsilon_0 = (550)(0.55)(5.03\times 10^{-3})(754).
 $$

First,

$$ 
(550)(0.55)=302.5,
 $$

then

$$ 
302.5(5.03\times 10^{-3})\approx 1.52,
 $$

and

$$ 
1.52(754)\approx 1.15\times 10^3\ \text{V}.
 $$

So the rms emf is

$$ 
\varepsilon_{\text{rms}}=\frac{1.15\times 10^3}{\sqrt{2}}
\approx 8.1\times 10^2\ \text{V}.
 $$

Thus,

$$ 
\boxed{810\ \text{V}}
 $$

for part (a).

### Part (b)
Since

$$ 
\varepsilon_{\text{rms}}=\frac{N B A \omega}{\sqrt{2}},
 $$

the rms voltage is directly proportional to the angular speed $\omega $, and therefore directly proportional to the rotation frequency $f $. To double the rms voltage, you must double $f $.

So the rotation frequency should be increased by a factor of 2.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic flux and Faraday’s law for a rotating coil generator
- Peak emf of a generator: $\varepsilon_0 = NAB\omega $
- RMS relation for sinusoidal voltage: $\varepsilon_{\text{rms}}=\varepsilon_0/\sqrt{2} $
- Angular speed and frequency relation: $\omega = 2\pi f $
- Linear proportionality of emf with rotation frequency
