# Problem 21.31

## Question
(II) A model-train transformer plugs into 120-V ac and draws 0.35 A while supplying 6.8 A to the train. (a) What voltage is present across the tracks? (b) Is the transformer step-up or step-down?

## Final answer from source
(a) 6.2 V; (b) step-down.

## Worked solution
For an ideal transformer, input power equals output power:

\[
P_{\text{in}} = P_{\text{out}}
\]

Using \(P = IV\),

\[
I_p V_p = I_s V_s
\]

where:
- \(V_p = 120\ \text{V}\) is the primary (input) voltage,
- \(I_p = 0.35\ \text{A}\) is the primary current,
- \(I_s = 6.8\ \text{A}\) is the secondary current,
- \(V_s\) is the secondary voltage across the tracks.

Solve for \(V_s\):

\[
V_s = \frac{I_p V_p}{I_s}
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
V_s = \frac{(0.35)(120)}{6.8}
\]

\[
V_s = \frac{42}{6.8} \approx 6.18\ \text{V}
\]

Rounding to two significant figures:

\[
V_s \approx 6.2\ \text{V}
\]

So the voltage across the tracks is \(6.2\ \text{V}\).

For part (b), the transformer reduces the voltage from \(120\ \text{V}\) to \(6.2\ \text{V}\), so it is a **step-down** transformer.

## Key concepts used
- Ideal transformer power conservation: \(I_pV_p = I_sV_s\)
- Solving for secondary voltage
- Step-up vs. step-down transformer classification based on voltage change
