# Problem 21.45

## Question
(III) A physics professor wants to demonstrate the large size of the henry unit. On the outside of a 12-cm-diameter plastic hollow tube, she wants to wind an air-filled solenoid with self-inductance of 1.0 H using copper wire with a 0.81-mm diameter. The solenoid is to be tightly wound with each turn touching its neighbor (the wire has a thin insulating layer on its surface so the neighboring turns are not in electrical contact). How long will the plastic tube need to be and how many kilometers of copper wire will be required? What will be the resistance of this solenoid? l

## Final answer from source
46 m, 21 km;

## Worked solution
For a long air-core solenoid,

$$ 
L=\mu_0 \frac{N^2 A}{\ell}
 $$

where:
- $L=1.0\ \text{H} $
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi\times 10^{-7}\ \text{H/m} $
- $N $ is the number of turns
- $A=\pi r^2 $ is the cross-sectional area
- $\ell $ is the solenoid length

The tube diameter is $12\ \text{cm} $, so

$$ 
r=0.060\ \text{m}
 $$

and

$$ 
A=\pi(0.060)^2=1.13\times 10^{-2}\ \text{m}^2.
 $$

Because the wire is tightly wound and each turn touches the next, the turn spacing equals the wire diameter:

$$ 
d = 0.81\ \text{mm}=8.1\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}.
 $$

Hence the number of turns is

$$ 
N=\frac{\ell}{d}.
 $$

Substitute into the inductance formula:

$$ 
L=\mu_0 \frac{(\ell/d)^2 A}{\ell}
=\mu_0 \frac{\ell A}{d^2}.
 $$

Solve for $\ell $:

$$ 
\ell=\frac{L d^2}{\mu_0 A}.
 $$

Now compute:

$$ 
\ell=\frac{(1.0)(8.1\times 10^{-4})^2}{(4\pi\times 10^{-7})(1.13\times 10^{-2})}
\approx 4.6\times 10^{1}\ \text{m}.
 $$

So the tube must be about

$$ 
\boxed{46\ \text{m}}.
 $$

Next, find the number of turns:

$$ 
N=\frac{46}{8.1\times 10^{-4}}\approx 5.7\times 10^4.
 $$

The length of wire is approximately one circumference per turn:

$$ 
\text{wire length} = N(2\pi r)
 $$

with $r=0.060\ \text{m} $, so

$$ 
2\pi r = 2\pi(0.060)\approx 0.377\ \text{m}.
 $$

Thus

$$ 
\text{wire length}\approx (5.7\times 10^4)(0.377)\approx 2.1\times 10^4\ \text{m}
= 21\ \text{km}.
 $$

So the required copper wire length is

$$ 
\boxed{21\ \text{km}}.
 $$

For the resistance, use

$$ 
R=\rho \frac{L_{\text{wire}}}{A_{\text{wire}}},
 $$

where for copper $\rho \approx 1.7\times 10^{-8}\ \Omega\cdot\text{m} $.

The wire diameter is $0.81\ \text{mm} $, so

$$ 
A_{\text{wire}}=\pi\left(\frac{0.81\times 10^{-3}}{2}\right)^2
\approx 5.15\times 10^{-7}\ \text{m}^2.
 $$

Then

$$ 
R \approx \frac{(1.7\times 10^{-8})(2.1\times 10^4)}{5.15\times 10^{-7}}
\approx 6.9\times 10^2\ \Omega.
 $$

So the resistance is approximately

$$ 
\boxed{7.0\times 10^2\ \Omega}.
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Inductance of a long air-core solenoid: $L=\mu_0 N^2A/\ell $
- Tight winding means turn spacing equals wire diameter
- Wire length is number of turns times circumference
- Resistance from $R=\rho L/A $
- Unit conversion and dimensional consistency
