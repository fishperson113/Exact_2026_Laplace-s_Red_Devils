# Problem 21.73

## Question
(III) A 2200-pF capacitor is charged to 120 V and then quickly connected to an inductor. The frequency of oscillation is observed to be 19 kHz. Determine (a) the inductance, (b) the peak value of the current, and (c) the maximum energy stored in the magnetic field of the inductor.

## Final answer from source
(a) 0.032 H; (b) 0.032 A; (c)

## Worked solution
For an ideal LC oscillator,

\[
f=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}
\]

where  
- \(f=19\,\text{kHz}=1.9\times 10^4\,\text{Hz}\)
- \(C=2200\,\text{pF}=2.2\times 10^{-9}\,\text{F}\)

### (a) Inductance
Solve for \(L\):

\[
L=\frac{1}{(2\pi f)^2 C}
\]

Substitute values:

\[
L=\frac{1}{(2\pi \cdot 1.9\times 10^4)^2(2.2\times 10^{-9})}
\approx 3.2\times 10^{-2}\,\text{H}
\]

So,

\[
\boxed{L=0.032\,\text{H}}
\]

### (b) Peak current
The initial energy is all electric potential energy in the capacitor:

\[
U=\frac{1}{2}CV^2
\]

with \(V=120\,\text{V}\). In an LC circuit, this becomes magnetic energy at the moment of maximum current:

\[
\frac{1}{2}LI_{\max}^2=\frac{1}{2}CV^2
\]

Thus,

\[
I_{\max}=V\sqrt{\frac{C}{L}}
\]

Substitute:

\[
I_{\max}=120\sqrt{\frac{2.2\times 10^{-9}}{3.2\times 10^{-2}}}
\approx 3.2\times 10^{-2}\,\text{A}
\]

So,

\[
\boxed{I_{\max}=0.032\,\text{A}}
\]

### (c) Maximum magnetic energy
This equals the initial energy stored in the capacitor:

\[
U_{\max}=\frac{1}{2}CV^2
\]

\[
U_{\max}=\frac{1}{2}(2.2\times 10^{-9})(120)^2
\]

\[
U_{\max}=1.58\times 10^{-5}\,\text{J}
\]

So the maximum magnetic energy is

\[
\boxed{1.6\times 10^{-5}\,\text{J}}
\]

## Key concepts used
- LC oscillations and resonance frequency formula
- Energy conservation in an ideal LC circuit
- Capacitor energy: \(U=\tfrac{1}{2}CV^2\)
- Magnetic energy in an inductor: \(U=\tfrac{1}{2}LI^2\)
