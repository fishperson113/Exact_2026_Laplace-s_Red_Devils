# Problem 21.81

## Question
A pair of power transmission lines each have a 0.95-Ω resistance and carry 740 A over 9.0 km. If the rms input voltage is 42 kV, calculate (a) the voltage at the other end, (b) the power input, (c) power loss in the lines, and (d) the power output.

## Final answer from source
(a) 41 kV; (b) 31 MW; (c) 1.0 MW; (d) 30 MW.

## Worked solution
Treat the two transmission wires as having a total series resistance of

\[
R_{\text{total}} = 0.95\ \Omega + 0.95\ \Omega = 1.90\ \Omega
\]

The line current is \(I = 740\ \text{A}\), and the input rms voltage is

\[
V_{\text{in}} = 42\ \text{kV} = 4.2\times 10^4\ \text{V}
\]

### (a) Voltage at the other end
The voltage drop across the transmission lines is

\[
\Delta V = IR_{\text{total}} = (740)(1.90) = 1406\ \text{V} \approx 1.4\ \text{kV}
\]

So the voltage at the far end is

\[
V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{in}} - \Delta V
= 42\ \text{kV} - 1.4\ \text{kV}
\approx 40.6\ \text{kV}
\approx 41\ \text{kV}
\]

### (b) Power input
Using rms values, the electrical power input is

\[
P_{\text{in}} = V_{\text{in}} I = (4.2\times 10^4)(740)
= 3.108\times 10^7\ \text{W}
\approx 31\ \text{MW}
\]

### (c) Power loss in the lines
The resistive power loss is

\[
P_{\text{loss}} = I^2 R_{\text{total}}
= (740)^2(1.90)
\]

\[
P_{\text{loss}} = 1.04044\times 10^6\ \text{W}
\approx 1.0\ \text{MW}
\]

### (d) Power output
The power delivered to the load is the input power minus the line loss:

\[
P_{\text{out}} = P_{\text{in}} - P_{\text{loss}}
\]

\[
P_{\text{out}} \approx 31\ \text{MW} - 1.0\ \text{MW}
= 30\ \text{MW}
\]

This matches the source answers.

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power in a circuit: \(P = VI\)
- Resistive voltage drop: \(\Delta V = IR\)
- Joule heating / line loss: \(P_{\text{loss}} = I^2R\)
- For transmission lines, the total resistance is the sum of both wire resistances
- Rms values are used directly for power calculations in AC circuits
