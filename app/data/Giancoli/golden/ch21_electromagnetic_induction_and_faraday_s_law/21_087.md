# Problem 21.87

## Question
Calculate the peak output voltage of a simple generator whose square armature windings are 6.60 cm on a side; the armature contains 125 loops and rotates in a field of 0.200 T at a rate of 120 rev⁄s.

## Final answer from source
82 V.

## Worked solution
For a rotating coil generator, the peak induced emf is

\[
\mathcal{E}_{\text{peak}} = N B A \omega
\]

where:
- \(N = 125\) is the number of loops,
- \(B = 0.200\ \text{T}\) is the magnetic field,
- \(A\) is the area of one square loop,
- \(\omega\) is the angular speed in rad/s.

### 1) Find the area of one loop
The side length is

\[
6.60\ \text{cm} = 0.0660\ \text{m}
\]

So the area is

\[
A = (0.0660)^2 = 4.356\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2
\]

### 2) Convert rotation rate to angular speed
The coil rotates at

\[
120\ \text{rev/s}
\]

Thus,

\[
\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi(120) = 240\pi \approx 754\ \text{rad/s}
\]

### 3) Compute the peak emf
Now substitute into the generator formula:

\[
\mathcal{E}_{\text{peak}} = (125)(0.200)(4.356\times 10^{-3})(754)
\]

First,

\[
(125)(0.200) = 25
\]

Then,

\[
25(4.356\times 10^{-3}) = 0.1089
\]

Finally,

\[
0.1089(754) \approx 82.1\ \text{V}
\]

Rounded to two significant figures,

\[
\boxed{82\ \text{V}}
\]

This agrees with the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Faraday’s law for a rotating coil generator
- Peak induced emf: \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{peak}} = N B A \omega\)
- Area of a square: \(A = s^2\)
- Conversion from revolutions per second to angular speed: \(\omega = 2\pi f\)
