# Problem 21.89

## Question
Determine the inductance $L $ of the primary of a transformer whose input is 220 V at 60.0 Hz if the current drawn is 6.3 A. Assume no current in the secondary. 623

## Final answer from source
93 mH.

## Worked solution
For a transformer with no current in the secondary, the primary behaves like a pure inductor drawing current from an AC source.

For an inductor on an AC circuit, the inductive reactance is

$$ 
X_L = \omega L = 2\pi f L
 $$

and the rms voltage and current satisfy

$$ 
V_{\text{rms}} = I_{\text{rms}} X_L = I_{\text{rms}} (2\pi f L).
 $$

Solve for $L $:

$$ 
L = \frac{V_{\text{rms}}}{I_{\text{rms}}\, 2\pi f}.
 $$

Substitute the given values:

$$ 
L = \frac{220\ \text{V}}{(6.3\ \text{A})(2\pi)(60.0\ \text{Hz})}.
 $$

Compute the denominator:

$$ 
(6.3)(2\pi)(60.0) \approx 2375.
 $$

Thus,

$$ 
L \approx \frac{220}{2375}\ \text{H} \approx 0.093\ \text{H}.
 $$

Convert to millihenries:

$$ 
0.093\ \text{H} = 93\ \text{mH}.
 $$

So the inductance of the primary is

$$ 
\boxed{93\ \text{mH}}.
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Inductive reactance: $X_L = 2\pi f L $
- AC Ohm’s law for a pure inductor: $V_{\text{rms}} = I_{\text{rms}} X_L $
- Solving for inductance from voltage, current, and frequency
- Unit conversion: henry to millihenry
