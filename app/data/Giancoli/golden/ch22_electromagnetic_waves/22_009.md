# Problem 22.9

## Question
(II) How long should it take the voices of astronauts on the Moon to reach the Earth? Explain in detail. 22-3 Electromagnetic Spectrum

## Final answer from source
1.25 s.

## Worked solution
Voice travels from the Moon to Earth as a sound signal that must first be converted to an electromagnetic radio signal for transmission through space; sound itself cannot travel through the vacuum between the Moon and Earth.

The transmission speed is therefore the speed of light:
\[
c \approx 3.00\times 10^8\ \text{m/s}
\]

Use the average Earth–Moon distance:
\[
d \approx 3.75\times 10^8\ \text{m}
\]

Then the travel time is
\[
t=\frac{d}{c}
\]
\[
t=\frac{3.75\times 10^8\ \text{m}}{3.00\times 10^8\ \text{m/s}}
=1.25\ \text{s}
\]

So the voices would take about **1.25 s** to reach Earth.

## Key concepts used
- Sound does not propagate through the vacuum of space.
- Communication from the Moon to Earth is carried by electromagnetic waves.
- Wave travel time is given by \(t=d/v\).
- Speed of light in vacuum: \(c \approx 3.00\times 10^8\ \text{m/s}\).
