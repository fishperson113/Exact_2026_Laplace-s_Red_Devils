# Problem 22.35

## Question
(I) Estimate the wavelength for a 1.9-GHz cell phone transmitter.

## Final answer from source
0.16 m.

## Worked solution
For an electromagnetic wave, the wavelength \(\lambda\) is related to speed \(c\) and frequency \(f\) by

\[
\lambda = \frac{c}{f}
\]

where:

- \(c = 3.00 \times 10^8\ \text{m/s}\) is the speed of light,
- \(f = 1.9\ \text{GHz} = 1.9 \times 10^9\ \text{Hz}\).

Substitute:

\[
\lambda = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8\ \text{m/s}}{1.9 \times 10^9\ \text{s}^{-1}}
\]

\[
\lambda \approx 1.58 \times 10^{-1}\ \text{m}
\]

\[
\lambda \approx 0.16\ \text{m}
\]

This matches the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light \(c\)
- Wave relation: \(\lambda = c/f\)
- Unit conversion: GHz to Hz
- Estimation and rounding to appropriate significant figures
