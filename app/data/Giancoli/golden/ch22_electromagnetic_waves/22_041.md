# Problem 22.41

## Question
(II) An amateur radio operator wishes to build a receiver that can tune a range from 14.0 MHz to 15.0 MHz. A variable capacitor has a minimum capacitance of 86 pF. (a) What is the required value of the inductance? (b) What is the maximum capacitance used on the variable capacitor?

## Final answer from source
(a) (b)

## Worked solution
The problem describes an LC tuning circuit whose resonant frequency is

$$ 
f=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}.
 $$

To tune over the range 14.0 MHz to 15.0 MHz, the circuit must satisfy:

- the **lowest frequency** occurs at the **largest capacitance**
- the **highest frequency** occurs at the **smallest capacitance**

Given:
- $f_{\min}=14.0\ \text{MHz} $
- $f_{\max}=15.0\ \text{MHz} $
- $C_{\min}=86\ \text{pF} $

### (a) Inductance
Use the highest frequency with the minimum capacitance:

$$ 
L=\frac{1}{(2\pi f_{\max})^2 C_{\min}}
 $$

Substitute:

$$ 
L=\frac{1}{(2\pi \cdot 15.0\times10^6)^2(86\times10^{-12})}
 $$

$$ 
L \approx 1.31\times 10^{-6}\ \text{H}
 $$

So,

$$ 
\boxed{L \approx 1.3\ \mu\text{H}}
 $$

### (b) Maximum capacitance
Now use the lowest frequency with the same inductance:

$$ 
C_{\max}=\frac{1}{(2\pi f_{\min})^2 L}
 $$

Substitute:

$$ 
C_{\max}=\frac{1}{(2\pi \cdot 14.0\times10^6)^2(1.31\times10^{-6})}
 $$

$$ 
C_{\max}\approx 96.6\times10^{-12}\ \text{F}
 $$

$$ 
\boxed{C_{\max}\approx 97\ \text{pF}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Resonance frequency of an LC circuit: $f=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} $
- Lower frequency corresponds to larger capacitance
- Higher frequency corresponds to smaller capacitance
- Unit conversion: MHz to Hz, pF to F
