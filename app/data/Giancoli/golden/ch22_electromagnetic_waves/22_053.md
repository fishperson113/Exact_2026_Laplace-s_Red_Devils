## Worked solution
The OCR/source text for the answer list is clearly incomplete and contaminated by many unrelated answers from other problems. Also, the problem statement is missing the numerical value of the average intensity, so the result cannot be derived uniquely from the prompt alone.

Because of that, a source-constrained solution can only be given in outline:

1. Let the average intensity of the TV signal be \(I\) at the antenna.
2. The antenna diameter is \(33\ \text{cm}\), so its radius is
   \[
   r = 0.165\ \text{m}.
   \]
3. The antenna area is
   \[
   A = \pi r^2 = \pi (0.165)^2\ \text{m}^2.
   \]
4. The viewing time is
   \[
   t = 4.0\ \text{h} = 1.44\times 10^4\ \text{s}.
   \]
5. The total energy received is
   \[
   E = IAt.
   \]
6. For a plane EM wave, the average intensity is related to the field amplitudes by
   \[
   I = \frac{1}{2}c\varepsilon_0 E_0^2 = \frac{1}{2}\frac{c}{\mu_0}B_0^2,
   \]
   with
   \[
   E_0 = cB_0.
   \]
7. Solving for the amplitudes gives
   \[
   E_0 = \sqrt{\frac{2I}{c\varepsilon_0}}, \qquad
   B_0 = \frac{E_0}{c}.
   \]

However, since the average intensity value is missing from the question text and the source answer excerpt is corrupted, I cannot reliably produce the numerical results for parts (a) and (b) from the provided material alone.

## Key concepts used
- Energy transported by electromagnetic waves: \(E = IAt\)
- Area of a circular antenna: \(A=\pi r^2\)
- EM wave intensity relations:
  - \(I = \tfrac12 c\varepsilon_0 E_0^2\)
  - \(I = \tfrac12 (c/\mu_0) B_0^2\)
- Field-amplitude relation for EM waves: \(E_0 = cB_0\)
