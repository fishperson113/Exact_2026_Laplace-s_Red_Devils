# Problem 22.55

## Question
A radio station is allowed to broadcast at an average power not to exceed 25 kW. If an electric field amplitude of is considered to be acceptable for receiving the radio transmission, estimate how many kilometers away you might be able to detect this station. 0.020 V(cid:2)m

## Final answer from source
61 km.

## Worked solution
This is an electromagnetic-wave intensity problem.

For a sinusoidal EM wave, the average intensity is related to the electric-field amplitude by

\[
I=\frac{1}{2}c\varepsilon_0 E_0^2
\]

where:
- \(c = 3.00\times 10^8\ \text{m/s}\)
- \(\varepsilon_0 = 8.85\times 10^{-12}\ \text{F/m}\)
- \(E_0 = 0.020\ \text{V/m}\) from the problem statement

First compute the intensity at the detection threshold:

\[
I=\frac{1}{2}(3.00\times10^8)(8.85\times10^{-12})(0.020)^2
\]

\[
(0.020)^2 = 4.0\times10^{-4}
\]

\[
I \approx \frac{1}{2}(3.00\times10^8)(8.85\times10^{-12})(4.0\times10^{-4})
\]

\[
I \approx \frac{1}{2}(1.062\times10^{-6})
\approx 5.3\times10^{-7}\ \text{W/m}^2
\]

Assume the station radiates uniformly in all directions, so the power spreads over a sphere:

\[
I=\frac{P}{4\pi r^2}
\]

Solve for distance \(r\):

\[
r=\sqrt{\frac{P}{4\pi I}}
\]

with \(P=25\ \text{kW}=2.5\times10^4\ \text{W}\):

\[
r=\sqrt{\frac{2.5\times10^4}{4\pi(5.3\times10^{-7})}}
\]

\[
r\approx \sqrt{3.8\times10^9}
\approx 6.1\times10^4\ \text{m}
\]

\[
r \approx 61\ \text{km}
\]

So the station could be detected at a distance of about

\[
\boxed{61\ \text{km}}
\]

which matches the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Average intensity of an electromagnetic wave: \(I=\tfrac{1}{2}c\varepsilon_0E_0^2\)
- Isotropic spherical spreading of power: \(I=P/(4\pi r^2)\)
- Unit conversion: kW to W, m to km
- Solving for distance from intensity and power
