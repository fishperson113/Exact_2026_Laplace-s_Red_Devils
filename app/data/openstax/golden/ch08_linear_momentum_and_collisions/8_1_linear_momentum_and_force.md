# 8.1 Linear Momentum and Force

Source: OpenStax College Physics 2e, Chapter 8, Section 8.1

## Concept explanation
Linear momentum is defined as the product of a system’s mass and its velocity. It is a vector, so it has the same direction as the velocity. In the SI system, momentum is measured in kilogram meters per second, written as kg · m/s.

Momentum is directly proportional to mass and to velocity. A larger mass or a larger speed gives a larger momentum.

Newton’s second law can be written in terms of momentum: the net external force equals the change in momentum divided by the time interval over which the change occurs. For constant mass, this reduces to the familiar form of Newton’s second law, F = ma.

## Key formulas
$$ 
\vec{p} = m \vec{v}
 $$

$$ 
p = mv
 $$

$$ 
\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \frac{\Delta \vec{p}}{\Delta t}
 $$

For constant mass:
$$ 
\Delta \vec{p} = m \Delta \vec{v}
 $$

$$ 
\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = m \vec{a}
 $$

## Variables and units
- $\vec{p} $: linear momentum, vector; SI unit kg · m/s
- $p $: magnitude of momentum, kg · m/s
- $m $: mass, kg
- $\vec{v} $: velocity, m/s
- $\vec{F}_{\text{net}} $: net external force, N
- $\Delta \vec{p} $: change in momentum, kg · m/s
- $\Delta t $: time interval, s
- $\Delta \vec{v} $: change in velocity, m/s
- $\vec{a} $: acceleration, m/s$^2 $

## Worked example
**Example 8.1: Calculating Momentum: A Football Player and a Football**

A 110-kg football player runs at 8.00 m/s, and a 0.410-kg football has a speed of 25.0 m/s.

(a) Player’s momentum:
$$ 
p = mv = (110\ \text{kg})(8.00\ \text{m/s}) = 8.80 \times 10^2\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
 $$

(b) Ball’s momentum:
$$ 
p = mv = (0.410\ \text{kg})(25.0\ \text{m/s}) = 10.3\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
 $$

Ratio of the player’s momentum to the ball’s momentum:
$$ 
\frac{8.80 \times 10^2}{10.3} \approx 85.4
 $$

So the player’s momentum is much greater than the football’s momentum.

**Example 8.2: Calculating Force: Venus Williams’ Racquet**

A 0.057-kg tennis ball is struck so that its speed just after impact is 58 m/s. The initial horizontal component of velocity is negligible, and the ball stays in contact with the racquet for 5.0 ms.

Change in momentum:
$$ 
\Delta p = m(v_f - v_i) = (0.057\ \text{kg})(58\ \text{m/s} - 0)
 $$

Average force:
$$ 
F_{\text{net}} = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t}
 $$

Using $\Delta t = 5.0\ \text{ms} = 5.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{s} $,
$$ 
F_{\text{net}} \approx 6.6 \times 10^2\ \text{N}
 $$

This is the average force exerted by the racquet on the tennis ball during contact.
