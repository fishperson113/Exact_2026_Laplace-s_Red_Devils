# Sample Problem 24.06: Potential energy of a system of three charged particles

## Problem
Figure 24-19 shows three charged particles held in fixed positions by forces that are not shown. What is the electric potential energy $U $ of this system of charges? Assume that $d = 12 \text{ cm} $ and that $q_1 = -q $, $q_2 = +4q $, and $q_3 = -2q $, in which $q = 150 \text{ nC} $.

## Key ideas
**KEY IDEA**  
The potential energy $U $ of the system is equal to the work we must do to assemble the system, bringing in each charge from an infinite distance.

## Solution
Let’s mentally build the system of Fig. 24-19, starting with one of the charges, say $q_1 $, in place and the others at infinity. Then we bring another one, say $q_2 $, in from infinity and put it in place. From Eq. 24-46 with $d $ substituted for $r $, the potential energy $U_{12} $ associated with the pair of charges $q_1 $ and $q_2 $ is

$$ 
U_{12} = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0}\frac{q_1 q_2}{d}.
 $$

We then bring the last charge $q_3 $ in from infinity and put it in place.

The total potential energy of a system of particles is the sum of the potential energies for every pair of particles in the system.

This result applies to a system for any given number of particles.

## Answer
$$ 
U = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0}\frac{q_1 q_2}{d}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Electric potential energy of a system of charges
- Work required to assemble charges from infinity
- Pairwise sum of potential energies
- Conservation of energy
