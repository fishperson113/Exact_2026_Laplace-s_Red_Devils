# Sample Problem 27.01: Single-loop circuit with two real batteries

## Problem
The emfs and resistances in the circuit of Fig. 27-8a have the following values:

- $\mathcal{E}_1 = 4.4\ \text{V} $
- $\mathcal{E}_2 = 2.1\ \text{V} $
- $r_1 = 2.3\ \Omega $
- $r_2 = 1.8\ \Omega $
- $R = 5.5\ \Omega $

(a) What is the current $i $ in the circuit?

## Key ideas
We can get an expression involving the current $i $ in this single-loop circuit by applying the loop rule, in which we sum the potential changes around the full loop.

## Solution
Although knowing the direction of $i $ is not necessary, we can easily determine it from the emfs of the batteries. The batteries oppose each other; that is, they tend to send current in opposite directions through the resistor.

Applying the loop rule around the circuit gives

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_1 - i r_1 - iR - i r_2 - \mathcal{E}_2 = 0.
 $$

Substituting the given values,

$$ 
4.4\ \text{V} - i(2.3\ \Omega) - i(5.5\ \Omega) - i(1.8\ \Omega) - 2.1\ \text{V} = 0.
 $$

So,

$$ 
4.4 - 2.1 = i(2.3 + 5.5 + 1.8),
 $$

$$ 
2.3 = i(9.6),
 $$

$$ 
i = \frac{2.3}{9.6}\ \text{A}.
 $$

This gives

$$ 
i \approx 0.24\ \text{A}.
 $$

## Answer
$$ 
i \approx 0.24\ \text{A}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Loop rule
- Potential changes around a closed loop
- Real batteries with internal resistance
