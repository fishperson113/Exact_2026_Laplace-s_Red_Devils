# Problem 16.57

## Question
A point charge (m = 1.0 gram) at the end of an insulating cord of length 55 cm is observed to be in equilibrium in a when the uniform horizontal electric field of pendulum’s position is as shown in Fig. 16-66, with the charge 12 cm above the lowest (vertical) position. If the field points to the right in Fig. 16-66, determine the magnitude and sign of the point charge. 9500 N(cid:2)C, l = 55 cm u B E Q m 12 cm FIGURE 16-66 Problem 57.

## Final answer from source
positive.

## Worked solution
Because the charge is displaced upward by 12 cm from its lowest position on a cord of length $L=55\text{ cm}=0.55\text{ m} $, the cord makes an angle $\theta $ with the vertical such that

$$ 
L - L\cos\theta = 0.12\text{ m}
 $$

so

$$ 
\cos\theta = 1 - \frac{0.12}{0.55} = 0.7818
 $$

and thus

$$ 
\theta \approx 38.6^\circ.
 $$

At equilibrium, the forces on the charge are:

- weight $mg $ downward,
- tension $T $ along the cord,
- electric force $F_E = |q|E $ horizontally.

Since the electric field points to the right and the bob is deflected to the right in the figure, the electric force must also point right. Therefore the charge must be **positive**.

Now apply force balance:

Vertical:
$$ 
T\cos\theta = mg
 $$

Horizontal:
$$ 
T\sin\theta = |q|E
 $$

Divide the horizontal equation by the vertical one:

$$ 
\tan\theta = \frac{|q|E}{mg}
 $$

so

$$ 
|q| = \frac{mg\tan\theta}{E}.
 $$

Substitute the given values:

$$ 
m = 1.0\text{ g} = 1.0\times 10^{-3}\text{ kg}, \quad g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2,\quad E = 9500\text{ N/C}
 $$

and

$$ 
\tan 38.6^\circ \approx 0.798.
 $$

Thus

$$ 
|q| = \frac{(1.0\times 10^{-3})(9.8)(0.798)}{9500}
\approx 8.2\times 10^{-7}\text{ C}.
 $$

So the charge is

$$ 
\boxed{q \approx +8.2\times 10^{-7}\ \text{C}}
 $$

and its sign is positive, consistent with the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Equilibrium of forces on a pendulum in a uniform electric field
- Resolving tension into horizontal and vertical components
- Geometry of a pendulum displaced from the vertical
- Electric force $F=qE $ and sign determination from force direction
