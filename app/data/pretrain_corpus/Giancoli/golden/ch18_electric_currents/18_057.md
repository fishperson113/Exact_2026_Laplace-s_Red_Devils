# Problem 18.57

## Question
(III) At a point high in the Earth’s atmosphere, 2.4 × 10^12 m^-3 He2+ ions are moving due north at a speed of 2.0 × 10^6 m/s. A concentration of 7.0 × 10^11 m^-3 ions is moving due south at a speed of 6.2 × 10^6 m/s. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net current passing through unit area (A/m^2).

## Final answer from source
north.

## Worked solution
Because current density is current per unit area, for a stream of ions moving through unit area we use

$$ 
J = nqv
 $$

where:
- $n $ = number concentration (m$^{-3} $),
- $q $ = charge of each ion,
- $v $ = speed,
- direction of current is the direction of motion of positive charge.

Here the ions are $ \text{He}^{2+}  $, so each has charge

$$ 
q = +2e = 2(1.60\times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}) = 3.20\times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}
 $$

Take north as positive.

### Current density from the northward ions
$$ 
J_1 = n_1 q v_1
 $$
$$ 
J_1 = (2.4\times 10^{12})(3.20\times 10^{-19})(2.0\times 10^6)
 $$

$$ 
J_1 = 1.536 \,\text{A/m}^2
 $$

Direction: north.

### Current density from the southward ions
These ions also carry positive charge, and since they move due south, their current is due south.

$$ 
J_2 = n_2 q v_2
 $$
$$ 
J_2 = (7.0\times 10^{11})(3.20\times 10^{-19})(6.2\times 10^6)
 $$

$$ 
J_2 = 1.3888 \,\text{A/m}^2
 $$

Direction: south.

### Net current density
Since the two contributions are opposite in direction, subtract magnitudes:

$$ 
J_{\text{net}} = 1.536 - 1.3888 = 0.1472 \,\text{A/m}^2
 $$

The result is positive, so the net current is **northward**.

$$ 
\boxed{J_{\text{net}} \approx 0.15\ \text{A/m}^2 \text{ north}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Current density: $J = nqv $
- Positive charge moving north produces northward current
- Positive charge moving south produces southward current
- Net current density is the vector sum of contributions
