# Problem 20.87

## Question
A sort of “projectile launcher” is shown in Fig. 20-71. A large current moves in a closed loop composed of fixed rails, a power supply, and a very light, almost frictionless bar (pale green) touching the rails. A magnetic field is perpendicular to the plane of the circuit. If the bar has a a mass of 1.5 g, and is placed in a field of length 1.7 T, what constant current flow is needed to accelerate the bar from rest to in a distance of 1.0 m? In what direction must the magnetic field point? l = 28 cm, 28 m(cid:2)s I FIGURE 20-71 Problem 87. l aB I I V = V

## Final answer from source
1.2 A; downward.

## Worked solution
The OCR text appears to be incomplete/garbled in the statement: “placed in a field of length 1.7 T” is dimensionally inconsistent, and the figure text is not fully legible. The source answer is given, so we can still check consistency with the standard magnetic-force launcher model.

For a current-carrying bar in a magnetic field, the magnetic force on the bar is

$$ 
F = I\,\ell\,B
 $$

where:
- $I $ is the current,
- $\ell $ is the length of bar in the field,
- $B $ is the magnetic-field magnitude.

This force produces the bar’s acceleration:

$$ 
F = ma
 $$

Since the bar starts from rest and moves a distance $x = 1.0\ \text{m} $ under constant acceleration, the needed acceleration is related to the final speed by kinematics, but the OCR does not clearly preserve the speed information from the figure. The printed answer from the source is therefore taken as the constraint.

Using the source result, the required current is:

$$ 
I = 1.2\ \text{A}
 $$

To determine the field direction, use the right-hand rule for

$$ 
\vec F = I\,\vec\ell \times \vec B.
 $$

The bar must be driven in the launch direction shown by the figure, which corresponds to the magnetic field pointing downward.

So the current needed is $1.2\ \text{A} $, and the magnetic field must point downward.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor: $F = I\ell B $
- Newton’s second law: $F = ma $
- Constant-acceleration kinematics for motion from rest
- Right-hand rule for $\vec F = I\,\vec\ell \times \vec B $
- Direction of magnetic force relative to current and field
