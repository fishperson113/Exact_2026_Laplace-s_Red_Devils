# Problem 21.47

## Question
(I) The magnetic field inside an air-filled solenoid 36 cm long and 2.0 cm in diameter is 0.72 T. Approximately how much energy is stored in this field?

## Final answer from source
23 J.

## Worked solution
The magnetic energy density in a magnetic field is

$$ 
u_B=\frac{B^2}{2\mu_0}
 $$

where:
- $B = 0.72\ \text{T} $
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\ \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A} $

The volume of the solenoid is approximately its cylindrical interior volume:

$$ 
V = \pi r^2 L
 $$

Given:
- length $L = 36\ \text{cm} = 0.36\ \text{m} $
- diameter $d = 2.0\ \text{cm} $, so radius $r = 1.0\ \text{cm} = 0.010\ \text{m} $

Thus,

$$ 
V = \pi (0.010)^2(0.36)
 $$

$$ 
V = \pi (1.0\times 10^{-4})(0.36)
 $$

$$ 
V \approx 1.13\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^3
 $$

Now compute the energy density:

$$ 
u_B = \frac{(0.72)^2}{2(4\pi\times 10^{-7})}
 $$

$$ 
u_B = \frac{0.5184}{8\pi\times 10^{-7}}
\approx 2.06\times 10^5\ \text{J/m}^3
 $$

Total energy stored:

$$ 
U = u_B V
 $$

$$ 
U \approx (2.06\times 10^5)(1.13\times 10^{-4})
 $$

$$ 
U \approx 23\ \text{J}
 $$

So the energy stored in the magnetic field is approximately

$$ 
\boxed{23\ \text{J}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic energy density: $u_B = \dfrac{B^2}{2\mu_0} $
- Volume of a cylinder: $V = \pi r^2 L $
- Total magnetic energy: $U = u_B V $
- Unit conversion from cm to m
