# Problem 21.53

## Question
(III) After how many time constants does the current in Fig. 21-37 reach within (a) 10%, (b) 1.0%, and (c) 0.1% of its maximum value? *21-13 AC Circuits and Reactance

## Final answer from source
(a) 2.3; (b) 4.6; (c) 6.9.

## Worked solution
For a current that rises toward its maximum value with an exponential time dependence, the standard form is

$$ 
I(t)=I_{\max}\left(1-e^{-t/\tau}\right),
 $$

where $\tau $ is the time constant.

“Within $x\% $ of its maximum value” means the current is missing only $x\% $ of $I_{\max} $. So set

$$ 
I(t)=\left(1-\frac{x}{100}\right)I_{\max}.
 $$

Then

$$ 
1-e^{-t/\tau}=1-\frac{x}{100}
 $$

which gives

$$ 
e^{-t/\tau}=\frac{x}{100}.
 $$

Taking the natural logarithm:

$$ 
-\frac{t}{\tau}=\ln\!\left(\frac{x}{100}\right),
 $$

so

$$ 
\frac{t}{\tau}=-\ln\!\left(\frac{x}{100}\right)=\ln\!\left(\frac{100}{x}\right).
 $$

Now evaluate each case:

### (a) Within 10%
$$ 
\frac{t}{\tau}=\ln\!\left(\frac{100}{10}\right)=\ln 10 \approx 2.30
 $$

### (b) Within 1.0%
$$ 
\frac{t}{\tau}=\ln\!\left(\frac{100}{1.0}\right)=\ln 100 \approx 4.61
 $$

### (c) Within 0.1%
$$ 
\frac{t}{\tau}=\ln\!\left(\frac{100}{0.1}\right)=\ln 1000 \approx 6.91
 $$

Thus the current reaches within the stated percentages of its maximum value after:

$$ 
\boxed{(a)\ 2.3\tau,\quad (b)\ 4.6\tau,\quad (c)\ 6.9\tau}
 $$

which matches the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Exponential growth of current in an RL/charging-type circuit
- Time constant $\tau $
- Interpreting “within $x\% $ of maximum” as a remaining fraction $x/100 $
- Solving exponential equations with natural logarithms
