# Example 27.1: Magnetic force on a proton

## Problem
A beam of protons moves at $3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s} $ through a uniform $2.0\ \text{T} $ magnetic field directed along the positive $z $-axis, as in Fig. 27.10. The velocity of each proton lies in the $xz $-plane and is directed at $30^\circ $ to the $+z $-axis. Find the force on a proton.

## Setup
IDENTIFY and SET UP: This problem uses the expression for the magnetic force on a moving charged particle. The target variable is $\mathbf{F} $.

Given:
- $q = +1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C} $
- $v = 3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s} $
- $B = 2.0\ \text{T} $
- angle between $\mathbf{v} $ and $\mathbf{B} $: $30^\circ $

Use the magnetic force law:
$$ 
\mathbf{F} = q\,\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}
 $$
and
$$ 
F = qvB\sin\phi
 $$
where $\phi $ is the angle between $\mathbf{v} $ and $\mathbf{B} $.

## Solution
EXECUTE: The charge is positive, so the force is in the same direction as the vector product $\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B} $. From the right-hand rule, this direction is along the negative $y $-axis.

The magnitude of the force is
$$ 
F = qvB\sin\phi
 $$
with $\phi = 30^\circ $, so
$$ 
F = (1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C})(3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s})(2.0\ \text{T})\sin 30^\circ
 $$
$$ 
F = 4.8 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{N}
 $$

Therefore,
$$ 
\mathbf{F} = -4.8 \times 10^{-14}\,\hat{\mathbf{y}}\ \text{N}
 $$

## Evaluation
We check our result by evaluating the force using vector language and Eq. (27.2). We have
$$ 
\mathbf{B} = (2.0\ \text{T})\,\hat{\mathbf{z}}
 $$
$$ 
\mathbf{v} = (3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s})\sin 30^\circ\,\hat{\mathbf{y}} + (3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s})\cos 30^\circ\,\hat{\mathbf{z}}
 $$
Then
$$ 
\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}
= (3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s})\sin 30^\circ\,\hat{\mathbf{y}} \times (2.0\ \text{T})\hat{\mathbf{z}}
 $$
$$ 
= (3.0 \times 10^5\ \text{m/s})(2.0\ \text{T})\sin 30^\circ\,\hat{\mathbf{x}}
 $$
Using the given cross-product directions from the text, the force is again in the negative $y $-direction with magnitude
$$ 
F = 4.8 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{N}
 $$

If the beam consists of electrons rather than protons, the charge is negative $(q=-1.6\times10^{-19}\ \text{C}) $ and the direction of the force is reversed. The force is now directed along the positive $y $-axis, but the magnitude is the same as before:
$$ 
F = 4.8 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{N}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a moving charge:
  $$ 
  \mathbf{F} = q\,\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}
   $$
- Magnitude of magnetic force:
  $$ 
  F = qvB\sin\phi
   $$
- Direction from the right-hand rule
- Sign of charge reverses the force direction
