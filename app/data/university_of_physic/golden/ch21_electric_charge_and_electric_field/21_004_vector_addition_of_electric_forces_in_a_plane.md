# Example 21.4: Vector addition of electric forces in a plane

## Problem
Two equal positive charges are located at and respectively. What are the magnitude and direction of the total electric force that and exert on a third charge at ?

## Setup
**IDENTIFY and SET UP:** As in Example 21.3, we must compute the force that each charge exerts on and then find the vector sum of those forces. Figure 21.14 shows the situation. Since the three charges do not all lie on a line, the best way to calculate the forces is to use components.

Given from the worked example:
- \(Q = 4.0\,\mu\text{C}\)
- \(q_1 = q_2 = 2.0\,\mu\text{C}\)
- \(x = 0.40\,\text{m},\ y = 0\)
- \(x = 0,\ y = -0.30\,\text{m}\)
- \(x = 0,\ y = 0.30\,\text{m}\)

## Solution
**EXECUTE:** Figure 21.14 shows the forces on due to the identical charges and , which are at equal distances from . From Coulomb’s law, both forces have magnitude

\[
F_{1 \text{ or } 2 \text{ on } Q}
=
\frac{(9.0 \times 10^9\ \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2)(4.0 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{C})(2.0 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{C})}{(0.50\ \text{m})^2}
= 0.29\ \text{N}
\]

The x-components of the two forces are equal:

\[
F_{1 \text{ or } 2 \text{ on } Q,x}
=
F_{1 \text{ or } 2 \text{ on } Q}\cos a
=
(0.29\ \text{N})\frac{0.40\ \text{m}}{0.50\ \text{m}}
=
0.23\ \text{N}
\]

From symmetry we see that the y-components of the two forces are equal and opposite. Hence their sum is zero and the total force on has only an x-component \(F_x\).

\[
F_x = 0.23\ \text{N} + 0.23\ \text{N} = 0.46\ \text{N}
\]

The total force on is in the \(+x\)-direction, with magnitude \(0.46\ \text{N}\).

## Evaluation
**EVALUATE:** The total force on points neither directly away from nor directly away from . Rather, this direction is a compromise that points away from the system of charges and . Can you see that the total force would not be in the \(+x\)-direction if and were not equal or if the geometrical arrangement of the charges were not so symmetric?

## Key concepts used
- Coulomb’s law for the magnitude of the force between point charges
- Vector addition of forces using components
- Symmetry to show that the y-components cancel
- The total force is the vector sum of the individual forces
