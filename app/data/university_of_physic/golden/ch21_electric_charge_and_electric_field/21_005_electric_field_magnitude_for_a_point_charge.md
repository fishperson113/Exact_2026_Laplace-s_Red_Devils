# Example 21.5: Electric-field magnitude for a point charge

## Problem
What is the magnitude of the electric field at a field point 2.0 m from a point charge $q = 4.0\ \text{nC} $?

## Setup
This problem concerns the electric field due to a point charge. We are given the magnitude of the charge and the distance from the charge to the field point, so we use Eq. (21.6) to calculate the field magnitude.

## Solution
From Eq. (21.6),
$$ 
E = \frac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_0}\frac{|q|}{r^2}
= \left(9.0 \times 10^9\ \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2\right)
\frac{4.0 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{C}}{(2.0\ \text{m})^2}
 $$
$$ 
E = 9.0\ \text{N/C}
 $$

## Evaluation
Our result means that if we placed a $1.0 $-C charge at a point $2.0\ \text{m} $ from $q $, it would experience a $9.0 $-N force. The force on a $2.0 $-C charge at that point would be $2.0\ \text{C} \times 9.0\ \text{N/C} = 18\ \text{N} $, and so on.

## Key concepts used
- Electric field due to a point charge
- Magnitude relation $E = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\dfrac{|q|}{r^2} $
- Electric field units: $\text{N/C} $
