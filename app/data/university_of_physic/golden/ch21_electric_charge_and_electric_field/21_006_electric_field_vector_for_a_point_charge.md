# Example 21.6: Electric-field vector for a point charge

## Problem
A point charge $q $ is located at the origin. Find the electric-field vector at the field point $S $.

## Setup
**IDENTIFY and SET UP:** We must find the electric-field vector due to a point charge. Figure 21.19 shows the situation. We use Eq. (21.7); to do this, we must find the distance from the source point (the position of the charge $q $, which in this example is at the origin) to the field point $S $, and we must obtain an expression for the unit vector $\hat{\mathbf{r}} $ that points from $P $ to $S $.

## Solution
**EXECUTE:** The distance from $P $ to $S $ is

$$ 
r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}=\sqrt{(1.2\ \text{m})^2+(-1.6\ \text{m})^2}=2.0\ \text{m}
 $$

The unit vector is then

$$ 
\hat{\mathbf{r}}=\frac{\mathbf{r}}{r}
=\frac{(1.2\ \text{m})\hat{\mathbf{i}}+(-1.6\ \text{m})\hat{\mathbf{j}}}{2.0\ \text{m}}
=0.60\,\hat{\mathbf{i}}-0.80\,\hat{\mathbf{j}}
 $$

Then, from Eq. (21.7),

$$ 
\mathbf{E}
=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}\hat{\mathbf{r}}
 $$

Substituting the given values,

$$ 
\mathbf{E}
=(9.0\times10^9\ \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2)
\frac{-8.0\times10^{-9}\ \text{C}}{(2.0\ \text{m})^2}
\left(0.60\,\hat{\mathbf{i}}-0.80\,\hat{\mathbf{j}}\right)
 $$

$$ 
\mathbf{E}=(-18\ \text{N/C})\left(0.60\,\hat{\mathbf{i}}-0.80\,\hat{\mathbf{j}}\right)
 $$

$$ 
\mathbf{E}=-11\ \text{N/C}\,\hat{\mathbf{i}}+14\ \text{N/C}\,\hat{\mathbf{j}}
 $$

## Evaluation
Since $q $ is negative, $\mathbf{E} $ points from the field point to the charge (the source point), in the direction opposite to $\hat{\mathbf{r}} $ (compare Fig. 21.17c).

## Key concepts used
- Electric field of a point charge:
  $$ 
  \mathbf{E}=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}\hat{\mathbf{r}}
   $$
- Distance from coordinates:
  $$ 
  r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}
   $$
- Unit vector from source to field point:
  $$ 
  \hat{\mathbf{r}}=\mathbf{r}/r
   $$
- A negative charge produces an electric field directed toward the charge.
