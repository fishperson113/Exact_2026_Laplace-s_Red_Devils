# Example 22.4: Electric flux and enclosed charge

## Problem
Figure 22.15 shows the field produced by two point charges, \(+q\) and \(-q\) (an electric dipole). Find the electric flux through each of the closed surfaces A, B, C, and D.

## Setup
Gauss’s law, Eq. (22.8), says that the total electric flux through a closed surface is equal to the total enclosed charge divided by \(\varepsilon_0\).

The flux depends only on the charge enclosed within each Gaussian surface, not on the precise shape of the surface.

From the figure:
- Surface A encloses the positive charge, so \(Q_{\text{encl}} = +q\).
- Surface B encloses the negative charge, so \(Q_{\text{encl}} = -q\).
- Surface C encloses both charges, so \(Q_{\text{encl}} = +q + (-q) = 0\).
- Surface D encloses no charge, so \(Q_{\text{encl}} = 0\).

## Solution
Using Gauss’s law,
\[
\Phi_E = \frac{Q_{\text{encl}}}{\varepsilon_0}.
\]

Therefore,
\[
\Phi_{EA} = \frac{+q}{\varepsilon_0},
\]
\[
\Phi_{EB} = \frac{-q}{\varepsilon_0},
\]
\[
\Phi_{EC} = 0,
\]
\[
\Phi_{ED} = 0.
\]

We can also see these results by examining the electric field lines. All the field lines that cross surface A are directed out of the surface, so the flux through A is positive. All the field lines that cross surface B point inward, so the flux through B is negative. For both surface C and surface D, there are as many field lines pointing into the surface as there are pointing outward, so the flux through each of these surfaces is zero.

## Evaluation
These results are consistent with Gauss’s law: the net flux through a closed surface depends only on the net enclosed charge.

The answer does not depend on the precise shapes of the surfaces, only on the charge enclosed by each one.

## Key concepts used
- Gauss’s law: \(\Phi_E = Q_{\text{encl}}/\varepsilon_0\)
- Electric flux through a closed surface depends only on enclosed charge
- Positive enclosed charge gives positive flux; negative enclosed charge gives negative flux
- Zero enclosed charge gives zero net flux
