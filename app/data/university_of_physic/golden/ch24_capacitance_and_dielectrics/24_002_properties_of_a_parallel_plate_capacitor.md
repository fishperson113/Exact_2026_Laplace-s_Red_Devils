# Example 24.2: Properties of a parallel-plate capacitor

## Problem
The plates of a parallel-plate capacitor in vacuum are apart and in area. A 10.0-kV potential difference is applied across the capacitor. Compute (a) the capacitance; (b) the charge on each plate; and (c) the magnitude of the electric field between the plates.

## Setup
**IDENTIFY and SET UP:** We are given the plate area and the plate spacing, and the potential difference \(V_{ab}\) for this parallel-plate capacitor. Our target variables are the capacitance \(C\), the charge \(Q\) on each plate, and the electric-field magnitude \(E\).

We use Eq. (24.2) to calculate \(C\), and then use Eq. (24.1) and \(Q = CV_{ab}\) to find \(Q\). We use \(E = Q/(\epsilon_0 A)\) to find \(E\).

## Solution
**EXECUTE:**  
(a) From Eq. (24.2),
\[
C = \frac{\epsilon_0 A}{d}
= \frac{(8.85 \times 10^{-12}\ \text{F/m})(12.00\ \text{m}^2)}{5.00 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}}
= 3.54 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{F}
= 0.00354\ \text{mF}
\]

(b) The charge on the capacitor is
\[
Q = CV_{ab} = (3.54 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{C/V})(1.00 \times 10^{4}\ \text{V})
= 3.54 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{C}
= 35.4\ \text{mC}
\]

The plate at higher potential has charge \(+35.4\ \text{mC}\), and the other plate has charge \(-35.4\ \text{mC}\).

(c) The electric-field magnitude is
\[
E = \frac{Q}{\epsilon_0 A}
= \frac{3.54 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{C}}{(8.85 \times 10^{-12}\ \text{C}^2/\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)(12.00\ \text{m}^2)}
= 3.33 \times 10^{5}\ \text{N/C}
\]
However, the field between the plates is uniform, so it may also be found from the potential gradient.

## Evaluation
**EVALUATE:** We can also find \(E\) by recalling that the electric field is equal in magnitude to the potential gradient [Eq. (23.22)]. The field between the plates is uniform, so
\[
E = \frac{V_{ab}}{d}
= \frac{1.00 \times 10^{4}\ \text{V}}{5.00 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}}
= 2.00 \times 10^{6}\ \text{V/m}
= 2.00 \times 10^{6}\ \text{N/C}
\]
(Remember that \(1\ \text{N/C} = 1\ \text{V/m}\).)

## Key concepts used
- Capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor: \(\displaystyle C = \epsilon_0 A/d\)
- Charge on a capacitor: \(\displaystyle Q = CV\)
- Electric field between parallel plates: \(\displaystyle E = V/d\)
- Relation between field and charge: \(\displaystyle E = Q/(\epsilon_0 A)\)
