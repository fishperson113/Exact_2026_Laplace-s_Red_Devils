# Example 27.3: Electron motion in a magnetron

## Problem
A magnetron in a microwave oven emits electromagnetic waves with frequency 2450 MHz. What magnetic field strength is required for electrons to move in circular paths with this frequency?

## Setup
The problem refers to circular motion as shown in Fig. 27.17a. We use Eq. (27.12) to solve for the field magnitude \(B\).

## Solution
The angular speed that corresponds to the frequency is
\[
\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi (2450 \times 10^{6}\ \text{s}^{-1}) = 1.54 \times 10^{10}\ \text{s}^{-1}.
\]

Then from Eq. (27.12),
\[
B = \frac{mv}{q} = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\ \text{kg})(1.54 \times 10^{10}\ \text{s}^{-1})}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}} = 0.0877\ \text{T}.
\]

## Evaluation
This is a moderate field strength, easily produced with a permanent magnet. Incidentally, 2450-MHz electromagnetic waves are useful for heating and cooking food because they are strongly absorbed by water molecules.

## Key concepts used
- Circular motion of a charged particle in a magnetic field
- Relation between cyclotron angular speed and frequency: \(\omega = 2\pi f\)
- Magnetic field magnitude for circular motion, Eq. (27.12)
