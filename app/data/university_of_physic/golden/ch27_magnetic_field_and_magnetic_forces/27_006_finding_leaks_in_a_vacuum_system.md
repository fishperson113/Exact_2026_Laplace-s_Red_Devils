# Example 27.6: Finding leaks in a vacuum system

## Problem
There is almost no helium in ordinary air, so helium sprayed near a leak in a vacuum system will quickly show up in the output of a vacuum pump connected to such a system. You are designing a leak detector that uses a mass spectrometer to detect helium ions, \(\mathrm{He^+}\), with charge \(+e = +1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}\) and mass \(6.65 \times 10^{-27}\ \mathrm{kg}\).

The ions emerge from the velocity selector with a speed of \(1.00 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{m/s}\). They are curved in a semicircular path by a magnetic field and are detected at a distance of \(10.16\ \mathrm{cm}\) from the slit in Fig. 27.24.

Calculate the magnitude of the magnetic field.

## Setup
IDENTIFY and SET UP: After it passes through the slit, the ion follows a circular path as described in Section 27.4 (see Fig. 27.17). We solve Eq. (27.11) for \(B\):

\[
R = \frac{mv}{qB}
\]

## Solution
EXECUTE: The distance given is the diameter of the semicircular path shown in Fig. 27.24, so the radius is

\[
R = \frac{1}{2}(10.16 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m})
\]

From Eq. (27.11), we get

\[
B = \frac{mv}{qR}
= \frac{(6.65 \times 10^{-27}\ \mathrm{kg})(1.00 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{m/s})}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C})(5.08 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{m})}
\]

\[
B = 0.0818\ \mathrm{T}
\]

## Evaluation
EVALUATE: Helium leak detectors are widely used with high-vacuum systems. Our result shows that only a small magnetic field is required, so leak detectors can be relatively compact.

## Key concepts used
- Charged particle motion in a uniform magnetic field
- Circular motion of an ion in a magnetic field
- Radius relation \(R = mv/(qB)\)
- Semicircular path geometry: diameter \(= 2R\)
