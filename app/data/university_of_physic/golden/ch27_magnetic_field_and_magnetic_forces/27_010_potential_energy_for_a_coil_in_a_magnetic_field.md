# Example 27.10: Potential energy for a coil in a magnetic field

## Problem
If the coil in Example 27.9 rotates from its initial orientation to one in which its magnetic moment is parallel to **B**, what is the change in potential energy?

## Setup
IDENTIFY and SET UP: Equation (27.27) gives the potential energy for each orientation. The initial position is as shown in Fig. 27.35, with  
$$ 
\phi_1 = 90^\circ.
 $$
In the final orientation, the coil has been rotated clockwise so that **m** and **B** are parallel, so the angle between these vectors is  
$$ 
\phi_2 = 0.
 $$

## Solution
EXECUTE: From Eq. (27.27), the potential energy change is
$$ 
\Delta U = U_2 - U_1 = -mB\cos\phi_2 - \left(-mB\cos\phi_1\right)
 $$
$$ 
= -mB(\cos 0^\circ - \cos 90^\circ)
 $$
$$ 
= - (11.18\ \text{A}\cdot\text{m}^2)(1.20\ \text{T})(1 - 0)
 $$
$$ 
= -1.41\ \text{J}.
 $$

## Evaluation
The potential energy decreases because the rotation is in the direction of the magnetic torque that we found in Example 27.9.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic dipole potential energy: $\,U = -\mathbf{m}\cdot\mathbf{B} = -mB\cos\phi\, $
- Change in potential energy between two orientations:
  $$ 
  \Delta U = -mB(\cos\phi_2 - \cos\phi_1)
   $$
- Alignment of $\mathbf{m} $ with $\mathbf{B} $ lowers potential energy
