# Example 28.5: Forces between parallel wires

## Problem
Two straight, parallel, superconducting wires 4.5 mm apart carry equal currents of 15,000 A in opposite directions. What force, per unit length, does each wire exert on the other?

## Setup
**IDENTIFY and SET UP:** Figure 28.10 shows the situation. We find $F/L $, the magnetic force per unit length of wire, using Eq. (28.11).

## Solution
**EXECUTE:** The conductors repel each other because the currents are in opposite directions. From Eq. (28.11), the force per unit length is
$$ 
\frac{F}{L}=\frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}
 $$
With $I_1 = I_2 = 15{,}000\ \text{A} $ and $r = 4.5\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m} $,
$$ 
\frac{F}{L}=\frac{(4\pi\times 10^{-7}\ \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A})(15{,}000\ \text{A})(15{,}000\ \text{A})}{2\pi(4.5\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m})}
 $$

## Evaluation
$$ 
\frac{F}{L}=1.0\times 10^{4}\ \text{N/m}
 $$

This is a large force, more than one ton per meter. Currents and separations of this magnitude are used in superconducting electromagnets in particle accelerators, and mechanical stress analysis is a crucial part of the design process.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force per unit length between two long, parallel currents:
  $$ 
  \frac{F}{L}=\frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}
   $$
- Currents in opposite directions repel.
- Unit consistency: $\text{T}\cdot\text{m/A} $, A, and m give $\text{N/m} $.
