# Example 28.11: Magnetic dipoles in a paramagnetic material

## Problem
Nitric oxide is a paramagnetic compound. The magnetic moment of each NO molecule has a maximum component in any direction of about one Bohr magneton. Compare the interaction energy of such magnetic moments in a 1.5-T magnetic field with the average translational kinetic energy of molecules at 300 K.

## Setup
This problem involves the energy of a magnetic moment in a magnetic field and the average thermal kinetic energy.

Use the interaction energy of a magnetic moment with a magnetic field:
$$ 
U = -\mathbf{M}\cdot \mathbf{B}
 $$
so that
$$ 
U = -M_{\parallel} B
 $$
where $M_{\parallel} $ is the component of the magnetic moment in the direction of the field.

Use the average translational kinetic energy of a molecule at temperature $T $:
$$ 
K = \frac{3}{2}kT
 $$

Here the maximum value of the magnetic moment component is about one Bohr magneton:
$$ 
M_{\parallel,\max} \approx \mu_B = 9.27\times 10^{-24}\ \text{J/T}
 $$

## Solution
The maximum interaction energy magnitude is
$$ 
|U|_{\max} \approx \mu_B B
 $$
$$ 
|U|_{\max} = (9.27\times 10^{-24}\ \text{J/T})(1.5\ \text{T})
 $$
$$ 
|U|_{\max} = 1.4\times 10^{-23}\ \text{J} = 8.7\times 10^{-5}\ \text{eV}
 $$

The average translational kinetic energy is
$$ 
K = \frac{3}{2}kT
 $$
$$ 
K = \frac{3}{2}(1.38\times 10^{-23}\ \text{J/K})(300\ \text{K})
 $$
$$ 
K = 6.2\times 10^{-21}\ \text{J} = 0.039\ \text{eV}
 $$

## Evaluation
At $300\ \text{K} $ the magnetic interaction energy is only about $0.2\% $ of the thermal kinetic energy, so we expect only a slight degree of alignment. This is why paramagnetic susceptibilities at ordinary temperature are usually very small.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic dipole interaction energy in a field: $U = -\mathbf{M}\cdot\mathbf{B} $
- Maximum energy magnitude: $|U|_{\max} = \mu_B B $
- Average translational kinetic energy of a molecule: $K = \frac{3}{2}kT $
- Comparison of magnetic and thermal energies in paramagnetism
