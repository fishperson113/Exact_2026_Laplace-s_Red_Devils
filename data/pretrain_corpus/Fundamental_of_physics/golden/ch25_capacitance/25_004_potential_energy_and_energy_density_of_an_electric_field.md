# Sample Problem 25.04: Potential energy and energy density of an electric field

## Problem
An isolated conducting sphere whose radius $R $ is $6.85\ \text{cm} $ has a charge $q = 1.25\ \text{nC} $.

(a) How much potential energy is stored in the electric field of this charged conductor?

## Key ideas
1. An isolated sphere has capacitance given by Eq. 25-18:
   $$ 
   C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0 R.
    $$
2. The energy $U $ stored in a capacitor depends on the capacitor’s charge $q $ and capacitance $C $ according to Eq. 25-21:
   $$ 
   U = \frac{q^2}{2C}.
    $$

## Solution
Substituting $C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0 R $ into Eq. 25-21 gives
$$ 
U = \frac{q^2}{2C} = \frac{q^2}{8\pi\varepsilon_0 R}.
 $$

Now substitute the given values:
$$ 
U = \frac{(1.25\times10^{-9}\ \text{C})^2}{(8\pi)(8.85\times10^{-12}\ \text{F/m})(0.0685\ \text{m})}.
 $$

This yields
$$ 
U = 1.03\times10^{-7}\ \text{J}.
 $$

## Answer
$$ 
U = 1.03\times10^{-7}\ \text{J} = 103\ \text{nJ}.
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Capacitance of an isolated conducting sphere:
  $$ 
  C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0 R
   $$
- Energy stored in a capacitor:
  $$ 
  U = \frac{q^2}{2C}
   $$
- Substitution of the sphere’s capacitance into the energy formula.
