# Sample Problem 26.06: Rate of energy dissipation in a wire carrying current

## Problem
You are given a length of uniform heating wire made of a nickel-chromium-iron alloy called Nichrome; it has a resistance $R $ of $72\ \Omega $. At what rate is energy dissipated in each of the following situations?

1. A potential difference of $120\ \text{V} $ is applied across the full length of the wire.
2. The wire is cut in half, and a potential difference of $120\ \text{V} $ is applied across the length of each half.

## Key ideas
Current in a resistive material produces a transfer of mechanical energy to thermal energy; the rate of transfer (dissipation) is given by Eqs. 26-26 to 26-28.

## Solution
Because we know the potential $V $ and resistance $R $, we use Eq. 26-28, which yields, for situation 1,

$$ 
P=\frac{V^2}{R}=\frac{(120\ \text{V})^2}{72\ \Omega}=200\ \text{W}.
 $$

For situation 2, when the wire is cut in half, each half has half the original resistance. Thus, for each half,

$$ 
R'=\frac{72\ \Omega}{2}=36\ \Omega.
 $$

With the same potential difference of $120\ \text{V} $ across each half, the rate of energy dissipation in each half is

$$ 
P'=\frac{V^2}{R'}=\frac{(120\ \text{V})^2}{36\ \Omega}=400\ \text{W}.
 $$

## Answer
1. $200\ \text{W} $
2. $400\ \text{W} $ for each half

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power in a resistor: $P=iV $
- Resistive dissipation: $P=i^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R} $
- Resistance of a uniform wire is proportional to its length
