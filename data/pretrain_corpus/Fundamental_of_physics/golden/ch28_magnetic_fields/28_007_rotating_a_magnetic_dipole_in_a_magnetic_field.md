# Sample Problem 28.07: Rotating a magnetic dipole in a magnetic field

## Problem
Figure 28-21 shows a circular coil with 250 turns, an area $A $ of $2.52 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{m}^2 $, and a current of $100\,\text{mA} $. The coil is at rest in a uniform magnetic field of magnitude $B = 0.85\,\text{T} $, with its magnetic dipole moment initially aligned with $ \mathbf{B}  $.

(a) In Fig. 28-21, what is the direction of the current in the coil?

(b) How much work would the torque applied by an external agent have to do on the coil to rotate it $90^\circ $ from its initial orientation?

## Key ideas
- Right-hand rule for a current loop: curl the fingers in the direction of current; the thumb gives the direction of the magnetic dipole moment $\mathbf{m} $.
- The magnetic dipole orientation energy is
  $$ 
  U(\theta) = -\mathbf{m}\cdot \mathbf{B} = -mB\cos\theta.
   $$
- If an external agent rotates a dipole slowly from $\theta_i $ to $\theta_f $ and the dipole is stationary initially and finally, the work done is
  $$ 
  W_a = \Delta U = U_f - U_i.
   $$

## Solution
(a) Use the right-hand rule: imagine cupping the coil with your right hand so that your right thumb is outstretched in the direction of $\mathbf{m} $. The direction in which your fingers curl around the coil is the direction of the current in the coil.

Thus, in the wires on the near side of the coil — those we see in Fig. 28-21 — the current is from top to bottom.

(b) The magnetic dipole moment magnitude is
$$ 
m = NiA
 $$
with
$$ 
N = 250,\quad i = 100\,\text{mA} = 0.100\,\text{A},\quad A = 2.52\times 10^{-4}\,\text{m}^2.
 $$
Thus,
$$ 
m = (250)(0.100)(2.52\times 10^{-4}) = 6.30\times 10^{-3}\,\text{A}\cdot\text{m}^2.
 $$

Initially, $\mathbf{m} $ is aligned with $\mathbf{B} $, so $\theta_i = 0 $. After a $90^\circ $ rotation, $\theta_f = 90^\circ $.

The initial and final orientation energies are
$$ 
U_i = -mB\cos 0 = -mB,
 $$
$$ 
U_f = -mB\cos 90^\circ = 0.
 $$

So the work done by the external agent is
$$ 
W_a = U_f - U_i = 0 - (-mB) = mB.
 $$

Substitute the values:
$$ 
W_a = (6.30\times 10^{-3})(0.85) = 5.355\times 10^{-3}\,\text{J}.
 $$

Rounded appropriately,
$$ 
W_a \approx 5.4\times 10^{-3}\,\text{J}.
 $$

## Answer
(a) The current is such that, on the near side of the coil, it goes from top to bottom.

(b) The required work is
$$ 
5.4\times 10^{-3}\,\text{J}.
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic dipole moment of a current loop
- Right-hand rule for current loops
- Magnetic orientation energy $U=-\mathbf{m}\cdot\mathbf{B} $
- Work done by an external agent in rotating a dipole
