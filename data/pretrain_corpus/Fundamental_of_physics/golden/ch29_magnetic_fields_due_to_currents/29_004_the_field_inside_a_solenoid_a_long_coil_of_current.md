# Sample Problem 29.04: The field inside a solenoid (a long coil of current)

## Problem
A solenoid has length $L = 1.23\ \text{m} $ and inner diameter $d = 3.55\ \text{cm} $, and it carries a current $i = 5.57\ \text{A} $. It consists of five close-packed layers, each with 850 turns along length $L $. What is $B $ at its center?

## Key ideas
The magnitude $B $ of the magnetic field along the solenoid’s central axis is related to the solenoid’s current $i $ and number of turns per unit length $n $ by Eq. 29-23:
$$ 
B = \mu_0 i n .
 $$

## Solution
The total number of turns is
$$ 
N = 5(850) = 4250.
 $$

Thus the number of turns per unit length is
$$ 
n = \frac{N}{L} = \frac{4250}{1.23\ \text{m}}.
 $$

Then
$$ 
B = \mu_0 i n
= (4\pi \times 10^{-7}\ \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A})(5.57\ \text{A})\left(\frac{4250}{1.23\ \text{m}}\right).
 $$

Carrying out the calculation gives
$$ 
B \approx 2.42 \times 10^{-2}\ \text{T}.
 $$

## Answer
$$ 
\boxed{2.42 \times 10^{-2}\ \text{T}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic field inside an ideal long solenoid
- Turns per unit length, $n = N/L $
- $\,B = \mu_0 i n $
