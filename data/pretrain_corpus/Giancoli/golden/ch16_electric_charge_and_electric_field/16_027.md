# Problem 16.27

## Question
(II) An electron is released from rest in a uniform electric field and accelerates to the north at a rate of 105 m/s². Find the magnitude and direction of the electric field.

## Final answer from source
south.

## Worked solution
For a charge in an electric field, the electric force is

$$ 
\vec F = q\vec E
 $$

and by Newton’s second law,

$$ 
\vec F = m\vec a
 $$

So,

$$ 
m\vec a = q\vec E
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\vec E = \frac{m\vec a}{q}
 $$

For an electron:

- mass: $m_e = 9.11\times 10^{-31}\,\text{kg} $
- charge: $q = -1.60\times 10^{-19}\,\text{C} $
- acceleration: $a = 105\,\text{m/s}^2 $ north

### Magnitude
$$ 
E = \frac{m a}{|q|}
= \frac{(9.11\times 10^{-31})(105)}{1.60\times 10^{-19}}
 $$

$$ 
E \approx 5.98\times 10^{-10}\,\text{N/C}
 $$

### Direction
Because the electron has negative charge, the electric force is opposite the electric field direction.

The electron accelerates north, so the force on it is north. Therefore the electric field must point south.

## Key concepts used
- Electric force: $\vec F = q\vec E $
- Newton’s second law: $\vec F = m\vec a $
- Negative charges accelerate opposite the direction of the electric field
- Electric field magnitude from $E = ma/|q| $
