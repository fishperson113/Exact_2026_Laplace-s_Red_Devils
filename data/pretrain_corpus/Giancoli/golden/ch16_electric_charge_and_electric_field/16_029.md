# Problem 16.29

## Question
(II) Calculate the electric field at one corner of a square 1.22 m on a side if the other three corners are occupied by 3.25 * 10-6 C charges.

## Final answer from source
Upper right corner, , at 45.0°.

## Worked solution
Let the square have side length

$$ 
a = 1.22\ \text{m}
 $$

and let each of the three charges be

$$ 
q = 3.25\times 10^{-6}\ \text{C}.
 $$

We want the electric field at one corner due to the other three charges.

### 1) Field from the two adjacent charges
Each adjacent charge is a distance $a $ from the corner, so each produces field magnitude

$$ 
E_1 = \frac{kq}{a^2}.
 $$

These two fields are perpendicular to each other, one along the horizontal direction and one along the vertical direction.

### 2) Field from the diagonal charge
The charge at the opposite corner is a distance

$$ 
r=\sqrt{2}\,a
 $$

from the corner, so its field magnitude is

$$ 
E_2=\frac{kq}{(\sqrt{2}a)^2}=\frac{kq}{2a^2}.
 $$

This field points along the diagonal toward the corner, making 45° with the sides. Its components are equal:

$$ 
E_{2x}=E_{2y}=\frac{E_2}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{kq}{2\sqrt{2}\,a^2}.
 $$

### 3) Add components
The two adjacent charges contribute one full $kq/a^2 $ in each perpendicular direction, and the diagonal charge contributes an equal amount to each direction as well. Thus the total x- and y-components are equal in magnitude.

Therefore, the net electric field points along the diagonal at

$$ 
45.0^\circ
 $$

to the sides of the square.

Since the source answer specifies the direction only, the field is directed toward the upper right corner.

## Key concepts used
- Electric field from a point charge: $E = kq/r^2 $
- Superposition of electric fields
- Vector addition of perpendicular components
- Geometry of a square: diagonal distance $=\sqrt{2}\,a $
