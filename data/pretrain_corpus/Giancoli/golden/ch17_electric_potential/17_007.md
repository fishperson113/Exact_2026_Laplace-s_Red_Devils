# Problem 17.7

## Question
(I) An electric field of 525 is desired between two parallel plates 11.0 mm apart. How large a voltage should be applied? V(cid:2)m

## Final answer from source
5.78 V.

## Worked solution
For parallel plates, the magnitude of the uniform electric field is related to the potential difference by

$$ 
E=\frac{V}{d}
 $$

so

$$ 
V = E d
 $$

Given:
- $E = 525\ \text{V/m} $
- $d = 11.0\ \text{mm} = 0.0110\ \text{m} $

Now calculate:

$$ 
V = (525\ \text{V/m})(0.0110\ \text{m}) = 5.775\ \text{V}
 $$

Rounding to three significant figures:

$$ 
V \approx 5.78\ \text{V}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Uniform electric field between parallel plates
- Relationship $E = V/d $
- Unit conversion from mm to m
- Significant figures
