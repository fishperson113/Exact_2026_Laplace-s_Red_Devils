# Problem 17.59

## Question
(I) Write the decimal number 116 in binary.

## Final answer from source
1110100.

## Worked solution
To convert $116 $ from decimal to binary, express it as a sum of powers of 2:

$$ 
116 = 64 + 32 + 16 + 4
 $$

Now write the place values of powers of 2 from $64 $ down to $1 $:

$$ 
64,\ 32,\ 16,\ 8,\ 4,\ 2,\ 1
 $$

Assign 1 to the powers included in the sum and 0 to those not included:

- $64 $: 1
- $32 $: 1
- $16 $: 1
- $8 $: 0
- $4 $: 1
- $2 $: 0
- $1 $: 0

So the binary representation is:

$$ 
1110100_2
 $$

This matches the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Decimal-to-binary conversion by powers of 2
- Place-value representation in base 2
- Identifying which powers of 2 sum to the given decimal number
