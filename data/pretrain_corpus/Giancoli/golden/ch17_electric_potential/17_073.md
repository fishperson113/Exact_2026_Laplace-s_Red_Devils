# Problem 17.73

## Question
A proton are accelerated by the same voltage V. Which gains the greater kinetic energy, and by what factor? and an alpha particle (q = ±2e)

## Final answer from source
Alpha particle, 2.

## Worked solution
When a charged particle is accelerated through a potential difference $V $, the electric potential energy change is converted into kinetic energy:

$$ 
\Delta K = qV
 $$

So the kinetic energy gained depends on the magnitude of the particle’s charge.

- For a proton: $q = +e $, so
  $$ 
  K_p = eV
   $$

- For an alpha particle: $q = +2e $, so
  $$ 
  K_\alpha = 2eV
   $$

Now compare the two kinetic energies:

$$ 
\frac{K_\alpha}{K_p}=\frac{2eV}{eV}=2
 $$

So the alpha particle gains twice the kinetic energy of the proton.

## Key concepts used
- A charge accelerated through a potential difference gains kinetic energy $K = qV $.
- Kinetic energy gained is proportional to the magnitude of charge.
- A proton has charge $+e $; an alpha particle has charge $+2e $.
