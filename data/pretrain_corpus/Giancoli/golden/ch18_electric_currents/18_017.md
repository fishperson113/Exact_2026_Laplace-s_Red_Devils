# Problem 18.17

## Question
(II) Compute the voltage drop along a 21-m length of household no. 14 copper wire (used in 15-A circuits). The wire has diameter 1.628 mm and carries a 12-A current.

## Final answer from source
2.0 V.

## Worked solution
Use the relation between voltage drop, current, and resistance:

$$ 
V = IR
 $$

For a uniform wire,

$$ 
R = \rho \frac{L}{A}
 $$

where:
- $\rho $ is the resistivity of copper,
- $L = 21\ \text{m} $,
- $A $ is the cross-sectional area of the wire.

For copper, take

$$ 
\rho \approx 1.72\times 10^{-8}\ \Omega\cdot\text{m}
 $$

The wire diameter is $1.628\ \text{mm} = 1.628\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m} $, so its radius is

$$ 
r = \frac{1.628\times 10^{-3}}{2} = 8.14\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}
 $$

Thus the cross-sectional area is

$$ 
A = \pi r^2 = \pi(8.14\times 10^{-4})^2
\approx 2.08\times 10^{-6}\ \text{m}^2
 $$

Now compute the resistance:

$$ 
R = \left(1.72\times 10^{-8}\right)\frac{21}{2.08\times 10^{-6}}
\approx 0.174\ \Omega
 $$

Then the voltage drop is

$$ 
V = IR = (12\ \text{A})(0.174\ \Omega) \approx 2.09\ \text{V}
 $$

Rounding appropriately gives:

$$ 
V \approx 2.0\ \text{V}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Ohm’s law: $V = IR $
- Resistance of a uniform wire: $R = \rho L/A $
- Cross-sectional area of a circular wire: $A = \pi r^2 $
- Unit conversion from mm to m
- Significant figures and rounding
