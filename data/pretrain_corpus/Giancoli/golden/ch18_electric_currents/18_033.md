# Problem 18.33

## Question
(II) A 120-V hair dryer has two settings: 950 W and 1450 W. (a) At which setting do you guess the resistance to be higher? After making a guess, determine the resistance at (b) the lower setting, and (c) the higher setting.

## Final answer from source
(a) 950 W; (b) (c)

## Worked solution
For a device operating at a fixed voltage, the electrical power is

$$ 
P=\frac{V^2}{R}
 $$

so the resistance is

$$ 
R=\frac{V^2}{P}.
 $$

Because $R $ is inversely proportional to $P $ when $V $ is fixed, the setting with the **lower power** has the **higher resistance**.

### (a) Which setting has the higher resistance?
The lower power setting is **950 W**, so that setting has the higher resistance.

### (b) Resistance at the lower setting, 950 W
Using $V=120\text{ V} $ and $P=950\text{ W} $:

$$ 
R=\frac{(120)^2}{950}
=\frac{14400}{950}
\approx 15.2\ \Omega
 $$

So the resistance is about

$$ 
\boxed{15\ \Omega}
 $$

to two significant figures.

### (c) Resistance at the higher setting, 1450 W
Using $V=120\text{ V} $ and $P=1450\text{ W} $:

$$ 
R=\frac{(120)^2}{1450}
=\frac{14400}{1450}
\approx 9.93\ \Omega
 $$

So the resistance is about

$$ 
\boxed{9.9\ \Omega}
 $$

to two significant figures.

These results are consistent with the source answer’s identification of the higher-resistance setting as 950 W.

## Key concepts used
- Electrical power relation for a resistor: $P=V^2/R $
- For fixed voltage, resistance is inversely proportional to power
- Unit consistency: volts, watts, ohms
- Two significant figure rounding for reported answers
