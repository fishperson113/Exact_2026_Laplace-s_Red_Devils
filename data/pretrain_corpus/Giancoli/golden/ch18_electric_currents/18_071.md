# Problem 18.71

## Question
Suppose a current is given by the equation $ I = 1.40 \sin 210\, t  $, where $ I  $ is in amperes and $ t  $ is in seconds. (a) What is the frequency? (b) What is the rms value of the current? (c) If this is the current through a resistor, write the equation that describes the voltage as a function of time.

## Final answer from source
(a) 33 Hz; (b) 0.990 A; (c)

## Worked solution
The current has the standard sinusoidal form

$$ 
I(t)=I_0\sin(\omega t)
 $$

so we can identify:

- Peak current: $\;I_0 = 1.40\ \text{A} $
- Angular frequency: $\;\omega = 210\ \text{rad/s} $

### (a) Frequency
The relation between angular frequency and frequency is

$$ 
\omega = 2\pi f
 $$

so

$$ 
f=\frac{\omega}{2\pi}=\frac{210}{2\pi}\approx 33.4\ \text{Hz}
 $$

Rounded to the source answer:

$$ 
\boxed{f=33\ \text{Hz}}
 $$

### (b) RMS current
For a sinusoidal current,

$$ 
I_{\text{rms}}=\frac{I_0}{\sqrt{2}}
 $$

Thus,

$$ 
I_{\text{rms}}=\frac{1.40}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.990\ \text{A}
 $$

So,

$$ 
\boxed{I_{\text{rms}}=0.990\ \text{A}}
 $$

### (c) Voltage as a function of time
If the current passes through a resistor of resistance $R $, then Ohm’s law gives

$$ 
V(t)=RI(t)
 $$

Substitute the given current:

$$ 
V(t)=R\bigl(1.40\sin(210t)\bigr)
 $$

So the voltage is

$$ 
\boxed{V(t)=1.40R\sin(210t)}
 $$

where $R $ is the resistance in ohms and $V $ is in volts.

## Key concepts used
- Sinusoidal current form $I=I_0\sin(\omega t) $
- Angular frequency relation $\omega=2\pi f $
- RMS value for a sine wave: $I_{\text{rms}}=I_0/\sqrt{2} $
- Ohm’s law for a resistor: $V(t)=RI(t) $
