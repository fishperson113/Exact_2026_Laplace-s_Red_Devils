# Problem 18.77

## Question
A proposed electric vehicle makes use of storage batteries as its source of energy. It is powered by 24 batteries, each 95 A·h. Assume that the car is driven on level roads at 12 V, an average speed of 45 km/h, and the average friction force is 440 N. Assume 100% efficiency and neglect energy used for acceleration. No energy is consumed when the vehicle is stopped, since the engine doesn’t need to idle.  
(a) Determine the horsepower required.  
(b) After approximately how many kilometers must the batteries be recharged?

## Final answer from source
(a) 7.4 hp; (b) 220 km.

## Worked solution
We use the fact that, at constant speed on level ground, the motor only needs to supply power against the average friction force.

### (a) Horsepower required
The mechanical power needed is

$$ 
P = Fv
 $$

where  
- $F = 440\ \text{N} $ is the average friction force,  
- $v = 45\ \text{km/h} $ is the speed.

Convert the speed to SI units:

$$ 
45\ \text{km/h} = 45 \times \frac{1000\ \text{m}}{3600\ \text{s}} = 12.5\ \text{m/s}
 $$

Now compute the power:

$$ 
P = (440\ \text{N})(12.5\ \text{m/s}) = 5500\ \text{W}
 $$

Convert watts to horsepower using $1\ \text{hp} \approx 746\ \text{W} $:

$$ 
P = \frac{5500}{746} \approx 7.37\ \text{hp} \approx 7.4\ \text{hp}
 $$

So the required horsepower is

$$ 
\boxed{7.4\ \text{hp}}
 $$

---

### (b) Distance before recharging
Each battery has a capacity of $95\ \text{A·h} $. With 24 batteries, the total battery capacity is

$$ 
Q = 24(95\ \text{A·h}) = 2280\ \text{A·h}
 $$

The problem statement includes “12 V,” which is taken as the battery voltage for the energy calculation. Thus the total stored energy is

$$ 
E = VQ
 $$

Convert ampere-hours to coulombs or use watt-hours directly:

$$ 
E = (12\ \text{V})(2280\ \text{A·h}) = 27360\ \text{W·h}
 $$

Since

$$ 
1\ \text{W·h} = 3600\ \text{J}
 $$

the total energy in joules is

$$ 
E = 27360 \times 3600 \approx 9.85\times 10^7\ \text{J}
 $$

Now use work against friction:

$$ 
W = Fd
 $$

At 100% efficiency, all stored energy goes into overcoming friction, so

$$ 
E = Fd
 $$

Thus

$$ 
d = \frac{E}{F} = \frac{9.85\times 10^7\ \text{J}}{440\ \text{N}}
\approx 2.24\times 10^5\ \text{m}
 $$

Convert to kilometers:

$$ 
d \approx 224\ \text{km} \approx 220\ \text{km}
 $$

So the batteries must be recharged after approximately

$$ 
\boxed{220\ \text{km}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Mechanical power at constant speed: $P = Fv $
- Unit conversion: km/h to m/s
- Horsepower conversion: $1\ \text{hp} \approx 746\ \text{W} $
- Battery energy from capacity: $E = VQ $
- Work-energy relation against friction: $W = Fd $
- Idealized 100% efficiency assumption
