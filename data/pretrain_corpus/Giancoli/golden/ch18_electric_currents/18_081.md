# Problem 18.81

## Question
In an automobile, the system voltage varies from about 12 V when the car is off to about 13.8 V when the car is on and the charging system is in operation, a difference of 15%. By what percentage does the power delivered to the headlights vary as the voltage changes from 12 V to 13.8 V? Assume the headlight resistance remains constant. 1.5 (cid:3),

## Final answer from source
32% increase.

## Worked solution
For a headlight with constant resistance $R $, the electrical power is

$$ 
P=\frac{V^2}{R}
 $$

So the power is proportional to the square of the voltage:

$$ 
P \propto V^2
 $$

Let the initial voltage be $V_1 = 12\text{ V} $ and the final voltage be $V_2 = 13.8\text{ V} $.

The ratio of the powers is

$$ 
\frac{P_2}{P_1}=\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)^2
= \left(\frac{13.8}{12}\right)^2
 $$

$$ 
\frac{13.8}{12}=1.15
 $$

$$ 
\frac{P_2}{P_1}=(1.15)^2=1.3225
 $$

Thus the power increases by

$$ 
(1.3225-1)\times 100\% = 32.25\%
 $$

Rounded to the nearest whole percent, the power delivered to the headlights increases by about

$$ 
\boxed{32\%}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Electric power in a resistor: $P=V^2/R $
- With constant resistance, power scales as the square of voltage
- Percentage change from a ratio: $\left(P_2/P_1 - 1\right)\times 100\% $
