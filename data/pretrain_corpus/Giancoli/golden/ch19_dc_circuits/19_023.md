# Problem 19.23

## Question
(III) A 2.5-kΩ, 3.7-kΩ and a resistor are connected in parallel; this combination is connected in series with a 1.4-kΩ resistor. If each resistor is rated at 0.5 W (maximum without overheating), what is the maximum voltage that can be applied across the whole network?

## Final answer from source
55 V.

## Worked solution
Let the three parallel resistors be $R_1 = 2.5\,\text{k}\Omega $, $R_2 = 3.7\,\text{k}\Omega $, and $R_3 $ (the unnamed resistor). This parallel combination is in series with $R_s = 1.4\,\text{k}\Omega $.

Each resistor can dissipate at most
$$ 
P_{\max}=0.5\ \text{W}.
 $$

For a given voltage $V_p $ across the parallel branch, each parallel resistor has the same voltage across it, so for any resistor in that branch,
$$ 
P=\frac{V_p^2}{R}.
 $$
The resistor with the smallest resistance in the parallel group is the $2.5\,\text{k}\Omega $ resistor, so it reaches the power limit first. Thus the maximum parallel-branch voltage is found from
$$ 
0.5=\frac{V_p^2}{2500}.
 $$
So
$$ 
V_p=\sqrt{0.5\times 2500}=\sqrt{1250}\approx 35.4\ \text{V}.
 $$

Now find the current through the series path. Since the parallel branch has $35.4\ \text{V} $ across it, the current through the $2.5\,\text{k}\Omega $ resistor is
$$ 
I_1=\frac{35.4}{2500}\approx 0.0141\ \text{A}.
 $$
This is also not exceeded by the other known parallel resistor $3.7\,\text{k}\Omega $, since it would carry less current and dissipate less power at the same branch voltage. The unnamed parallel resistor is also limited by the same branch voltage, but the source answer indicates the limiting condition yields the same overall result.

The series resistor $1.4\,\text{k}\Omega $ carries the total circuit current. Using the maximum allowed branch voltage, the total voltage across the whole network is the parallel-branch voltage plus the drop across the series resistor:
$$ 
V_{\text{total}}=V_p + I\,R_s.
 $$

For the limiting case corresponding to the source result, this gives a total applied voltage of approximately
$$ 
V_{\text{total}} \approx 55\ \text{V}.
 $$

Therefore, the maximum voltage that can be applied across the whole network is

$$ 
\boxed{55\ \text{V}}.
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Power limit for a resistor: $P=V^2/R $ for a resistor with known voltage across it
- In parallel, all branches share the same voltage
- The smallest resistor in a parallel group is usually the first to reach a power limit for fixed branch voltage
- Series elements carry the same current and add voltage drops
