In a circuit with real batteries, the terminal voltage of each battery is related to its emf by
$$ 
V_{\text{term}} = \varepsilon - Ir
 $$
for a battery delivering current, where $r $ is the internal resistance and $I $ is the current through that battery. For a battery being charged, the sign convention reverses, but the terminal voltage still follows from the circuit current and the resistor drop across the internal resistance.
