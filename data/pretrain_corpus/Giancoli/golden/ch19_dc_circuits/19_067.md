# Problem 19.67

## Question
Suppose that you wish to apply a 0.25-V potential difference between two points on the human body. The resistance is about and you only have a 1.5-V battery. How can you connect up one or more resistors to produce the desired voltage? 1800 (cid:2),

## Final answer from source
Put in series with the body.

## Worked solution
The problem statement appears to have an OCR omission in the sentence giving the body’s resistance; the intended resistance is about $1800\,\Omega $. Even with that missing detail, the source answer indicates the required circuit idea.

Let the human body be modeled as a resistance of about
$$ 
R_{\text{body}} \approx 1800\,\Omega.
 $$

We want only
$$ 
V_{\text{body}} = 0.25\,\text{V}
 $$
across the body, but the battery provides
$$ 
V_{\text{battery}} = 1.5\,\text{V}.
 $$

To reduce the voltage across the body, connect an additional resistor **in series** with the body. In a series circuit, the same current flows through both resistors, and the battery voltage divides between them.

The required current through the body is
$$ 
I=\frac{V_{\text{body}}}{R_{\text{body}}}
=\frac{0.25}{1800}
\approx 1.39\times 10^{-4}\,\text{A}.
 $$

With a 1.5-V battery, the total series resistance needed is
$$ 
R_{\text{total}}=\frac{V_{\text{battery}}}{I}
=\frac{1.5}{1.39\times10^{-4}}
\approx 1.08\times 10^{4}\,\Omega.
 $$

So the added series resistor should be
$$ 
R_{\text{add}} = R_{\text{total}} - R_{\text{body}}
\approx 1.08\times10^{4} - 1800
\approx 9.0\times10^{3}\,\Omega.
 $$

Thus, a resistor of about $9.0\,\text{k}\Omega $ placed **in series** with the body will drop the extra voltage so that the body gets about $0.25\,\text{V} $.

This matches the source answer: **put the resistor in series with the body**.

## Key concepts used
- Ohm’s law: $V=IR $
- Series circuits have the same current through each element
- Voltage division in series
- Using a resistor in series to reduce the voltage across a load
