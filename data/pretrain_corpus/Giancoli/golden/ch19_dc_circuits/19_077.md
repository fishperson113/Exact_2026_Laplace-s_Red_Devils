# Problem 19.77

## Question
A solar cell, 3.0 cm square, has an output of 350 mA at 0.80 V when exposed to full sunlight. A solar panel that delivers close to 1.3 A of current at an emf of 120 V to an external load is needed. How many cells will you need to create the panel? How big a panel will you need, and how should you connect the cells to one another?

## Final answer from source
600 cells; 4 banks in parallel, each containing 150 cells in series.

## Worked solution
A single solar cell provides:

- Current: $I_{\text{cell}} = 350\ \text{mA} = 0.350\ \text{A} $
- Voltage: $V_{\text{cell}} = 0.80\ \text{V} $

The required panel output is:

- Current: $I_{\text{panel}} \approx 1.3\ \text{A} $
- Voltage: $V_{\text{panel}} = 120\ \text{V} $

### 1) Determine how many cells must be in series
Cells connected in series add their voltages, while the current stays the same.

So the number of cells needed in one series string is

$$ 
N_s = \frac{120\ \text{V}}{0.80\ \text{V}} = 150
 $$

Thus, each series chain must contain **150 cells**.

### 2) Determine how many such strings must be in parallel
Parallel connections add currents, while the voltage stays the same.

Each series string can supply about $0.350\ \text{A} $, so the number of parallel strings needed is

$$ 
N_p = \frac{1.3\ \text{A}}{0.350\ \text{A}} \approx 3.71
 $$

Since you need a whole number of strings, round up to **4 parallel banks**.

Then the total current is

$$ 
4(0.350\ \text{A}) = 1.4\ \text{A}
 $$

which is close to the required $1.3\ \text{A} $.

### 3) Total number of cells
$$ 
N_{\text{total}} = N_s N_p = 150 \times 4 = 600
 $$

So you need **600 cells** total.

### 4) Panel size
Each cell is a square of side $3.0\ \text{cm} $, so its area is

$$ 
A_{\text{cell}} = (3.0\ \text{cm})^2 = 9.0\ \text{cm}^2
 $$

With 600 cells, the total cell area is

$$ 
A_{\text{panel}} = 600(9.0\ \text{cm}^2) = 5400\ \text{cm}^2
 $$

A convenient arrangement is 4 banks in parallel, each bank containing 150 cells in series. If arranged as 4 rows of 150 cells, the panel dimensions would be approximately

$$ 
(150)(3.0\ \text{cm}) = 450\ \text{cm}
 $$

by

$$ 
(4)(3.0\ \text{cm}) = 12\ \text{cm}
 $$

so the panel would be about **4.5 m by 12 cm**, depending on the exact layout.

### Conclusion
You need **600 cells total**, arranged as **4 parallel banks**, each bank consisting of **150 cells in series**.

## Key concepts used
- Cells in **series** add voltage
- Cells in **parallel** add current
- Total number of cells is the product of series cells per string and number of parallel strings
- Panel area comes from the area of each square cell times the number of cells
