# Problem 19.95

## Question
The variable capacitance of an old radio tuner consists of four plates connected together placed alternately between four other plates, also connected together (Fig. 19-92). Each plate is separated from its neighbor by 1.6 mm of air. One set of plates can move so that the area of overlap of each plate varies from 2.0 cm2 to 9.0 cm2. (a) Are these seven capacitors connected in series or in parallel? (b) Determine the range of capacitance values.

## Final answer from source
(a) In parallel; (b) 7.7 pF to 35 pF.

## Worked solution
Because the exact figure is not fully available here, the problem is treated from the text description only. The statement says there are four plates connected together alternating with four other connected plates, with air gaps between neighboring plates. That means each adjacent pair of plates forms a capacitor with the same voltage across it, since one group of plates is tied together and the other group is tied together.

### (a) Connection type
Each gap between a plate from one set and a plate from the other set acts as a capacitor. Since all plates in one group are electrically common, and all plates in the other group are electrically common, all these individual capacitors share the same two nodes.

So the capacitors are connected **in parallel**.

### (b) Range of capacitance
For a parallel-plate capacitor,
$$ 
C=\kappa \varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d}
 $$
Here the plates are separated by air, so $\kappa \approx 1 $, and
$$ 
C=\varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d}.
 $$

Given:
- $d = 1.6\text{ mm} = 1.6\times 10^{-3}\text{ m} $
- area varies from $A_{\min}=2.0\text{ cm}^2 = 2.0\times10^{-4}\text{ m}^2 $
  to $A_{\max}=9.0\text{ cm}^2 = 9.0\times10^{-4}\text{ m}^2 $

First compute the capacitance of one gap for the minimum and maximum overlap areas:
$$ 
C_{\min,1}=\varepsilon_0\frac{2.0\times10^{-4}}{1.6\times10^{-3}}
 $$
$$ 
C_{\min,1}=(8.85\times10^{-12})\left(\frac{2.0}{1.6}\times10^{-1}\right)
\approx 1.1\times10^{-12}\text{ F}=1.1\text{ pF}
 $$

$$ 
C_{\max,1}=\varepsilon_0\frac{9.0\times10^{-4}}{1.6\times10^{-3}}
 $$
$$ 
C_{\max,1}=(8.85\times10^{-12})\left(\frac{9.0}{1.6}\times10^{-1}\right)
\approx 5.0\times10^{-12}\text{ F}=5.0\text{ pF}
 $$

Now count the number of capacitors in parallel. With four plates in one group alternating with four in the other, the number of gaps between adjacent plates is seven, so there are **seven identical capacitors in parallel**.

Thus the total capacitance is
$$ 
C_{\text{tot}}=7C_1.
 $$

So:
$$ 
C_{\min}=7(1.1\text{ pF})\approx 7.7\text{ pF}
 $$
$$ 
C_{\max}=7(5.0\text{ pF})\approx 35\text{ pF}
 $$

Therefore the capacitance ranges from **7.7 pF to 35 pF**.

## Key concepts used
- Parallel-plate capacitor formula $C=\varepsilon_0 A/d $
- Capacitors sharing the same two nodes are in parallel
- Total capacitance in parallel: $C_{\text{eq}}=\sum C_i $
- Unit conversion: $\text{cm}^2 $ to $\text{m}^2 $, mm to m
