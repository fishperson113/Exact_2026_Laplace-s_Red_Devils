# Problem 20.13

## Question
(I) Determine the direction of **F** for each case in Fig. 20-53, where **B** represents the maximum magnetic force on a positively charged particle moving with velocity **v**. FIGURE 20-53 Problem 13.  
(a) **v** **B** **F**  
(b) **v** **B** **F**  
(c) **v** **B** **F**

## Final answer from source
(a) To the right; (b) downward; (c) into the page.

## Worked solution
This problem uses the magnetic force on a moving charge:

$$ 
\vec F = q\, \vec v \times \vec B
 $$

For a **positively charged** particle, the force direction is given by the **right-hand rule** for the cross product $\vec v \times \vec B $.

Because the OCR/scan of Fig. 20-53 is incomplete in the prompt, the exact vector orientations in each subfigure are not fully visible here. So the detailed geometric derivation for each panel cannot be reconstructed with certainty from the text alone. However, using the source answer as the constraint, the force directions are:

- **(a)** to the right
- **(b)** downward
- **(c)** into the page

These are the directions consistent with the right-hand rule applied to the corresponding $\vec v $ and $\vec B $ directions in the original figure.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a moving charge: $\vec F = q\,\vec v \times \vec B $
- Right-hand rule for cross products
- For a positive charge, $\vec F $ points in the direction of $\vec v \times \vec B $
- Direction depends on the orientation of $\vec v $ and $\vec B $, not on their magnitudes
