# Problem 20.17

## Question
(II) A 1.5-MeV (kinetic energy) proton enters a 0.30-T field, in a plane perpendicular to the field. What is the radius of its path? See Section 17-4.

## Final answer from source
0.59 m.

## Worked solution
For a charged particle moving perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field, the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:

$$ 
qvB=\frac{mv^2}{r}
 $$

so

$$ 
r=\frac{mv}{qB}.
 $$

We are given the proton’s kinetic energy:

$$ 
K = 1.5\ \text{MeV} = 1.5\times 10^6 \text{ eV}
 $$

Convert to joules:

$$ 
K = (1.5\times 10^6)(1.60\times 10^{-19}) \approx 2.40\times 10^{-13}\ \text{J}.
 $$

Use the nonrelativistic kinetic-energy relation:

$$ 
K=\frac12 mv^2
 $$

to find the speed:

$$ 
v=\sqrt{\frac{2K}{m}}.
 $$

For a proton,

$$ 
m_p = 1.67\times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg},
\qquad
q = 1.60\times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}.
 $$

Thus,

$$ 
v=\sqrt{\frac{2(2.40\times 10^{-13})}{1.67\times 10^{-27}}}
\approx 1.7\times 10^7\ \text{m/s}.
 $$

Now compute the radius:

$$ 
r=\frac{mv}{qB}
= \frac{(1.67\times 10^{-27})(1.7\times 10^7)}{(1.60\times 10^{-19})(0.30)}.
 $$

$$ 
r \approx 0.59\ \text{m}.
 $$

This matches the source answer.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a moving charge: $F = qvB $ for motion perpendicular to $B $
- Circular motion: $F = mv^2/r $
- Kinetic energy relation: $K = \tfrac12 mv^2 $
- Unit conversion: MeV to joules
- Proton charge and mass values
