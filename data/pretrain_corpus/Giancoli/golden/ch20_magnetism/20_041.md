# Problem 20.41

## Question
(II) A third wire is placed in the plane of the two wires shown in Fig. 20-57 parallel and just to the right. If it carries 25.0 A upward, what force per meter of length does it exert on each of the other two wires? Assume it is 2.8 mm from the nearest wire, center to center.

## Final answer from source
Closer wire: attract; farther wire: repel.

## Worked solution
Because the problem refers to **Fig. 20-57** for the directions of the original two wires’ currents, the full geometry is not included in the text provided here. The only source answer available states the **nature of the force** on each wire:

- the **closer wire** is **attracted**
- the **farther wire** is **repelled**

This is consistent with the standard rule for parallel current-carrying wires:

- **currents in the same direction attract**
- **currents in opposite directions repel**

The third wire carries **25.0 A upward** and is placed to the **right** of the two existing wires. Therefore:

1. The wire whose current is in the **same direction** as the 25.0 A upward current must be **attracted** toward the third wire.
2. The wire whose current is in the **opposite direction** must be **repelled** away from the third wire.

So the source answer is:

- **Closer wire: attract**
- **Farther wire: repel**

Because the statement depends on the omitted figure for the full current directions and the source answer gives only the attraction/repulsion result, the numerical force per meter cannot be reconstructed from the text alone here.

## Key concepts used
- Force between parallel current-carrying wires
- Same-direction currents attract
- Opposite-direction currents repel
- Need for full figure geometry to determine complete vector force details
