## Worked solution
We use Ampère’s law for a circular Amperian loop of radius $r $ centered on the toroid:

$$ 
\oint \mathbf{B}\cdot d\mathbf{\ell}=\mu_0 I_{\text{enc}}
 $$

Because the toroid is symmetric, $\mathbf{B} $ is tangent to the circular path and has constant magnitude on that path, so

$$ 
\oint \mathbf{B}\cdot d\mathbf{\ell}=B(2\pi r)
 $$

### (a) Field inside the toroid
For a circular path inside the winding region, the loop encloses all $N $ turns, each carrying current $I $. Thus

$$ 
I_{\text{enc}}=NI
 $$

So Ampère’s law gives

$$ 
B(2\pi r)=\mu_0 NI
 $$

and therefore

$$ 
B=\frac{\mu_0 NI}{2\pi r}
 $$

This matches the source answer form written with $R $:

$$ 
B=\frac{\mu_0 NI}{2\pi R}
 $$

for a path at radius $R $ inside the toroid.

### (b) Field outside the toroid
For a circular Amperian loop outside the toroid, the net enclosed current is zero because the currents through the surface cancel in pairs. Hence

$$ 
I_{\text{enc}}=0
 $$

So

$$ 
B(2\pi r)=0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad B=0
 $$

Thus the magnetic field outside the toroid is

$$ 
B=0
 $$

### (c) Uniformity of the field inside
The field is not uniform, because from

$$ 
B=\frac{\mu_0 NI}{2\pi r}
 $$

we see that $B $ depends on the distance $r $ from the center of the toroid. Therefore, inside the toroid the field varies inversely with radius:

$$ 
B\propto \frac{1}{r}
 $$

So the field is stronger on the inner side of the toroid and weaker on the outer side.

## Key concepts used
- Ampère’s law: $\oint \mathbf{B}\cdot d\mathbf{\ell}=\mu_0 I_{\text{enc}} $
- Symmetry of the toroid makes $\mathbf{B} $ tangential and constant on a circular path
- Enclosed current is $NI $ inside the toroid and zero outside
- Magnetic field inside a toroid varies as $1/r $, so it is not uniform
