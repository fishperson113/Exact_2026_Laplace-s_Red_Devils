# Problem 20.71

## Question
Near the equator, the EarthŌĆÖs magnetic field points almost horizontally to the north and has magnitude $B = 0.50 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{T} $. What should be the magnitude and direction for the velocity of an electron if its weight is to be exactly balanced by the magnetic force?

## Final answer from source
west.

## Worked solution
To balance the electronŌĆÖs weight, the magnetic force must be equal in magnitude to the gravitational force and directed upward.

### 1) Forces involved
- Weight: $F_g = mg $, downward.
- Magnetic force on a moving charge:
  $$ 
  F_B = |q|vB\sin\theta
   $$
  where $\theta $ is the angle between the velocity and the magnetic field.

Near the equator, EarthŌĆÖs magnetic field is:
- horizontal,
- directed north.

To get an upward magnetic force on an electron, we need to use the right-hand rule carefully, remembering the charge is negative.

### 2) Direction of the velocity
Let:
- north be $+\hat{y} $,
- east be $+\hat{x} $,
- up be $+\hat{z} $.

The magnetic field is north, so $\vec B = +\hat{y} $.

For a positive charge, $\vec v \times \vec B $ must point upward. Since the electron is negative, its magnetic force is opposite to $\vec v \times \vec B $.

If the electron moves west, then $\vec v = -\hat{x} $.  
Compute:
$$ 
\vec v \times \vec B = (-\hat{x}) \times (+\hat{y}) = -(\hat{x}\times\hat{y}) = -\hat{z}
 $$
This is downward for a positive charge, so for an electron the force is upward.

Thus the electron must move **west**.

### 3) Magnitude of the velocity
For the magnetic force to balance the weight:
$$ 
|q|vB = mg
 $$
since the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field ($\sin\theta = 1 $).

So:
$$ 
v = \frac{mg}{|q|B}
 $$

Using:
- $m = 9.11\times10^{-31}\,\text{kg} $
- $g = 9.80\,\text{m/s}^2 $
- $|q| = 1.60\times10^{-19}\,\text{C} $
- $B = 0.50\times10^{-4}\,\text{T} = 5.0\times10^{-5}\,\text{T} $

$$ 
v = \frac{(9.11\times10^{-31})(9.80)}{(1.60\times10^{-19})(5.0\times10^{-5})}
 $$

$$ 
v \approx \frac{8.93\times10^{-30}}{8.0\times10^{-24}}
\approx 1.1\times10^{-6}\,\text{m/s}
 $$

### 4) Result
- **Direction:** west
- **Speed:** $1.1\times10^{-6}\,\text{m/s} $

This is consistent with the source answer, which gives the direction as **west**.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic force on a moving charge: $F = |q|vB\sin\theta $
- Balancing magnetic force with weight: $ |q|vB = mg  $
- Right-hand rule for $\vec v \times \vec B $
- Direction reversal for negative charges like the electron
