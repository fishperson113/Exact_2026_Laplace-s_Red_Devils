# Problem 21.3

## Question
(I) The rectangular loop in Fig. 21-58 is being pushed to the right, where the magnetic field points inward. In what direction is the induced current? Explain your reasoning. FIGURE 21-58 Problem 3.

## Final answer from source
Counterclockwise.

## Worked solution
As the rectangular loop is pushed to the right into a magnetic field directed inward (into the page), the amount of magnetic flux through the loop changes.

1. **Determine the change in magnetic flux**
   - The field points **inward**.
   - Pushing the loop to the right changes the area of the loop that lies in the magnetic-field region, so the magnetic flux through the loop changes.
   - By Lenz’s law, the induced current must oppose the **change** in flux.

2. **Apply Lenz’s law**
   - Since the loop is being pushed rightward into the region of inward magnetic field, the inward flux through the loop is increasing.
   - The induced current must produce a magnetic field that opposes this increase, so it must produce a field **out of the page**.

3. **Use the right-hand rule**
   - A loop current that produces a magnetic field **out of the page** must be **counterclockwise**.

Therefore, the induced current is **counterclockwise**.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic flux through a loop
- Faraday’s law and Lenz’s law
- Right-hand rule for the magnetic field of a current loop
- Direction of induced current opposing the change in flux
