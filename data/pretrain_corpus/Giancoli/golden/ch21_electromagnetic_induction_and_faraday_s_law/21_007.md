# Problem 21.7

## Question
(II) A 16-cm-diameter circular loop of wire is placed in a 0.50-T magnetic field. (a) When the plane of the loop is perpendicular to the field lines, what is the magnetic flux through the loop? (b) The plane of the loop is rotated until u it makes a 42° angle with the field lines. What is the angle in Eq. 21-1 for this situation? (c) What is the magnetic flux through the loop at this angle?

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) 48°; (c)

## Worked solution
The source answer appears incomplete/OCR-corrupted, so the final numerical values for parts (a) and (c) are not visible. Only part of the answer for (b) is legible: **48°**.

Using the standard magnetic-flux relation from Eq. 21-1,
$$ 
\Phi_B = BA\cos\theta
 $$
where $\theta $ is the angle between the magnetic field $\vec B $ and the **area vector** (normal to the plane of the loop), not the angle between the field and the plane itself.

### (a) Flux when the plane is perpendicular to the field lines
If the plane of the loop is perpendicular to the field lines, then the normal to the loop is parallel to the field. Thus,
$$ 
\theta = 0^\circ
 $$
The loop diameter is $16\text{ cm} $, so the radius is
$$ 
r = 8.0\text{ cm} = 0.080\text{ m}
 $$
Area:
$$ 
A = \pi r^2 = \pi(0.080)^2 = 2.01\times10^{-2}\ \text{m}^2
 $$
Hence,
$$ 
\Phi_B = (0.50)(2.01\times10^{-2})\cos 0^\circ
= 1.0\times10^{-2}\ \text{Wb}
 $$

### (b) Angle in Eq. 21-1
The plane of the loop makes a $42^\circ $ angle with the field lines. Since Eq. 21-1 uses the angle between $\vec B $ and the area normal, the required angle is
$$ 
\theta = 90^\circ - 42^\circ = 48^\circ
 $$
This matches the visible source answer.

### (c) Flux at this angle
Now use
$$ 
\Phi_B = BA\cos 48^\circ
 $$
with the same area as above:
$$ 
\Phi_B = (0.50)(2.01\times10^{-2})\cos 48^\circ
 $$
$$ 
\Phi_B \approx (1.005\times10^{-2})(0.669)
\approx 6.7\times10^{-3}\ \text{Wb}
 $$

Because the source answer text is incomplete, these computed values are the consistent physics result, but the source’s missing parts cannot be verified exactly.

## Key concepts used
- Magnetic flux: $\Phi_B = BA\cos\theta $
- $\theta $ is measured from the **area normal**, not the plane
- Circle area: $A=\pi r^2 $
- Unit conversion: $16\text{ cm diameter} \rightarrow 8.0\text{ cm radius} = 0.080\text{ m} $
- Relationship between plane angle and normal angle: $\theta = 90^\circ - \text{(plane-field angle)} $
