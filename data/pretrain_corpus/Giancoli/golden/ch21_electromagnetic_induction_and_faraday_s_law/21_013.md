# Problem 21.13

## Question
(II) When a car drives through the Earth’s magnetic field, an emf is induced in its vertical 55-cm-long radio antenna. If the Earth’s field points north with a dip angle of 38°, what is the maximum emf induced in the antenna and which direction(s) will the car be moving to produce this maximum value? The car’s speed is on a horizontal road. A5.0 * 10-5 TB 30.0 m(cid:2)s

## Final answer from source
0.65 mV, east or west.

## Worked solution
For a straight conductor moving through a magnetic field, the motional emf magnitude is

$$ 
\mathcal{E} = B L v \sin\theta
 $$

where:
- $B = 5.0 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{T} $ is Earth’s magnetic field,
- $L = 55\,\text{cm} = 0.55\,\text{m} $ is the antenna length,
- $v = 30.0\,\text{m/s} $ is the car’s speed,
- $\theta $ is the angle between the antenna and $\vec v \times \vec B $, or equivalently the relevant component of $\vec B $ perpendicular to the antenna.

The antenna is vertical, so only the horizontal component of Earth’s magnetic field contributes to the emf, because the vertical component is parallel to the antenna and does not produce a separation of charge along its length.

Earth’s field points north and dips downward by $38^\circ $. Therefore the horizontal component is

$$ 
B_h = B\cos 38^\circ
 $$

$$ 
B_h = (5.0\times10^{-5})(\cos 38^\circ)
\approx (5.0\times10^{-5})(0.788)
\approx 3.94\times10^{-5}\,\text{T}
 $$

For maximum emf, the car must move so that $\vec v $ is perpendicular to the horizontal magnetic field component. Since the horizontal field points north, the car must move east or west.

Thus,

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_{\max}= B_h L v
 $$

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_{\max}= (3.94\times10^{-5})(0.55)(30.0)
\approx 6.5\times10^{-4}\,\text{V}
 $$

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_{\max} \approx 0.65\,\text{mV}
 $$

So the maximum induced emf is

$$ 
\boxed{0.65\,\text{mV}}
 $$

and it occurs when the car is moving

$$ 
\boxed{\text{east or west}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Motional emf: $\mathcal{E} = B L v \sin\theta $
- Only the component of $\vec B $ perpendicular to the conductor contributes
- Earth’s magnetic field with dip angle has horizontal and vertical components
- Maximum emf occurs when motion is perpendicular to the relevant magnetic-field component
