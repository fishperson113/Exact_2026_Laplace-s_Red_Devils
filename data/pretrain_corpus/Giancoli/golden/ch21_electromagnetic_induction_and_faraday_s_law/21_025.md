# Problem 21.25

## Question
(I) The back emf in a motor is 72 V when operating at 1800 rpm. What would be the back emf at 2300 rpm if the magnetic field is unchanged?

## Final answer from source
92 V.

## Worked solution
For a motor with unchanged magnetic field, the back emf is proportional to the rotation rate:

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_{\text{back}} \propto \omega
 $$

So we can write

$$ 
\frac{\mathcal{E}_2}{\mathcal{E}_1}=\frac{\omega_2}{\omega_1}
 $$

Given:
- $\mathcal{E}_1 = 72\ \text{V} $
- $\omega_1 = 1800\ \text{rpm} $
- $\omega_2 = 2300\ \text{rpm} $

Solve for $\mathcal{E}_2 $:

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_2 = 72\ \text{V}\times \frac{2300}{1800}
 $$

$$ 
\mathcal{E}_2 = 72 \times 1.2778 \approx 92.0\ \text{V}
 $$

Thus, the back emf at 2300 rpm is

$$ 
\boxed{92\ \text{V}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Back emf in a motor is proportional to angular speed when the magnetic field is unchanged.
- Ratios can be used to compare two operating speeds.
- Units of rpm cancel in the ratio, leaving voltage as the result.
