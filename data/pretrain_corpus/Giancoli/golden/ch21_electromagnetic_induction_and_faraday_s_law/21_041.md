# Problem 21.41

## Question
(I) Determine the inductance L of a 0.60-m-long air-filled solenoid 2.9 cm in diameter containing 8500 loops.

## Final answer from source
0.10 H.

## Worked solution
For a long air-filled solenoid, the inductance is

$$ 
L=\mu_0 \frac{N^2 A}{\ell}
 $$

where:
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\ \text{H/m} $ is the permeability of free space,
- $N = 8500 $ is the number of turns,
- $A $ is the cross-sectional area,
- $\ell = 0.60\ \text{m} $ is the length of the solenoid.

### 1) Find the radius
The diameter is $2.9\ \text{cm} $, so the radius is

$$ 
r = \frac{2.9\ \text{cm}}{2} = 1.45\ \text{cm} = 0.0145\ \text{m}
 $$

### 2) Compute the area
$$ 
A = \pi r^2 = \pi (0.0145)^2 \approx 6.61\times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2
 $$

### 3) Substitute into the inductance formula
$$ 
L = (4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \frac{(8500)^2(6.61\times 10^{-4})}{0.60}
 $$

First compute $N^2 $:

$$ 
(8500)^2 = 7.225\times 10^7
 $$

Then

$$ 
L \approx (4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \frac{(7.225\times 10^7)(6.61\times 10^{-4})}{0.60}
 $$

$$ 
L \approx 0.10\ \text{H}
 $$

So the inductance of the solenoid is

$$ 
\boxed{0.10\ \text{H}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Inductance of a long solenoid: $L=\mu_0 N^2A/\ell $
- Cross-sectional area of a circle: $A=\pi r^2 $
- Unit conversion from cm to m
- Air-filled solenoid implies $\mu = \mu_0 $
