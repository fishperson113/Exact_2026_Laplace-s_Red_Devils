# Problem 21.43

## Question
(II) An air-filled cylindrical inductor has 2600 turns, and it is 2.5 cm in diameter and 28.2 cm long. (a) What is its inductance? (b) How many turns would you need to generate the same inductance if the core were iron-filled instead? Assume the magnetic permeability of iron is about 1200 times that of free space.

## Final answer from source
(a) (b) 75 turns.

## Worked solution
For a long cylindrical solenoid, the inductance is

$$ 
L=\mu \frac{N^2 A}{\ell}
 $$

where:
- $\mu $ is the permeability of the core material,
- $N $ is the number of turns,
- $A $ is the cross-sectional area,
- $\ell $ is the length.

### (a) Air-filled inductor
For air, $\mu=\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\ \text{H/m} $.

Given:
- $N=2600 $
- diameter $=2.5\text{ cm}\Rightarrow r=1.25\text{ cm}=0.0125\text{ m} $
- $\ell=28.2\text{ cm}=0.282\text{ m} $

Cross-sectional area:

$$ 
A=\pi r^2=\pi(0.0125)^2
 $$

$$ 
A\approx \pi(1.5625\times10^{-4})\approx 4.91\times10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2
 $$

Now compute $L $:

$$ 
L=(4\pi\times10^{-7})\frac{(2600)^2(4.91\times10^{-4})}{0.282}
 $$

$$ 
(2600)^2=6.76\times10^6
 $$

$$ 
L\approx (4\pi\times10^{-7})\frac{(6.76\times10^6)(4.91\times10^{-4})}{0.282}
 $$

$$ 
L\approx 1.5\times10^{-2}\ \text{H}
 $$

$$ 
\boxed{L\approx 0.015\ \text{H} = 15\ \text{mH}}
 $$

### (b) Number of turns with an iron core
For the same geometry, inductance scales as

$$ 
L\propto \mu N^2
 $$

To keep $L $ the same when replacing air by iron, with

$$ 
\mu_{\text{iron}}\approx 1200\,\mu_0
 $$

we need

$$ 
\mu_0 N_{\text{air}}^2 = \mu_{\text{iron}} N_{\text{iron}}^2
 $$

$$ 
N_{\text{iron}} = N_{\text{air}}\sqrt{\frac{\mu_0}{\mu_{\text{iron}}}}
=2600\sqrt{\frac{1}{1200}}
 $$

$$ 
N_{\text{iron}}\approx \frac{2600}{34.6}\approx 75
 $$

$$ 
\boxed{75\ \text{turns}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Inductance of a long solenoid: $L=\mu N^2A/\ell $
- Cross-sectional area of a cylinder: $A=\pi r^2 $
- Inductance scales linearly with permeability and with the square of turn count
- For equal inductance: $N\propto 1/\sqrt{\mu} $
