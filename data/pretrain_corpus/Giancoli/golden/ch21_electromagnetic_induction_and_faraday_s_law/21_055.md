# Problem 21.55

## Question
(I) At what frequency will a 32.0-mH inductor have a reac- tance of 660 (cid:3)?

## Final answer from source
3300 Hz.

## Worked solution
For an inductor, the inductive reactance is

$$ 
X_L = 2\pi f L
 $$

We are given:

- $X_L = 660\ \Omega $
- $L = 32.0\ \text{mH} = 32.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{H} $

Solve for the frequency $f $:

$$ 
f = \frac{X_L}{2\pi L}
 $$

Substitute the values:

$$ 
f = \frac{660}{2\pi(32.0\times 10^{-3})}
 $$

$$ 
f = \frac{660}{0.20106}
 $$

$$ 
f \approx 3.28\times 10^3\ \text{Hz}
 $$

Rounding to two significant figures, this is

$$ 
f \approx 3.3\times 10^3\ \text{Hz}
 $$

So the frequency is

$$ 
\boxed{3300\ \text{Hz}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- Inductive reactance formula: $X_L = 2\pi f L $
- Unit conversion: mH to H
- Solving for frequency from reactance
- Significant figures and rounding
