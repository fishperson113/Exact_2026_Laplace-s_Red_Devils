# Problem 22.7

## Question
(I) In an EM wave traveling west, the B field oscillates up and down vertically and has a frequency of 90.0 kHz and an rms strength of Determine the frequency and rms strength of the electric field. What is the direction of its oscillations? 7.75 * 10-9 T.

## Final answer from source
90.0 kHz, along the horizontal north-south line.

## Worked solution
For an electromagnetic wave:

1. **Electric and magnetic fields oscillate with the same frequency**  
   So the electric field frequency is the same as the magnetic field frequency:
   $$ 
   f_E = f_B = 90.0\ \text{kHz}
    $$

2. **RMS field strengths are related by the wave speed relation**
   For an EM wave in vacuum or air,
   $$ 
   E_{\text{rms}} = c\,B_{\text{rms}}
    $$
   where
   $$ 
   c = 3.00\times 10^8\ \text{m/s}, \quad B_{\text{rms}} = 7.75\times 10^{-9}\ \text{T}
    $$
   Thus,
   $$ 
   E_{\text{rms}} = (3.00\times 10^8)(7.75\times 10^{-9})
    $$
   $$ 
   E_{\text{rms}} = 2.33\ \text{V/m}
    $$

3. **Direction of the electric field**
   The wave travels **west**. In an EM wave, the propagation direction is perpendicular to both $\vec E $ and $\vec B $, with
   $$ 
   \vec E \times \vec B
    $$
   pointing in the direction of propagation.

   Since $\vec B $ oscillates **vertically** (up and down), $\vec E $ must be horizontal and perpendicular to both the westward travel direction and the vertical magnetic field. That places $\vec E $ along the **north-south line**.

So the electric field has:
- the same frequency as the magnetic field: **90.0 kHz**
- rms strength: **2.33 V/m**
- direction: **along the horizontal north-south line**

## Key concepts used
- In an EM wave, $E $ and $B $ have the same frequency.
- The magnitudes satisfy $E_{\text{rms}} = cB_{\text{rms}} $.
- $\vec E $, $\vec B $, and the direction of propagation are mutually perpendicular.
- The propagation direction is given by $\vec E \times \vec B $.
