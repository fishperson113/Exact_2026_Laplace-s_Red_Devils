# Problem 22.31

## Question
(II) Estimate the radiation pressure due to a bulb that emits 25 W of EM radiation at a distance of 9.5 cm from the center of the bulb. Estimate the force exerted on your fingertip if you place it at this point. 1.0-m2

## Final answer from source
away from bulb.

What can be done from the visible data:

1. Assume the bulb radiates isotropically with power  
   $$ 
   P = 25\ \text{W}
    $$
   at distance  
   $$ 
   r = 9.5\ \text{cm} = 0.095\ \text{m}.
    $$

2. The intensity at that distance is
   $$ 
   I=\frac{P}{4\pi r^2}
   =\frac{25}{4\pi(0.095)^2}.
    $$
   Compute:
   $$ 
   4\pi(0.095)^2 \approx 4\pi(0.009025)\approx 0.1134
    $$
   so
   $$ 
   I \approx \frac{25}{0.1134}\approx 2.2\times 10^2\ \text{W/m}^2.
    $$

3. For an absorbing surface, radiation pressure is
   $$ 
   p=\frac{I}{c}
    $$
   where $c=3.00\times 10^8\ \text{m/s} $. Thus
   $$ 
   p \approx \frac{2.2\times 10^2}{3.00\times 10^8}
   \approx 7.4\times 10^{-7}\ \text{Pa}.
    $$

4. If the fingertip area is $A=1.0\ \text{cm}^2=1.0\times10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2 $, then the force is
   $$ 
   F=pA\approx (7.4\times10^{-7})(1.0\times10^{-4})
   \approx 7.4\times10^{-11}\ \text{N}.
    $$

So the estimated radiation pressure is about

\boxed{7\times10^{-7}\ \text{Pa}}

- Isotropic radiation intensity: $I=P/(4\pi r^2) $
- Radiation pressure for absorbed light: $p=I/c $
