# Problem 22.37

## Question
(I) What are the wavelengths for two TV channels that broad- cast at 54.0 MHz (Channel 2) and 692 MHz (Channel 51)?

## Final answer from source
Channel 2: 5.56 m, Channel 51: 0.434 m.

## Worked solution
For electromagnetic waves, the wave speed in air is essentially the speed of light:

$$ 
c = f\lambda
 $$

so

$$ 
\lambda = \frac{c}{f}
 $$

with $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \,\text{m/s} $.

### Channel 2
Frequency:

$$ 
f = 54.0\,\text{MHz} = 54.0 \times 10^6\,\text{Hz}
 $$

Then

$$ 
\lambda = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{54.0 \times 10^6}
= 5.56\,\text{m}
 $$

### Channel 51
Frequency:

$$ 
f = 692\,\text{MHz} = 692 \times 10^6\,\text{Hz}
 $$

Then

$$ 
\lambda = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{692 \times 10^6}
= 0.4335\,\text{m}
\approx 0.434\,\text{m}
 $$

Thus, the wavelengths are:

- Channel 2: $5.56\,\text{m} $
- Channel 51: $0.434\,\text{m} $

## Key concepts used
- Electromagnetic wave relation: $c = f\lambda $
- Wavelength-frequency inverse relationship
- Unit conversion from MHz to Hz
