# Problem 22.45

## Question
A global positioning system (GPS) functions by determining the travel times for EM waves from various satellites to a land-based GPS receiver. If the receiver is to detect a change in travel distance on the order of 3 m, what is the associated change in travel time (in ns) that must be measured?

## Final answer from source
10 ns.

## Worked solution
For electromagnetic waves, the signal travels at the speed of light:

$$ 
c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
 $$

The change in travel time is

$$ 
\Delta t = \frac{\Delta x}{c}
 $$

with

$$ 
\Delta x = 3 \text{ m}
 $$

So,

$$ 
\Delta t = \frac{3}{3.0 \times 10^8} \text{ s}
= 1.0 \times 10^{-8} \text{ s}
 $$

Convert seconds to nanoseconds:

$$ 
1.0 \times 10^{-8} \text{ s} = 10 \text{ ns}
 $$

Thus, the required change in travel time is:

$$ 
\boxed{10 \text{ ns}}
 $$

## Key concepts used
- EM waves travel at the speed of light, $c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8\ \text{m/s} $
- Time–distance relation: $\Delta t = \Delta x / c $
- Unit conversion: $1\ \text{ns} = 10^{-9}\ \text{s} $
