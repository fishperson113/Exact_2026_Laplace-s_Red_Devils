# 9.3 Stability

Source: OpenStax College Physics 2e, Chapter 9, Section 9.3

## Concept explanation
A system may be in equilibrium without being stable. Stability depends on what happens after a small displacement from equilibrium.

There are three types of equilibrium:

- **Stable equilibrium**: if displaced from equilibrium, the system experiences a net force or torque in the direction opposite the displacement, so it tends to return to equilibrium.
  - Example: a marble at the bottom of a bowl experiences a restoring force when displaced.
- **Unstable equilibrium**: if displaced, the system experiences a net force or torque in the same direction as the displacement, so it moves farther from equilibrium.
  - Example: a ball on top of a hill accelerates away after a small displacement.
- **Neutral equilibrium**: equilibrium is independent of displacement from the original position.
  - Example: a marble on a flat horizontal surface.

A balanced system has its center of gravity directly over the pivot, so the torque of the total weight is zero. This is equivalent to the torques of the individual parts balancing about the pivot.

A system in stable equilibrium can become unstable if displaced far enough that the center of gravity is no longer above the base of support. Stability is increased by widening the base of support and lowering the center of gravity. Examples given include spreading the feet apart, bending the knees, and using a cane, crutch, or walker.

## Key formulas
- Stable equilibrium condition, stated qualitatively: after a small displacement, the net force or net torque acts opposite the displacement.
- Unstable equilibrium condition, stated qualitatively: after a displacement, the net force or net torque acts in the same direction as the displacement.
- Neutral equilibrium condition, stated qualitatively: the equilibrium does not depend on displacement.

Torque equilibrium for a balanced system:
- Total torque about the pivot is zero when the center of gravity is directly over the pivot.

## Variables and units
- **cg**: center of gravity
- **pivot**: point about which torque is considered
- **base of support**: region over which the object is supported
- **displacement**: change from equilibrium position

Units are not introduced explicitly in this section beyond the standard physical quantities of force and torque.
