# 11.3 Pressure

Source: OpenStax College Physics 2e, Chapter 11, Section 11.3

## Concept explanation
Pressure is defined as the force exerted on an area perpendicular to the force, divided by that area. In symbol form, pressure is the force per unit area.

A given force can have very different effects depending on the area over which it is applied. The same force spread over a large area produces less pressure than the same force applied over a small area. This is why a finger poke may have little lasting effect, while the same force applied to a sharp needle tip can break the skin.

The SI unit of pressure is the pascal. Pressure is also commonly expressed in millibar, pounds per square inch, and millimeters of mercury in certain applications such as meteorology, tire pressure, and blood pressure.

Pressure is defined for all states of matter but is especially important for fluids. For a static fluid, the force due to pressure acts perpendicular to any surface. Fluids cannot withstand shearing forces, so fluid pressure has no direction; it is a scalar quantity.

## Key formulas
$$ 
P = \frac{F}{A}
 $$

$$ 
F = PA
 $$

$$ 
1\ \text{Pa} = 1\ \text{N/m}^2
 $$

$$ 
1\ \text{mb} = 100\ \text{Pa}
 $$

## Variables and units
- $P $: pressure, measured in pascals (Pa)
- $F $: force, measured in newtons (N)
- $A $: area perpendicular to the force, measured in square meters (m$^2 $)
- $1\ \text{Pa} = 1\ \text{N/m}^2 $
- millibar (mb): often used for atmospheric pressure
- pounds per square inch: sometimes used for tire pressure
- millimeters of mercury (mm Hg): often used for blood pressure

## Worked example
**Calculating Force Exerted by the Air: What Force Does a Pressure Exert?**

An astronaut is working outside the International Space Station where the atmospheric pressure is essentially zero. The pressure gauge on her air tank reads $6.86 \times 10^6\ \text{Pa} $. What force does the air inside the tank exert on the flat end of the cylindrical tank, a disk $0.150\ \text{m} $ in diameter?

### Strategy
Use the definition of pressure:
$$ 
P = \frac{F}{A}
 $$
Solve for force:
$$ 
F = PA
 $$
The area is the area of a circle with diameter $0.150\ \text{m} $.

### Solution
The radius is
$$ 
r = \frac{0.150\ \text{m}}{2} = 0.0750\ \text{m}
 $$

The area is
$$ 
A = \pi r^2 = \pi (0.0750\ \text{m})^2
 $$

Then the force is
$$ 
F = PA = (6.86 \times 10^6\ \text{Pa})\pi(0.0750\ \text{m})^2
 $$

$$ 
F \approx 1.21 \times 10^5\ \text{N}
 $$

### Discussion
The force exerted by a pressure is directly proportional to both the pressure and the area acted upon. The force on the end of the tank is perpendicular to its inside surface because it is exerted by a static fluid. Fluid pressure has no direction, but the force due to pressure is always perpendicular to a surface.

## Key concepts used
- Pressure is force per unit area.
- Force and area are related by $F = PA $.
- The SI unit of pressure is the pascal.
- Pressure acts perpendicular to surfaces in a static fluid.
- Fluid pressure is a scalar quantity.
- The same force can produce different effects depending on area.
