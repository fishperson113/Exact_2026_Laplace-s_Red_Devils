# 11.5 Pascal’s Principle

Source: OpenStax College Physics 2e, Chapter 11, Section 11.5

## Concept explanation
Pressure is defined as force per unit area.

In an enclosed fluid, a change in pressure applied at one point is transmitted undiminished to all portions of the fluid and to the walls of its container. This is Pascal’s principle, first clearly stated by Blaise Pascal.

Pascal’s principle means that pressures add in a fluid system. This is useful in fluid statics and in hydraulic systems.

A hydraulic system is an enclosed fluid system used to exert forces. If two pistons are connected by a fluid-filled line and are at the same height, a pressure applied to one piston is transmitted to the other piston. Because the piston areas can be different, the output force can be larger or smaller than the input force.

Hydraulic systems can increase force, but they do not do more work than is done on them. The larger force is balanced by a smaller distance moved.

## Key formulas
Pressure is defined by

$$ 
P = \frac{F}{A}
 $$

For a hydraulic system with pistons at the same vertical height and negligible friction:

$$ 
P_1 = P_2
 $$

so

$$ 
\frac{F_1}{A_1} = \frac{F_2}{A_2}
 $$

which can be rearranged as

$$ 
F_2 = F_1 \frac{A_2}{A_1}
 $$

For circular pistons, area may be found from diameter $d $ using

$$ 
A = \pi \left(\frac{d}{2}\right)^2
 $$

## Variables and units
- $P $: pressure, units of pascals (Pa) or newtons per square meter (N/m$^2 $)
- $F $: force, units of newtons (N)
- $A $: area, units of square meters (m$^2 $)
- $F_1 $: input force on the first piston
- $F_2 $: output force on the second piston
- $A_1 $: area of the first piston
- $A_2 $: area of the second piston
- $d $: diameter of a circular piston, units of meters (m) or centimeters (cm)

Conditions stated in the section:
- the pistons must be at the same vertical height
- friction in the system is negligible
- the fluid is enclosed

## Worked example
EXAMPLE 11.6 Calculating Force of Wheel Cylinders: Pascal Puts on the Brakes

A force of 100 N is applied to the brake pedal. The pedal force is increased by a lever to 500 N on the pedal cylinder. Pressure created in the pedal cylinder is transmitted to four wheel cylinders. The pedal cylinder has diameter 0.500 cm, and each wheel cylinder has diameter 2.50 cm. Calculate the force created at each wheel cylinder.

Using Pascal’s principle for the hydraulic system:

$$ 
\frac{F_1}{A_1} = \frac{F_2}{A_2}
 $$

The result is that each wheel cylinder exerts a force of 125 N.

## Key concepts used
- Pressure is force per unit area.
- Pascal’s principle: a change in pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished throughout the fluid and to the container walls.
- Hydraulic systems use this principle to transmit force.
- If piston areas differ, the output force scales with area ratio.
- Hydraulic systems can multiply force, but the output piston moves a shorter distance.
- Pressure from different sources adds in a fluid.
