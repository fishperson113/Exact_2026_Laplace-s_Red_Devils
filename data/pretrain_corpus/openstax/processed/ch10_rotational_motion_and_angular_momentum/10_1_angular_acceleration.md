Discussion

Note that the angular acceleration as the girl spins the wheel is small and positive; it takes 5 s to produce an
appreciable angular velocity. When she hits the brake, the angular acceleration is large and negative. The angular
velocity quickly goes to zero. In both cases, the relationships are analogous to what happens with linear motion. For
example, there is a large deceleration when you crash into a brick wall—the velocity change is large in a short time
interval.
