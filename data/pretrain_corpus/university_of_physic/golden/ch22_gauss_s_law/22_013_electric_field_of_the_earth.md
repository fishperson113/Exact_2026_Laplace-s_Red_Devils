# Example 22.13: Electric field of the earth

## Problem
The earth (a conductor) has a net electric charge. The resulting electric field near the surface has an average value of about 150 N/C directed toward the center of the earth.

(a) What is the corresponding surface charge density?  
(b) What is the total surface charge of the earth?

## Setup
IDENTIFY and SET UP: We are given the electric-field magnitude at the surface of the conducting earth. We can calculate the surface charge density $s $ using Eq. (22.10). The total charge $Q $ on the earth’s surface is then the product of $s $ and the earth’s surface area.

For a conductor,
$$ 
s = P_0 E_\perp
 $$
and the earth’s surface area is
$$ 
4\pi R^2
 $$
where $R $ is the radius of the earth.

## Solution
EXECUTE: (a) The direction of the field means that $s $ is negative (corresponding to $E_\perp $ being directed into the surface, so $s $ is negative). From Eq. (22.10),
$$ 
s = P_0 E_\perp = (8.85 \times 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{C^2/(N\cdot m^2)})(-150\ \mathrm{N/C})
 $$
$$ 
s = -1.33 \times 10^{-9}\ \mathrm{C/m^2} = -1.33\ \mathrm{nC/m^2}
 $$

(b) The earth’s surface area is
$$ 
4\pi R^2
 $$
with
$$ 
R = 6.38 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m}
 $$
(see Appendix F). The total charge is the product $s(4\pi R^2) $, or
$$ 
Q = 4\pi R^2 s
 $$
$$ 
Q = 4\pi (6.38 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m})^2(-1.33 \times 10^{-9}\ \mathrm{C/m^2})
 $$
$$ 
Q = -6.8 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{C}
 $$

## Evaluation
You can check our result in part (b) using the result of Example 22.5. Solving for $Q $, we find
$$ 
Q = \frac{1}{9.0 \times 10^9\ \mathrm{N\cdot m^2/C^2}}(6.38 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{m})^2(-150\ \mathrm{N/C})
 $$
$$ 
Q = -6.8 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{C}
 $$
One electron has a charge of $-1.60 \times 10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C} $. Hence this much excess negative electric charge corresponds to there being excess electrons on the earth, or about 7 moles of excess electrons. This is compensated by an equal deficiency of electrons in the earth’s upper atmosphere, so the combination of the earth and its atmosphere is electrically neutral.

## Key concepts used
- Electric field just outside a conducting surface is related to surface charge density by $E_\perp = s/P_0 $.
- The sign of the surface charge density follows the direction of the electric field.
- Total charge is surface charge density times surface area: $Q = s(4\pi R^2) $.
- The earth’s negative charge implies a deficiency of electrons in the atmosphere balancing the system overall.
