# Example 25.4: A source in an open circuit

## Problem
Figure 25.16 shows a source (a battery) with emf $E $ and internal resistance $r $. The wires to the left of and to the right of the ammeter are not connected to anything. Determine the respective readings of the idealized voltmeter and the idealized ammeter.

## Setup
- The source has emf $E = 12\ \text{V} $ and internal resistance $r = 2\ \Omega $.
- The voltmeter is idealized, so its resistance is infinitely large.
- The circuit is open: the wires are not connected, so there is no complete circuit.

## Solution
There is zero current because there is no complete circuit. Since the idealized voltmeter has infinitely large resistance, no current flows through it. Hence the ammeter reads

$$ 
I = 0.
 $$

Because there is no current through the battery, there is no potential difference across its internal resistance. From Eq. (25.15) with $I = 0 $, the potential difference $V_{ab} $ across the battery terminals is equal to the emf. So the voltmeter reads

$$ 
V_{ab} = E = 12\ \text{V}.
 $$

## Evaluation
The terminal voltage of a real, nonideal source equals the emf only if there is no current flowing through the source, as in this example.

## Key concepts used
- An open circuit carries no current.
- An ideal voltmeter draws no current.
- For a source with internal resistance, terminal voltage equals emf when $I = 0 $.
- The ammeter reading is $0\ \text{A} $, and the voltmeter reading is $12\ \text{V} $.
