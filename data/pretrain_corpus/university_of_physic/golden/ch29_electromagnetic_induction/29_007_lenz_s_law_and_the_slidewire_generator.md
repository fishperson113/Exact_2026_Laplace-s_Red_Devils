# Example 29.7: Lenz’s law and the slidewire generator

## Problem
In Fig. 29.11, the induced current in the loop causes an additional magnetic field in the area bounded by the loop. Explain the direction of the induced current and how this relates to Lenz’s law.

## Setup
- The induced current in the loop is counterclockwise.
- By the right-hand rule for a current loop, a counterclockwise current produces a magnetic field directed out of the plane of the figure.
- The original magnetic field is in the opposite direction.

## Solution
The induced current is counterclockwise, so the additional magnetic field it produces is directed out of the plane of the figure. This magnetic field opposes the original magnetic field, so it tends to cancel the effect of that field.

This is exactly what Lenz’s law predicts: an induced current always acts to oppose the change in magnetic flux through the circuit.

## Evaluation
The result is consistent with Lenz’s law because the induced magnetic field opposes the change that produced it. The direction of the induced current is therefore such that its magnetic field counteracts the original field.

## Key concepts used
- Lenz’s law
- Magnetic field direction from the right-hand rule for a current loop
- Induced current opposes change in magnetic flux
