# Example 29.9: Motional emf in the slidewire generator

## Problem
Suppose the moving rod in Fig. 29.15b is 0.10 m long, the velocity is 2.5 m/s, the total resistance of the loop is 0.030 Ω, and $B $ is 0.60 T. Find the motional emf, the induced current, and the force acting on the rod.

## Setup
IDENTIFY and SET UP: The first target variable is the motional emf $ \mathcal{E}  $ due to the rod’s motion, which we’ll find using Eq. (29.6). We’ll find the current from the values of $ \mathcal{E}  $ and the resistance $R $. The force on the rod is a magnetic force, exerted by $B $ on the current in the rod; we’ll find this force using $ \mathbf{F} = I \mathbf{L} \times \mathbf{B}  $.

## Solution
EXECUTE: From Eq. (29.6) the motional emf is
$$ 
\mathcal{E} = vBL = (2.5\ \text{m/s})(0.60\ \text{T})(0.10\ \text{m}) = 0.15\ \text{V}.
 $$

The induced current in the loop is
$$ 
I = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R} = \frac{0.15\ \text{V}}{0.030\ \Omega} = 5.0\ \text{A}.
 $$

In the expression for the magnetic force,
$$ 
\mathbf{F} = I \mathbf{L} \times \mathbf{B},
 $$
the vector $\mathbf{L} $ points in the same direction as the induced current in the rod (from $a $ to $b $ in Fig. 29.15). Applying the right-hand rule for vector products shows that this force is directed opposite to the rod’s motion. Since $\mathbf{L} $ and $\mathbf{B} $ are perpendicular, the magnetic force has magnitude
$$ 
F = ILB = (5.0\ \text{A})(0.10\ \text{m})(0.60\ \text{T}) = 0.30\ \text{N}.
 $$

## Evaluation
EVALUATE: We can check our answer for the direction of $\mathbf{F} $ by using Lenz’s law. If we take the area vector $\mathbf{A} $ to point into the plane of the loop, the magnetic flux is positive and increasing as the rod moves to the right and increases the area of the loop. Lenz’s law tells us that a force appears to oppose this increase in flux. Hence the force on the rod is to the left, opposite its motion.

## Key concepts used
- Motional emf for a rod moving perpendicular to a magnetic field: $\mathcal{E} = vBL $
- Ohm’s law for the induced current: $I = \mathcal{E}/R $
- Magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor: $\mathbf{F} = I \mathbf{L} \times \mathbf{B} $
- Lenz’s law to check the direction of the induced force
