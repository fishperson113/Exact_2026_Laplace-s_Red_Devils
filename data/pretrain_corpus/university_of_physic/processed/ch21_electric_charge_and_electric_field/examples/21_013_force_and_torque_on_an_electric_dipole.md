Now let’s think of an electric dipole as a source of electric field. What does the
field look like? The general shape of things is shown by the field map of Fig.
21.28b. At each point in the pattern the total 
field is the vector sum of the fields
from the two individual charges, as in Example 21.8 (Section 21.5). Try drawing

To get quantitative information about the field of an electric dipole, we have to

even for the relatively simple case of a field due to two charges. Field calcula-
